Hello, friends, and welcome to another edition of Building on a Budget. I'm happy the two weeks preceding last week's preview went so well. I feel I have a much better understanding of what my readers are looking for. This is the first part of a two-part column. Everything that is not covered here will be explained in next week's column.

This week I'd like to explore a deck idea that was brought to my attention by Bill Stark. I have seen the deck floating around in the casual room for the past few months, and I'm not sure of its true origins. The deck controls the board with cards like [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) and [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) and finishes the opponent off with [Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding). The most exciting part of the archetype is how consistently it beats the Five-Color Control decks. I wanted to work on another Standard deck and the first thing I decided was that whatever deck I made, it had to have a positive matchup against the Five-Color decks.

Why do we need to beat Five-Color?

Standard is in a very interesting place right now. The format seems very healthy, but Five-Color Control decks still seem to dominate when given the opportunity.

Faeries was the dominant deck in Standard prior to the release of *Conflux*. The Faeries player had strong matchups against almost every deck and had the luxury of beating the Five-Color Control decks with some regularity. *Conflux* gave the Five-Color decks a new anti-Faeries tool, though. [Volcanic Fallout](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/418/volcanic-fallout) changed everything. Since Nassif's victory in Kyoto people have realized that the new Five-Color decks have a positive matchup against Faeries. The mass removal and card draw available to the Five-Color deck let it maintain its good matchups and jump over the one deck that was keeping it in check.

I got really excited when I saw Bill Stark's Turbo Mill deck because it had so many things that I love. I'm a sucker for any deck that plays [Howling Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/c16/257/howling-mine). The first FNM I ever won, I had brewed up a [Howling Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/c16/257/howling-mine) / life gain deck that won with a few [Millstone](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/242/millstone) and a set of Mines.

[![Sanity Grinding](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/eve/29?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding)

I'll start by explaining each of the card choices.

**[Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding):** This is our win condition. The deck has enough blue cards that [Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding) will likely be milling the opponent for a good portion of their deck.

**[Howling Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/c16/257/howling-mine):** The mine is extremely important. Once we get to five mana it's very important to have a steady stream of [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation)s and other bounce. The mine also speeds up the milling process.

**[Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang):** The tempo granted by [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) is an absolute necessity. Against control deck you usually want to be using [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) on lands during the early stages of the game. Against aggressive decks it's worthwhile to bounce cards like [Windbrisk Heights](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/411/windbrisk-heights), but once the game progresses beyond the fourth turn it's usually best to save a [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) to bounce Ajani Vengeant before he fires off his Ultimate.

**[Broken Ambitions](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/54/broken-ambitions):** [Broken Ambitions](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/54/broken-ambitions) is a very reasonable counter in any deck. It's especially strong in this deck because the clash actually becomes relevant.

**[Twincast](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/78/twincast):** [Twincast](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/78/twincast) is very important here. Usually you're using it with your [Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding) to finish off the game, but a lot of times it can make things go very well for you in offbeat scenarios. Imagine if your opponent ever plays a [Cryptic Command](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/48/cryptic-command)—this card becomes absolutely absurd. I think playing a full four of this is extremely important to the archetype.

**[Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation):** [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) is one of our most valuable tools. Red-White [Reveillark](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/26/reveillark) decks will have a very difficult time dealing with multiple [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation)s and this deck is very capable of pulling that off.

**[Plumeveil](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/218/plumeveil):** [Plumeveil](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/218/plumeveil) is probably our best anti-aggro tool. It slows the game down to a crawl. Conveniently, it also has three blue mana symbols in its mana cost, it helps make our [Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding) extra juicy.

**[Dream Fracture](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/66/dream-fracture):** [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell)s are always nice. This one fits our theme very well.

**[Memory Erosion](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/45/memory-erosion):** This *Shards of Alara* enchantment will help us mill our opponents as quickly as possible.

**[Shelldock Isle](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/272/shelldock-isle):** I'm a huge fan of this card in Cube Draft, and I'm glad I'm finally getting a chance to play with it in a Constructed format.

**[Reliquary Tower](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/398/reliquary-tower):** Our deck may end up with too many cards in hand once we start to settle into the late game. [Reliquary Tower](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/398/reliquary-tower) helps us gain card advantage by letting us hold on to the entirety of our hand.

I want better anti-aggro measures in the sideboard, so I'm going to add a playset of [Unsummon](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/79/unsummon) and some [Remove Soul](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/95/remove-soul)s. I want to have some [Negate](https://scryfall.com/card/tmt/47/negate)s in there too for the matchups where [Plumeveil](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/218/plumeveil) isn't relevant. In a lot of matchups you'll find yourself wanting to take out both the [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) and the [Plumeveil](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/218/plumeveil). I'd recommend leaving in the [Plumeveil](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/218/plumeveil) because it mills more cards when revealed by your [Sanity Grinding](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/29/sanity-grinding). The deck still struggles against red decks, and I'm not not sure whether [Kraken's Eye](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/211/krakens-eye) or [Dragon's Claw](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/206/dragons-claw) is better. The sideboard looks like this.
