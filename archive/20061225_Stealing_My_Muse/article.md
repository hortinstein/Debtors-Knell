# Stealing My Muse

**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** December 25, 2006  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb92>

---

*There have been three writers for Building on a Budget on **magicthegathering.com** - Nate Heiss, JMS, and myself. Each of us has a different style of approaching the column. Of the three of us, I probably hate to lose the most. If you player profiled me, I'd either be Spike/Johnny or Johnny/Spike depending on the day, but the end result is this: I like building my own decks, and I don't like losing.*

*I stand behind the results posted by my decks from playtesting, and some of my decks have been hits (Exploding Wurms) and some misses (Double Life). It's important to touch on both, because you can learn just as much from a deck that didn't work as you can from a deck that did. With that said, Stealing my Muse was the first Building on a Budget deck that shifted the entire casual room metagame overnight, with dozens of copies of this deck flooding the casual rooms the Monday following the deck's posting. Prices on Muse Vessel quadrupled overnight, and people actively groused about having to face this deck over and over again.*

*I consider that a sign that the deck worked.*

*This article originally appeared on July 31, 2006*

---

Ye be sailing the seas today, into the great Blue are ya? Well mates, I've got a story for you. A story about theft, betrayal, and battles most foul. A tale, if you will, about a wizard who took all from others, and gave to himself. Most of all, I give to you the story of that which brings inspiration for all stories…

“Self”, I thought to me, “there comes a time in every young boy's life when he has to take a rare and build a deck around it with his own two hands.” “Well,” I countered to myself, “wherein creativity and productivity lay in congress, therein is found said deck ideas.”

So a deck it would be, mates! But which ring of sixty cards would I have festooned around my avatar's neck for the week? Struck by my muse I was.

[![Muse Vessel](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/dis/163?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel)

[Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel), that is, indeed.

Ah, but what is Vessel, what is Muse? Part [Disrupting Scepter](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/295/disrupting-scepter), part Grinning Totem, all *Dissension* .Wherein to take your opponent's hand and turn it against them. A betrayal most fiendish!

Armed with creativity I was, but productivity was still in coming. To take, and to take against, that was the goal. No lackadaisical confrontation, but a forced conscription to the other side. Consultation with funds and purchases led me to these choices:

[Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex), [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), Dream Leash, [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion), Mimeofacture, Psychic Possession.

Whereupon the hand was empty, wherefore finds the [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) its purpose? ‘Twas told the artificer who built that glorious engine of pilfering told weakness against foes who showed empty hand and kept empty mind. Therefore, we must make him think and keep his idle hands occupied.

[Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang), [Consuming Vortex](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/54/consuming-vortex), [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation), [Exhaustion](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/76/exhaustion), [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere), [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction), [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand), [Repeal](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/94/repeal), Skyscribing, [Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept).

Should it be fair that I spend four mana and three to take, and not be able to use? Nay, my dishonest work has led to no reward henceforth. It should be that I receive, and I engage. Solve this, and solve the Vessel.

[Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex), [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone), Shifting Borders, [Spectral Searchlight](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/411/spectral-searchlight), Vedalken Plotter

A foul brew to distill! To fleece, [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) and [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate) serve true times two. To reseat, [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang), [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere) and [Repeal](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/94/repeal) would be the suite. [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand), a denial. [Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept), the captain at the helm. A Searchlight, guiding the ship to the Islands, finding an [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion).

“Rare enough,” my sole concurrence. “It would cost no more than ten and five, hence no Eviction, no Mimeofacture, no Dream Leash, no Psychic Possession.” A [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction) would tell no tales against the lands, the Plotter only with. Perhaps later I'll find use for my other tools. Perhaps not.

Beware all ye who would give dalliance to this deck. Though you might find it a jittering riot to smash your foes with their own tools of destruction, denying them land, spell or creature, they might take offense. Many quit in frustration at my antics, and others cried foul. Part denial, part land destruction, part theft, all malice.

You have been warned.

**Opponent #1: TrapperJ (B/W Knights)**

 I am assailed at every quarter by the Paladins of en-Vec, and the Hands of Honor, guided by the [Ghost Council of Orzhova](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/176/ghost-council-of-orzhova) wielding [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte). Though I hold his hordes off for twelve seasons, eventually I am overcome by sheer numbers.
*Victories: 0. Defeats: 1.*

**Opponent #2: Halopappy (U/B Ninjas)**

 Alas, my beginnings are not enjoyable. I find two Islands, but no more. With [Spectral Searchlight](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/411/spectral-searchlight) and two [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) in grip, a horde of ninjas overwhelm my very territory. [Okiba-Gang Shinobi](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/35/okiba-gang-shinobi) claw at my life and my mind, and soon I am found with neither.
*Victories: 0. Defeats: 2*

**Opponent #3: Ockadox (Roxodon Hierarchy)**

 Whoin should I find but my foe from weeks past, [Ockadox](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042451/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb69)! He still harnesses the powers of the Swamps, Plains and Forests. Where he employs [Farseek](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/173/farseek) and [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) to take a step forward, I use [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) to take a step forward *while* making him take a step back. Oh unfair day!

[Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept) joins the fray, holding off his Kokusho. The dragon frustrated, I accelerate ahead and find the powers to cast the epic [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion). Armed with the knowledge of my opponent's deepest secrets, I summon forth Ink-Eyes, and then Kokusho on my following action, killing his Kokusho (which I had wisely left around with my Adept). Ink-Eyes makes the Dragon turncoat, and a trifecta of [Skeletal Vampire](https://scryfall.com/card/c17/126/skeletal-vampire)s come to my aid. All have turned against Ockadox, and all is lost for him. 
*Victories: 1. Defeats: 2*

**Opponent #4: Toddwylie (W/G [Natural Affinity](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/256/natural-affinity)/Genjus)**

 When one plays with lands as their only route to victory, what happens when the lands turn against them? I [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) his Plains, use the [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere) to return his Genju-enchanted Forests, and then build up towards the [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion). His mind is on the [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god), and his board on a sole [Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields). I steal three more, and Toddwylie concedes, with me beyond sixty life. *Victories: 2. Defeats: 2*

[![Eternal Dominion](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/sok/36?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion)

I have now won the day as much as I have lost… yet I find myself in a state of discontent. [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion)? This is not the highlight, it is the [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel), but I have yet to see a Vessel! I know I can delay until I find myself with ten mana, which in and of itself is a feat. This is not the goal though – the goal is to use the Vessel as an instrument of destruction. To force this issue, the Dominions may have to go shortly.

**Opponent #5: Jktk0401 (Mono-Black)**

 He gets stuck at three lands for a couple of turns. I [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand) his [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber)s on the sixth, draw into an [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex), and use it on his Swamp. He concedes. 
*Victories: 3. Defeats: 2*

**Opponent #6: Nolight (Nephilim)**

 I start this battle with an Island... His first play is a tapped [Temple Garden](https://scryfall.com/card/rtr/248/temple-garden), which I [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang), leaving him with nothing. He plays the Temple and a [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), and I play [Spectral Searchlight](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/411/spectral-searchlight). He plays a second land, and I [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere) it, plus [Repeal](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/94/repeal) his Birds. I then drop [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) and [Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept), and proceed to savage his hand and his board. Soon, I cast Dune-Brood Nephilim, and his hope is lost.
*Victories: 4. Defeats: 2*

It is time for a change, and to move forward. I am unhappy with the performance of [Spectral Searchlight](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/411/spectral-searchlight). In many cases, I have [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) in my grasp, but am unable to cast it until the fourth turn, despite a Searchlight in hand. Should the Searchlight be [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone), my opponent's land would be my land. Moreover, [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) serves the same purpose as [Spectral Searchlight](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/411/spectral-searchlight), but one step earlier – access to colors of mana not my own.

**Opponent #7: Tsmr (U/R Niv-Mizzet)**

 I [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) and [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere) his first three lands, leaving his board completely empty. I follow this with [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel), and he answers with Wee Dragonauts a turn later. I get to work on his hand, taking [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak), [Tidings](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/108/tidings), and [Repeal](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/94/repeal). For his part, he is allowing me to take the control elements, opting instead to try to burn me out with [Char](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/117/char) and [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze). This plan almost works, as I am dropped to a precarious three life. That is as far as I will fall.

I drop a second Vessel, and go after his Mountains. I take one with [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) and one with [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), leaving him without any sources of Red mana. I take a Niv-Mizzet and a Dragonaut of my own with the Vessel, and drop the Dragonaut to stop his. They trade (backed by his stolen [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) against a [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze)), and [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand) stops [Char](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/117/char) from finishing the game. I steal yet another Mountain, and earn a victory through concession when his Niv-Mizzet hits my side of the world.
*Victories: 5. Defeats: 2*

**Opponent #8: Lynnpaul (B/G *Legends*)**

 I face down an early [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu), and take some damage to set up [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel). I [Repeal](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/94/repeal) his plant, and [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) a Swamp. He [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize)s my Vessel, but I have another. Twin Vinelashers come down against me, but I return them to his hand when they get larger than two power. Meanwhile, [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) has taken Ink-Eyes, and I cast the Legend upon drawing a [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone). Isao is the next to be drawn into the Vessel, and he too begins playing for my team. With this pair of Legends arrayed against him, Lynnpaul quickly falls prey to treachery and betrayal.
 Victories: 6. Defeats: 2

**Opponent #9: Podwrangler (Samurais)**

 My opening hand is three Islands, two [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel)s, an [Annex](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/59/annex) and [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion). I draw [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), [Eternal Dominion](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/36/eternal-dominion), [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel), [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), [Eye of Nowhere](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/59/eye-of-nowhere), and then die to an army of Samurai. *Victories: 6. Defeats: 3*

My head is now clear. My losses have all come to quick waves of creatures. My hand has been cluttered by that which is too costly, and so I will make it that my hand is cluttered no more. I also have had a shortage of mana one time too many, so that must be remedied.

Many times I find myself wanting just one more turn to gain control of the game, so I figure an Exhausted opponent is an opponent who cannot do me harm. With the changes in mind, I return to battle. I would not taste defeat again.

**Opponent #15: EngineKid (G/W/U)**
 He's playing a lot of bounce lands, and I drop turn 3 [Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept). He has no way to remove it in his deck, and I send him back to zero lands within a turn, adding [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) to the mix. It was immensely fun to grab my opponent's permanents and spells over and over again, using his own tools against him to win the game. On the flip side, it was immensely frustrating for my opponents to have their permanents and spells stolen over and over again, and having their own tools used against them. In addition, the bounce aspect of the deck was also mind-rattling to play against, when used as mana-denial in the early game. Nobody wants to be hit by three [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang)-effects in a row, facing down [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) on turn 4 with no permanents on the board.

To sum up, I found that [Muse Vessel](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/163/muse-vessel) itself performed admirably, and the deck went on an undefeated tear after the third revision. It could still be improved (my workmate Aaron recommended I get [Sleight of Hand](https://scryfall.com/card/woe/67/sleight-of-hand) in the deck somehow, as a way to smooth out my hand on the first turn or late game), but overall I can put the Building On A Budget stamp of approval on Finding My Muse. Just please, please keep in mind that while this deck is a lot of fun to play, it *can* be frustrating to play against. Use with caution!
