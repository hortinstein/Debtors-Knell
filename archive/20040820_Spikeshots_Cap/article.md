# Spikeshot’s Cap

**Author:** Nate Heiss  
**Date:** August 20, 2004  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/nh28>

Nothing beats the feeling of a [Spikeshot Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/256/spikeshot-goblin) with a cool toy. I can remember countless limited games that were won simply on the power of [Spikeshot Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/256/spikeshot-goblin) and [Bonesplitter](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/458/bonesplitter).

Today's article takes this idea that extra step by using a new card from *Fifth Dawn* that has been getting lots of exposure: [Cranial Plating](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/245/cranial-plating). This particular headgear can turn an innocent little [Spikeshot Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/256/spikeshot-goblin) into a firebreathing monster. Just imagine how big that guy could get….5/2? 8/2? Even 10/2? Needless to say, shooting your opponent with one of these is likely to end the game in a hurry.

This deck idea was inspired by an email I got from a fan, John White. It just goes to show that I look at all your emails (even though I only am able to respond sometimes).

For a deck of all commons, I thought this was pretty good, and I really liked the concept of putting a [Cranial Plating](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/245/cranial-plating) on a [Spikeshot Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/256/spikeshot-goblin). I decided to take my own crack at a deck like this and came up with the following:

**Building on a Budget: Spikeshot's Cap**

- 4 Darksteel Citadel
- 4 Great Furnace
- 13 Mountain
  *(21 lands)*

- 4 Arcbound Stinger
- 4 Arcbound Worker
- 4 Frogmite
- 4 Myr Enforcer
- 3 Oxidda Golem
- 4 Spikeshot Goblin
  *(23 creatures)*

- 4 Bonesplitter
- 4 Cranial Plating
- 4 Pyrite Spellbomb
- 4 Shrapnel Blast
  *(16 other spells)*

*(Full decklist with sideboard: `decklist.txt`; priced breakdown: `decklist_priced.md`)*

This deck plays out by butting little artifact creatures in play along with artifact lands, thus increasing Affinity for [Frogmite](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/215/frogmite)s and [Myr Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/224/myr-enforcer)s. It then can utilize a huge [Cranial Plating](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/245/cranial-plating) (5+) on either a [Spikeshot Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/256/spikeshot-goblin) or an [Arcbound Stinger](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/199/arcbound-stinger) since it has evasion. If you don't have either of those, you can just equip the Plating to an [Arcbound Worker](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/201/arcbound-worker) and attack, trading with their guy and keeping your Modular counter.

There is only one contradicting part about this deck: [Oxidda Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem). The [Oxidda Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem) usually wants to see lots of Mountains and not artifact lands, but in this deck, he is still important because he is a powerful artifact creature with haste, and will still probably be cast for no more than 4 mana. Having the extra artifact creature to put the Modular counters on really helps out. The other good parts about this deck are the [Bonesplitter](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/458/bonesplitter)s and the [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast)s. These are your secondary way of relieving your opponent of his life total, and while they are not [Cranial Plating](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/245/cranial-plating), they are still quite good at it.

##### Tips on Playing the Deck

- [![Oxidda Golem](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/jvc/46?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem) Play out your artifact lands first unless you have [Oxidda Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem) in hand.
- [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb) is great for increasing your Affinity; you might want to cast your [Myr Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/224/myr-enforcer) before you blow the Spellbomb.
- [Arcbound Worker](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/201/arcbound-worker) is a very hard worker. He makes a great creature to block with since you can ship the counter onto your Stinger or other artifact creatures and swing in harder the next turn.
- Use those [Oxidda Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem)s to your advantage – haste can be a very important factor when dealing with [Cranial Plating](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/245/cranial-plating). They will never see it coming…when they tap all their guys to attack you, drop the Golem (and maybe the Plating) equip, and truck on in!
- Use [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) wisely – it is an extremely powerful burn spell. You can also pull tricks with your Modular creatures by [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast)ing and sacrificing them once blockers are declared. You can then move the Modular counter over to another creature, making it the victor in its combat.

##### Adding Money to the Deck

As John said in his email, [Arc-Slogger](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/85/arc-slogger) makes a great addition to this or any Red deck. Besides that, you could go with a more traditional Affinity build using Arcbound Ravagers and possibly Black for [Disciple of the Vault](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/86/disciple-of-the-vault) and the instant ability to move your Plating. Another fun thing to do with this deck would be adding [Atog](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/209/atog) or [Vulshok War Boar](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/72/vulshok-war-boar) (especially if your version has [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves)).

As a separate idea – you can cross this deck with the old Building on a Budget deck “Gunner” for some interesting action. Using [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer) is fun in any situation!

Until next time, make sure you got your cap on straight.
