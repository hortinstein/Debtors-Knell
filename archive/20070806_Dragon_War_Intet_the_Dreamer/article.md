[![Intet, the Dreamer](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/2x2/229?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/229/intet-the-dreamer)

Over the past few months, several dozen people have asked for me to evolve decks around one (or all) of the five Legendary Dragons from *Planar Chaos*. The next few weeks seem to be the absolute best time to make this request into a reality! Each deck will center around one of the five Legendary Dragons, but with a few deck building restrictions:

1. Each Dragon has an activated ability—the color of that ability is the main color of that Dragon. For instance, Intet's ability requires blue mana to activate. This makes Intet the blue dragon. The only mono-colored cards I can use for a Dragon are those of its activated ability!
2. However, a mono-colored deck with a splash solely to play a Dragon would be extremely boring. Each Dragon is made of three colors—the activation color, and the *enemy* colors of the activated ability. Intent is blue, and the enemies of blue are green and red. While I won't use any mono-green or mono-red cards to build an Intet deck, I am free to use any multi-colored cards that have both the primary colors and one (or more) of the secondary colors! So, not only will the Intet deck feature mono-blue cards, but she will be the general for Izzet (blue-red) and Simic (blue-green) cards as well!
3. Artifacts and lands of any kind are fair game.

In addition, I want to keep these decks as focused on the Dragons—and their abilities—as possible. These are going to be theme decks. While I still want to build these decks to perform as well as they can, they are within the three constraints above, plus the realization that I want these decks to FEEL like they are about a huge, powerful Dragon and his army.

**4 [Intet, the Dreamer](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/229/intet-the-dreamer) (0.5 Tickets Each)**

Since this is an Intet deck, let's start it out with four copies of Intet. Intet's ability lets her essentially [Mind's Desire](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/123/minds-desire) for one card. Since Intet is already hitting for 6 damage a turn, its ability is going to need to turn up a higher mana-cost card (one that could swing the game) in order to be worthwhile.

**3 [Simic Sky Swallower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/452/simic-sky-swallower) (1.5 Tickets Each) & 3 Niv-Mizzet (1.5 Tickets Each)**

Niv-Mizzet is another Dragon that falls under the colors of Intet's army, so in he goes! This is Dragon War! There's no time for slackers! In addition to Niv-Mizzet, [Simic Sky Swallower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/452/simic-sky-swallower) is another huge flying creature that fits the Dragon motif. Let's see what kind of use we can put these towards.

**2 [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double) (1 Ticket Each), 4 [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) (0.1 Tickets Each), 3 [Careful Consideration](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/56/careful-consideration) (0.1 Tickets Each), & 4 [Looter *il*-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor) (0.1 Tickets Each)**

Blue has a lot of spells these days that involve drawing and discarding cards. While blue doesn't have the reanimation spells found in black ([Beacon of Unrest](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/77/beacon-of-unrest), [Vigor Mortis](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/74/vigor-mortis), [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return)), it has the next-best thing in [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double). [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double) lets you copy a creature in any graveyard, and it lets you get two of the same (non-Legendary) creature. If you [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) a [Simic Sky Swallower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/452/simic-sky-swallower), there's no target left for your other [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) in hand. [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double) does not have these issues.

The other spells here ([Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), [Careful Consideration](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/56/careful-consideration), and [Looter *il*-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor)) are the workhorses of blue card drawing and filtering. They are at home in any blue deck, and especially so in one that doesn't mind pitching creatures to the bin. Also, hitting with Intet into a draw-three or draw-four spell isn't a bad call.

**3 [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate) (0.1 Tickets Each)**

If Intet is racing alone against an opponent's board, [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate) might be the one card that can save you. It's also a good, generic blue spell as a "catch-all" against anything that might be thrown against you. A perennial Building on a Budget favorite!

**4 [Coiling Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/172/coiling-oracle) (0.1 Tickets Each), 4 [Gruul Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/223/gruul-signet) (0.1 Tickets Each), 4 [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens) (0.1 Tickets Each)**

The mana acceleration for the deck, and the parts that make this deck work. Look, when your deck's win conditions start at six mana, you need to ramp up your speed, and fast. Having the redundancy of twelve two-drop accelerants ensures that you'll draw at least one a game, and sometimes two early on, especially when combined with the card drawing. This ties into:

**12 [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island) (Free), 4 [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse) (0.25 Tickets Each), 1 [Gruul Turf](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/152/gruul-turf) (0.1 Tickets Each), 1 [Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks) (0.1 Tickets Each),1 [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) (Free), 1 [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain) (Free)**

Total Deck Cost: 17 Tickets

Game 1: Waterdonkey (Mono-Red Suspend)
 He gets double Epochrasite going, with a suspended [Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon) and a [Rift Elemental](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/185/rift-elemental) on the board. I get down Intet and Niv-Mizzet, and the last turn comes down to him either having a [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt) or a Fury Charm (I had seen one of each). I choose to double-block his Gargadon with my guys, and he hits me down to 3 with his Epochrasites. Unfortunately, he has the [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt), and I die. If he had the Fury Charm, I would have won this game.
 Record: 0-1

Game 2: Mahatmajond (Blue-White Rescue)

I ramp up my mana with Signets and [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens) and [Coiling Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/172/coiling-oracle), and [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate) his guy on turn five. I then play [Simic Sky Swallower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/452/simic-sky-swallower), [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double) another [Sky Swallower](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/34/sky-swallower), and beat him senseless.
 Record: 1-1

Game 3: Nevinyrral803 (Green-White Aggro)

[Temporal Isolation](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/48/temporal-isolation) takes care of my Niv-Mizzet, and my Intet just stares blankly at his Ranger as I die. I also draw triple Intet this game.

Game 5: BigLandoz (Mono-Red Goblinstorm)
 He plays [Goblin King](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/207/goblin-king), then [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) / [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/96/rite-of-flame) into a large [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens), with a [Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon) looming. I get stuck at three mana, have to waste a turn dropping [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens)es, and can't [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double) for a Niv-Mizzet to stabilize.
 Record: 2-3

So far, all of my losses have been to creatures I either couldn't target, or that were too numerous to take out. Given the card pool available, I decide to give [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) a whirl. Against all three opponents I lost to, [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) would have turned the game around—those 2/2 goblin tokens would have vanished for good, I could have gotten rid of the Epochrasites and Gargadon in Game 1, and I might have slowed down the beatings from the [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger) in Game 3 (a 1/1 flyer is a lot easier to navigate around than a 3/3 flyer!).

I also really like being able to reanimate my guys with [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double), so I add a third one to the deck. In exchange, I take out a couple of [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate)s (everyone is playing [Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon) today!), a Niv-Mizzet, and one Signet.

Out: 2 [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate), 1 Niv-Mizzet, 1 [Gruul Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/223/gruul-signet)

Game 10: mpbjab (White-Green Aggro)
 He gets double [Watchwolf](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/220/watchwolf), and I drop a fifth-turn [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double)d Intet. I come a turn away from racing him, but I need [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation) to win this one, and it doesn't come up after two [Coiling Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/172/coiling-oracle) hits, two [Looter *il*-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor) hits, double Intet, [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), and two normal draw phases.
 Record: 6-4

The deck is doing better, but I've had multiple games where I've drawn multiple Intets, and they have been clogging up my hand. Plus, I haven't really had a need to use Intet's ability. I don't want to cut all the Intets from the deck, but three copies seems plenty—especially given the triple [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double)s I have now.

In place of the Intet, I add back in a Niv-Mizzet (to give myself three), and put in a singleton of [Tidespout Tyrant](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/63/tidespout-tyrant). I want a bounce spell in the deck, and the Tyrant's ability works perfectly to fill this slot, especially if I get an early [Body Double](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/75/body-double).

Out: 1 [Coiling Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/172/coiling-oracle), 1 [Intet, the Dreamer](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/229/intet-the-dreamer)
 In: 1 [Tidespout Tyrant](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/63/tidespout-tyrant), 1 Niv-Mizzet
