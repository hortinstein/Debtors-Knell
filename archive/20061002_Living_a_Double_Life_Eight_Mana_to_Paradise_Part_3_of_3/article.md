**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** October 2, 2006  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb79>

Welcome to the final installment of Double Life – the deck built around [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure). In [part one](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042440/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb77), I got my teeth kicked in by anyone who wanted to come and take a shot. In [part two](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042440/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb78), I sent my deck to be doctored by myself (long story – please don't call the men in the white jackets to take me away!) and ended up with three distinct builds. In [part three](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042440/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb79), you've just clicked a link that took you back to this article.

Now I'm not the only one living a double life.

The first deck I decided to playtest was the control version of Double Life. I wanted to see if the deck could support a three-color manabase, and I really just wanted to fiddle around with [Vanish into Memory](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/196/vanish-into-memory). It seems like it should be a good card to move through my deck, plus act as a roadbump in my opponent's quest to put a damper on my twenty life.

**Game 1: Seabear0 (Burn)**

Seabear0 ramps up to fifteen mana with his acceleration, and proceeds to burn the pants off of me with X spells. Even though I get off a [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality), I end up with a [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), three lands on top of my deck for several turns, and no way to shuffle things up.

*Record: 0-1*

**Game 2: Bemihovich (Mono-Black Control)**

I'm finding that I have some dead cards vs. creatureless decks. [Vanish into Memory](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/196/vanish-into-memory) and [Ribbons of Night](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/120/ribbons-of-night) sit in my hand as I end up getting destroyed by Cabal Coffers plus a lot of Black burn.

*Record: 0-2*

**Game 3: Blue Lightning (Dimir)**

Blue Lightning brings a mill strategy to the table, and gets me down to four cards before I get my combo off. However, this is really a pyrrhic victory, because A) I had no cards in my hand with those four cards left in my library, and had to use Sensei's Divining top to get both the Beacon and the [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure) in the same turn. It's nothing more than complete luck that, with only four cards left in my library, I still had one of each of those left, and B) Blue Lightning was new with his deck, and forgot to mill me several times with his Duskmantle, House of Shadow, so I should have lost anyhow.

*Record: 1-2 (but easily could have been 0-3)*

Wow, how discouraging. I'm pretty much right back where I started – I have a combo deck that can't win, gets run over by everybody, and is not even fun to play. Let's try a different build of the deck.

Unlike the control build of this deck, the [Test of Endurance](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/32/test-of-endurance) build seeks to proactively win the game. Instead of focusing on controlling my opponent's board, I mix up threats ([Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields)), discard ([Castigate](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/106/castigate)) and two separate win conditions (both [Test of Endurance](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/32/test-of-endurance) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure)).

**Game 1: Orangeman3 (Slivers)**

Shifting Sliver

I cast [Castigate](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/106/castigate), and I shift Shifting Sliver into his removed-from-game area. He casts [Farseek](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/173/farseek), and accelerates into a third-turn [Essence Sliver](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/8/essence-sliver). I drop [Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields), and he drops a freshly-drawn second Shifting Sliver. Frown. This sends us into race mode – I attack the Genju into his territory to gain life (remember, if you activate [Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields) multiple times in one turn, you gain life for each activation!), while he starts beating me with five points of Spirit-Linked damage a turn.

Orangeman3 looks to break the stalemate with Brood Sliver, but I have [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest), which allows my Genju to block safely. He drops Sigil of the New Dawn, and I draw into [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure), and cast it for the win.

*Record: 1-0*

**Game 2: Joe b 1 (Mono-Black Control)**

It looks like the game is going to go in my favor – I get up to seven lands, and have [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure) in hand, with a Divining Top on the board. Unfortunately, Joeb1 drops Phage the Untouchable via Cabal Coffers, and my only way to deal with it is [Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields). I have one turn to get an 8th land, but the top three cards of my library are all spells, and I end up having to chump block with my Plains. Without the ability to get to fifty life or eight mana, I lose the game.

*Record: 1-1*

**Game 3: King6666 (Clerics)**

On back to back turns, I [Castigate](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/106/castigate) out [Rotlung Reanimator](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/164/rotlung-reanimator) (which would have wrecked me) and [Proclamation of Rebirth](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/15/proclamation-of-rebirth). He casts a couple of one-drop Clerics and [Cabal Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/129/cabal-archon), which I [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest). We both build up mana for a bit, and he eventually casts [Patriarch's Bidding](https://scryfall.com/card/mh2/275/patriarchs-bidding), bringing into a play a cycled [Scion of Darkness](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/86/scion-of-darkness). I cast [Tower of Eons](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/266/tower-of-eons) and [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite), and start gaining ten life plus a land a turn. King6666 doesn't put on additional pressure, and eventually I just draw into [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure) for the win.

*Record: 2-1*

That's much better – proactively gaining life ([Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields), [Tower of Eons](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/266/tower-of-eons)) seems to work better than reactively killing creatures, but the presence of [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) helped me win two of the three games. Clearly removal is needed, but what type? The answer might be found in the last build.

This last build of Double Life tries to abuse [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards) as both a blocker, and as a way to recur [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields). It seeks to stall the game in a null-state until the combo of Beacon plus Cure can be drawn.

**Game 1: Slavetrader (White Weenie)**

I [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress), and get [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves). His hand was Leonin Elder and Skyhunter. He drops his creatures, and I use [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter) to clear away his board. I go into Beacon Recursion mode casting the spell three times in a row (thanks to [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) and [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite)). He gets a couple more creatures onto the board, but I have enough of a life cushion that I can leisurely draw [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) for a fourth time, and pair it this time with [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure).

*Record: 1-0*

**Game 2: Xavier12 (B/G)**

Cabal Coffers

He [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress)es me, taking [Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp). He then proceeds to cast double [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth) for Swamps, and drops three(!) Cabal Coffers. Unfortunately for Xavier12, that's all he does this game. He literally never casts another spell, and my [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) and [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite) go unanswered. I am able to get my combo shortly thereafter, and win.

*Record: 2-0*

**Game 3: Kohtah (R/G/B Aggro)**

Kohtah gets a fast start with turns 1 and 2 [Raging Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/153/raging-goblin)s, followed by [Reckless Charge](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/144/reckless-charge) on turns 3 and 4. I am on the draw, and I [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter) the board away after taking thirteen damage from his only-three spells. I [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress), and his hand is a pair of Black flyers. This gives me time to get down [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite) and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), but I can't seem to Top into anything good. A [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze) for five puts me on an immediate clock, but I respond like a whiz by drawing [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields)! This brings me back up to eighteen life in short order, and with that much of a cushion, I have no problem finding the time to get a Beacon plus a Cure for the win.

*Record: 3-0*

All right, so let's put it all together. The [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards) build did the best, even though I literally never needed to cast [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards). The [Test of Endurance](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/32/test-of-endurance) build did second best, even though I never needed to cast [Test of Endurance](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/32/test-of-endurance). The worst record was put up by the control build, even though control elements such as [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter) and [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) were what won me most of my games with the other two decks.

What does this all mean?

A) I don't need a full Wrath effect, such as [Kirtar's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/35/kirtars-wrath) – I just need a smaller, [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm)/[Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) type effect, to buy me enough time to get to my larger spells. [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards) is unnecessary, as is an alternate win condition – I need to focus on [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality) and [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure), and not [Test of Endurance](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/32/test-of-endurance). Some spot removal, such as [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters), can't hurt, so that I'm not hopeless against larger threats like Phage.

Putting it all together:

With all of these in mind, I decide to build off of the [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards) build, as it had the best record. I take out [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter) in favor of [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest), and take out the [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards) for [Night's Whisper](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/158/nights-whisper) – a card which will help me draw further into my deck, and whose loss of life will be offset by [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields).

**Out**: 3 [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter), 4 [Wall of Shards](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/23/wall-of-shards)
**In**: 3 [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest), 4 [Night's Whisper](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/158/nights-whisper)

**Game 1: Tevesh_Szat (Pro Tour Dredgeatog deck)**

This game is kind of annoying, because Tevesh_Szat is playing a slightly-modified Pro Tour Top 8 deck ([Billy Moreno's Madness Tog deck from Pro Tour LA 2005](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042440/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/ptla05/t8decks)), and refuses to acknowledge that, just maybe, this isn't a deck for the casual room. He proceeds to drop [Wild Mongrel](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/194/wild-mongrel), counters my spells with [Circular Logic](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/42/circular-logic), dredges back to get several [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) into his graveyard…and then plays badly, leaving no cards in hand so I can [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) his [Wild Mongrel](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/194/wild-mongrel), which in turn leaves me enough time to combo him out.

Guys, I've said it once and I've said it again – I don't mind seeing all sorts of decks in the casual room. I don't mind playing against decks that have five hundred dollars worth of rares. What tweaks my grinders is seeing someone take a deck that finished in the Top 8 of the Pro Tour, and having them try to get their jollies by steamrolling people who are playing much less competitive decks.

*Record: 1-0*

Let's think of happy trees, and move on.

**Game 2: Slappylittleworld (R/G Aggro)**

[![Slith Predator](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mrd/129?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/129/slith-predator)

He curves nearly perfectly with [Kird Ape](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/137/kird-ape), [Slith Predator](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/129/slith-predator), Burning-Tree Shaman, and Rumbling Slum. I Fetters the Shaman and keep afloat with [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields), with the full combo in my hand. On the seventh turn, I end up at three life, and he casts [Yamabushi's Flame](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/198/yamabushis-flame) to kill me after his attack. I had [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress)ed him two turns earlier, and he had nothing of the sort at the time – and the top card of my library was the 8th land I would have needed to win. So the cookie crumbles sometimes!

*Record: 1-1*

**Game 3: Crisis909 (R/B Burn)**

Crisis909 keeps a hand with only a single Swamp, and all Red cards. I Duress him three straight turns, after which he still discards until turn seven. He *STILL* wins, as I can't draw a Top, Kite or a combo piece, and he eventually gets two [Slith Firewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/39/slith-firewalker)s on the board that grow to 7/7 and 8/8 before my [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields) can't hold them back no more.

*Record: 1-2*

All right – when your opponent would effectively have been discarding until turn 7 with only one land on the board, and you still lose, something's wrong with your deck. I keep getting stuck with junk in my hand that can't help me combo out (such as [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields) and [Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp)), and not enough ways to draw through my deck. In addition, why is there so much White in my deck? I decide to move the deck towards more of a Black build, with substitute life gain that can double as creature kill, and more card drawing.

**Out**: 3 [Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp), 4 [Orzhov Basilica](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/292/orzhov-basilica), 3 Plains, 3 [Pulse of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/11/pulse-of-the-fields)

**Game 5: Gerca (B/W Control)**

I [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress) him out of a [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer), leaving him five lands and a [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal). He drops Sword of Kaldra and That Which Was Taken, and proceeds to start hitting me with a 6/6 Indestructible [Weathered Wayfarer](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/34/weathered-wayfarer). I get to seven Swamps, and my hand is, get this, three Corrupts and nothing else. I start shooting straight for Gerca's dome, but he drops [Mourning Thrull](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/204/mourning-thrull), makes it indestructible, and starts slapping me around with a 6/6 [Spirit Link](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/29/spirit-link)ed guy the turn before I could kill him.

Well, I have somewhat of a padded life total thanks to the first two [Corrupt](https://scryfall.com/card/bro/88/corrupt)s, and I get to use the third [Corrupt](https://scryfall.com/card/bro/88/corrupt) on [Blazing Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/8/blazing-archon), when Gerca tries casting it without being able to make it indestructible the same turn. This gives me just enough time to draw into [False Cure](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/146/false-cure) and [Beacon of Immortality](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/10/beacon-of-immortality), and I kill Gerca the turn before he kills me.

*Record: 2-3*

**Game 6: Rocore1 (B/W/G)**

Forgotten Ancient

I get completely worked over by Forgotten Ancient, as I have no real way of removing it once it gets going. It makes Exalted Angel and Eastern Paladin ridiculously large, and even my Corrupts and [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters) are to no avail against his [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize)s and constant onslaught of fatties.

*Record: 2-4*

All right, I've had it. I really, really, really wanted the draw engine of [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite) and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) to work – it's the pair of cards which inspired the deck in the first place – but they aren't working. I keep getting stuck with the Top, and no ways to shuffle my deck – and I keep getting the Kite, and no cards to go with all the mana. I need a direct way to get my combo pieces – such as [Diabolic Tutor](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/54/diabolic-tutor), because in a two-card combo, [Diabolic Tutor](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/54/diabolic-tutor) is a wild card for either half.

I also put back in a single [Kirtar's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/35/kirtars-wrath) (as a Tutor target, against fattie decks), and put back in three Basilicas, since I don't have three Kites and four Tops to help land selection in-game anymore.

**Out**: 3 [Journeyer's Kite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/65/journeyers-kite), 4 [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), 1 [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters)

Well, I did manage to get the deck to nearly a .500 record, but all-in-all, I don't consider this deck a success. Yeah, it took three weeks to get there, but the point isn't always to win – it's to understand why certain choices are made in deck building, and why certain decks don't work. In short, an eight-mana combo, based around two cards that don't help you win in-and-of-themselves, is not the way to build a deck. You can't build a deck to have no weaknesses (witness my fluctuation between [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest)/[Kirtar's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/35/kirtars-wrath)/[Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters)/[Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp) – a.k.a., weenie horde removal, fattie horde removal, spot removal, and artifact/enchantment removal), and sometimes, even though you work a deck into the ground, it still doesn't measure up.

But don't worry – I've already got a 6-0 record with my deck for next week!
