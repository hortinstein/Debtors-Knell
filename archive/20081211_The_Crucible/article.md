# The Crucible

**Author:** Jacob Van Lunen  
**Date:** December 11, 2008  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/boab/16>

*(Original article contained outbound links to: Gatherer.)*

---

> *"Dragon creatures you control get +3/+3."*

Seriously? That's a lot of pumpage. I've had this strange soft spot for [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire) ever since the first time I got one fourteenth pick in a draft. The reason the soft spot is strange is because I've never actually taken the time to build a deck around it. That's about to change, however.

"The dragon is a perfect marriage of power and the will to use it." —Sarkhan Vol

Dragons are complete bad asses*.* They're huge beasts that crave power and respect. That's the problem with [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire): it makes creatures huge that were already huge. If there's a good [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire) deck, then it needs to be built around undercosted dragons. When I play a [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire), I don't want it to sit there on the table and look pretty until the sixth turn when I get to play some fatty boom-boom dragons.

There's a solution to the high cost of Dragon cards, though. Changelings! I can justify playing [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire) if I play enough changelings that cost three or less.

[![Crucible of Fire](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ima/122?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire)

The deck is obviously going to play red for [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire). To start the building process I need to decide what my second color should be. After a quick look through [Gatherer](https://web.archive.org/web/20090602011402/http://beta.gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx), it becomes a pretty close call between green and black. Green gives us a great two-drop in the form of Woodland Changeling and a trampling fatty boom-boom in the form of Game-Trail Changeling. In black I have a regenerating two drop in the form of [Skeletal Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/140/skeletal-changeling) and a number of good three drops in the form of [Ghostly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/83/ghostly-changeling) and [Moonglove Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/67/moonglove-changeling). After thinking about support cards I decide to go with black because it gives me more support cards. (Burn spells, discard, etc.)

I decide to compile a list of changelings that I might be playing with.

**[Fire-Belly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/51/fire-belly-changeling):** This little guy may seem like it isn't good enough, but it's a changeling that costs two mana, so it'll probably make the cut.

**[Skeletal Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/140/skeletal-changeling):** Regenerate is a pretty relevant ability when this is a 4/4 or a 7/7. Again, two-drops are a commodity, and I'm going to be playing with this one.

**[Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler):** When this guy first got printed everyone seemed to go nuts over it. It's not very long before [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) really starts to get out of hand. My opponent needs to have a removal spell or this guy will get huge all on its own. It's more budget-friendly these days, so it won't be emptying anyone's bank account. (You can get it for less than 0.5 tix online.)

**[Ghostly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/83/ghostly-changeling):** It's a three-cost changeling that can be pumped—I like the sound of that. Slimer will demand a blocker every turn he attacks.

**[Moonglove Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/67/moonglove-changeling):** Deathtouch doesn't really seems relevant once I consider how big it'll be once I have crucible in play. If I could see myself cutting a changeling, it would definitely be this one.

**[Changeling Berserker](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/160/changeling-berserker):** I can see the champion ability helping my deck against board-sweeping effects. If we play a few copies of [Shriekmaw](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/85/shriekmaw) we could use the champion ability to its full potential. We might play this if we have a few copies of [Shriekmaw](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/85/shriekmaw).

The deck needs to have a good suite of support cards too. Red and black are a pretty good duet in the support department. We can play a good amount of reach and just enough disruption. The cards that first jump to mind in this category:

**[Shriekmaw](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/85/shriekmaw):** [Shriekmaw](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/85/shriekmaw) is quite the fine gentleman. He has the courtesy to remove one of my opponent's creatures and the class to stick around and fight afterward. He works extremely well with Changeling Beserker too.

**[Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate):** An extremely efficient removal spell that can go to the face in the late game. I'm definitely going to play four of these in a deck like this.

**[Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray):** A very underrated removal spell that gets rid of [Kitchen Finks](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/216/kitchen-finks) permanently. It's good against Faeries, Kithkin, and any deck with Kitchen finks. I'm not sure, but I don't think I can think of many decks that fall outside these categories.

**[Flame Javelin](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DD2-53/flame-javelin):** Our mana base probably can't support it, but I thought I'd talk about [Flame Javelin](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DD2-53/flame-javelin) because it would be so good in a non-budget version of this deck that played more nonbasic land. I can't stress how important it is to have burn that goes to the head when you're playing a creature-based deck. Four damage for one card is a fine deal these days.

**[Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast):** Again, this is a very good removal spell for a deck like ours. Like [Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray), [Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast) also permanently kills [Kitchen Finks](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/216/kitchen-finks).

**[Blightning](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/198/blightning):** Three to the dome with card advantage attached. This card is incredible; the fact that Zoo decks in Extended are playing with it should give you some idea of how good it is.

**[Raven's Crime](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/95/ravens-crime):** A good way to disrupt control decks and get rid of your excess land.

*(Full decklist with sideboard: `decklist_1_Changeling_Zoo_First_Take.txt`; priced breakdown: `decklist_1_Changeling_Zoo_First_Take_priced.md`)*

I played some games with this list and it was fun, but I still had a lot of trouble winning games with it. I would sometimes have huge creatures in play and be so far behind that I couldn't get back in the game, and I would sometimes get an early lead and then just get stamped out of the game by a Five-Color Control deck. The more I though about it, the more I decided that more burn solved all these problems.

It's my Copernican revelation in **Magic**. Copernicus figures out that the Earth is round and it rotates around the Sun. Nothing changes; the Earth was always round, and it always orbited the Sun. But, in fact, everything changes. Every time I'm working on an aggressive deck, I fall victim to "powerful" effects. The more I play, though, the more I realize that my decks should just be playing more burn. That never changes; the burn spells just changed names.

Here's the reworked list:

  *(23 lands)*

This version of the deck did much better. I was now playing enough removal to make my monsters actually relevant. I played a bunch of games with the deck like this and found that it did very well against a lot of the decks I was playing in the Casual Room.

##### Game 1 vs. Wort Goblins

[![Taurean Mauler](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/tdc/238?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler)

I start with the play, and I keep a pretty nice hand of [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse), [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Skeletal Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/140/skeletal-changeling), [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler), [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler), and [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate). My opponent keeps his starting seven also. My [Skeletal Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/140/skeletal-changeling) is the first play of the game. He plays an Adder-Staff Boggart and we both reveal lands off the clash. My land goes to the bottom, and I draw a [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire). I play a [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) and pass. My opponent plays a Boggart Harbinger and gets Wort, Boggart Auntie. On my turn I play [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire) and attack with both my creatures. The [Skeletal Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/140/skeletal-changeling) gets double-blocked, and my opponent takes 6 damage. On his turn he plays the Wort and passes. I have the [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) in my hand, and I attack for 7. He plays a few more creatures after this, but the gigantic [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) just became The Abyss.

1-0

##### Game 2 vs. Elves

I started on the play with a hand of [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), [Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray), [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler), [Blightning](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/198/blightning), and [Blightning](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/198/blightning). My opponent also kept, and the first play of the game, a [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), was met with a [Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray) from me. His second turn play, [Wren's Run Vanquisher](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/447/wrens-run-vanquisher), met an [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate). I had to worry about the Chameleon Colossus in his hand, which he'd revealed for [Wren's Run Vanquisher](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/447/wrens-run-vanquisher), and I had to make sure he discarded it before he hit his fourth land. I played [Blightning](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/198/blightning) the next turn and he discarded some lands. Bad news—he played a land and another Vanquisher on his turn. On my turn I got to make him discard his last two cards with [Blightning](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/198/blightning), including that Colossus. I drew an [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) to deal with the Vanquisher on the next turn. I thought my [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) would go all the way, but he drew another Chameleon Colossus and the game was over.

1-1

##### Game 3 vs. Reanimator

I started on the draw and kept [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Fire-Belly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/51/fire-belly-changeling), [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), [Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast), and [Ghostly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/83/ghostly-changeling). I started as the aggressor and kept pressure on my opponent throughout the early game. He managed to reanimate a [Woodfall Primus](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/322/woodfall-primus) after playing an [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) to kill my team. I had an [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) in my hand, though, and he was at 6 life. I sent the [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) at his head and drew for my turn ... [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain)! Damn. He attacked for 6 and I got to draw again ... [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain)! Damn. He attacked for 6 and I got to draw again ... [Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast)! Ding! Game over.

2-1

##### Game 4 vs. Bant

I start on the draw and have to mulligan to five, I end up keeping [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse), [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler), and [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler). I didn't draw another nonland card for the duration of this game. My opponent had a [Bant Charm](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/181/bant-charm) for my first [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) and an [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring) for the second one. I don't think I would have had a shot in this game even if I did draw the land. My opponent had a turn-two Sigiled Paladin backed up with [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring), [Bant Charm](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/181/bant-charm), [Rafiq of the Many](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/352/rafiq-of-the-many), and Battlegrace Angel.

2-2

##### Game 5 vs. Jund Tokens

I start on the play and keep [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse), [Ghostly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/83/ghostly-changeling), [Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast), [Puncture Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/eve/60/puncture-blast), and [Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray). My opponent makes a turn-two [Dragon Fodder](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/535/dragon-fodder), and I play my [Ghostly Changeling](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/83/ghostly-changeling) on turn three. He plays a [Sprouting Thrinax](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/278/sprouting-thrinax). On my turn I play my fourth land, I drew an incinerate. I decided to pass the turn which was definitely a mistake. I should have [Magma Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/akh/141/magma-spray)ed the [Sprouting Thrinax](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/278/sprouting-thrinax) and then [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate)d it, letting my opponent get three tokens isn't something I can encourage. I decided to do it on my opponent's combat step but he had a [Tarfire](https://scryfall.com/card/ddt/55/tarfire) to kill it himself and get the tokens. After combat he played another [Sprouting Thrinax](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/278/sprouting-thrinax)—this game was going to be pretty tough.

I drew and played [Taurean Mauler](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/238/taurean-mauler) and passed. He played his fifth land and played a [Torrent of Souls](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/213/torrent-of-souls) targeting the [Sprouting Thrinax](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/278/sprouting-thrinax) in his graveyard. He attacked me for 19. I could block two creatures, but I still wouldn't have any way to win the game. I conceded. I really love the token deck, and I missed getting to play with it. I've gotten a lot of emails about the [Sprouting Souls deck](https://web.archive.org/web/20090602011402/https://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/boab/8) and I'm happy a lot more people are starting to play with it.

2-3

I thought this deck was a blast to play and I hope some of you decide to play it amongst your friends. I didn't fare exceptionally well in the Casual Room, but I had a lot of fun playing with [Crucible of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/122/crucible-of-fire). If you can afford some upgrades and a new mana base, then the deck might play well at the tournament level.

I'd suggest playing [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout) against Kithkin in your sideboard. It's also quite strong against the token decks. Unfortunately, there isn't really a good way to deal with [Sprouting Thrinax](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/278/sprouting-thrinax). I'd also like to talk about [Vithian Stinger](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/115/vithian-stinger). [Vithian Stinger](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/115/vithian-stinger) is really strong against Faerie decks when you're the aggressor. If you're playing a deck that's controlling at all, then the Stinger isn't the best choice. Our deck plays enough two- and three-drops that I could see bringing in a few Stingers. I finished off the sideboard with the [Shriekmaw](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/85/shriekmaw) and Changeling Beserker combo; if you're playing in the Casual Room then it's important to play something that can kill a Mycoloth or some other giant devour monster.
