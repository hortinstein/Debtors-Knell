Welcome back to Building on a Budget! In [last week's article](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042113/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb99), I took my old Rakdos Control deck and tweaked it with several *Time Spiral* cards. At the end of last week's article, I made the decision to take Back to the Rak from the casual to the tournament room of **Magic** *Online*. What does this mean for the evolution of this deck?

> 1.) The competition will be a lot stiffer in the competitive room. This is where people go to test their Pro Tour Qualifier and Friday Night Magicdecks. In general, the people in the competitive room are there to win, and it tends more towards Spikes than towards Timmies or Johnnies (although not exclusively!)
>  2.) Instead of single games, full matches of best two-out-of-three are more typically played in the tournament room. It's not enough to win just the first game - I have to be able to take two games in a set to walk away with a winning record.
>  3.) Just like in tournament play, I'll have to add a sideboard to the deck. A sideboard is a set of 15 cards that is kept separate from the main deck. Between games one and two, and then between games two and three (if necessary), you are allowed to swap cards between your main deck and sideboard on a one-for-one basis.

Jeff Cunningham wrote a most incredible article about sideboards and sideboarding as part of the [Magic Academy](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042113/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/columnarchive) on this website (see "[Introducing Sideboards](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042113/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/academy/20)"). I suggest everyone check out that article, even if you're an experienced player, because Jeff explains clearly and succinctly how and why a sideboard works. This is one of the best articles ever written about sideboards and sideboarding, so you're doing yourself a disservice if you've skipped down to this sentence without clicking on the above link and reading through!

Word of Seizing

In the end, I decided to go with the versatility model of Jeff Cunningham's sideboard strategy. I wasn't sure which decks were dominating the Standard Online metagame (a fancy word for the makeup of decks being played at the time), so I wanted to fill the sideboard with the most broadly-usable sideboard cards possible. I ended up putting Hit/Run and [Seal of Doom](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/83/seal-of-doom) in the board, because they had narrowly been cut from my main deck, and I wanted to have tools against larger creatures such as those in Angel decks, [Loxodon Hierarch](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/15/loxodon-hierarch), and Reanimator. I also added [Sudden Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/194/sudden-shock) as a way to burn through permission decks, and a fourth [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister) for the aggro-on-aggro match-up.

My last choice was [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/118/blood-moon).

[![Blood Moon](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/2xm/118?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/118/blood-moon)

Everyone loves their new, shiny non-basic lands. There are a lot of them! Right now, I can think of dozens (literally) of non-basic lands that might show up in any given Standard deck - the ten *Ravnica* bounce lands, the ten *Ravnica* dual lands, the ten *9th Edition* painlands, [Quicksand](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/245/quicksand), [Desert](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/233/desert), the Urzatron three, [Pendelhaven](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/244/pendelhaven), the five *Time Spiral* storage lands, and other assorted guild lands from *Ravnica* block. With so many deck relying so heavily on non-basic lands, it seems like [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/118/blood-moon) can shut down their mana better than any of the land destruction spells listed above ([Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain), [Flashfires](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/183/flashfires), Boiling Seas), and just outright win games by color-screwing my opponents. My deck is blissfully unaffected by said [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/118/blood-moon), since I'm running all basic lands (plus, half my deck is red anyhow).

##### Match1: Netpuppy (B/R Storm)

Game 1: This is similar to my Blue/Red Storm deck from [a few weeks ago](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042113/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb89), but with black instead of blue. Around the 5th turn, he [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot)s me for four, and then throws down [Ignite Memories](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/164/ignite-memories) for five. Well, that puts me at 16 before the Ignite, and my hand is [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), [Phyrexian Totem](https://scryfall.com/card/dde/20/phyrexian-totem) and [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer). I end up taking nine (bringing me down to seven), but he has no cards left in hand and is out of gas. I already have a [Phyrexian Totem](https://scryfall.com/card/dde/20/phyrexian-totem) on the board, so I drop the second one and beat him to death quickly for ten a turn.

Game 2: Out: 3 [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/120/rakdos-pit-dragon), 1 [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer). In: 3 [Sudden Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/194/sudden-shock), 1 [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister).

[![Bottled Cloister](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rvr/252?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister)

My plan this game is to aggressively ramp to [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister), since he cannot effectively use [Ignite Memories](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/164/ignite-memories) if I have no hand on his turn. Amusingly enough, he drops a turn 4 Cloister, and I answer with turn 4 Cloister of my own. This not only makes me dodge his Ignites, but it also means that I can swing freely with my [Phyrexian Totem](https://scryfall.com/card/dde/20/phyrexian-totem)s, as he has no cards in hand on my turn. I swing him down to four with a [Seal of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/50/seal-of-fire) on the board, and he then casts [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) for three, and then again for four! I can't swing in with my Totem (since he'll block with everything, and make me lose all my permanents), so I swing in with a [Jagged Poppet](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/115/jagged-poppet), lose three [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)s in my hand when he blocks with five guys (to prevent [Seal of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/50/seal-of-fire) shenanigans on my side), and then swings back for a ton. This is followed by a lot of spells, culminating in [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot) for six and [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot) for seven. What a great comeback!

Game 3: Out: 2 [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer), 2 Jaya Ballard In: 4 Hit/Run.

This starts the same as the last game - we both get turn 4 [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister). However, I sided in the Hit/Runs after I saw the [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister) on his side, and I have one in my hand on turn 5, which allows me to Hit/Run his [Bottled Cloister](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/252/bottled-cloister) - and entire hand! - out of the game. He can't recover from a five-card Mind Twist with kicker four damage, and I drop a pair of [Jagged Poppet](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/115/jagged-poppet)s to seal the deal.
 Record: 1-0

So far, I haven't had any luck with [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/120/rakdos-pit-dragon), so it's time to bid him adieu. In its place I added a forum suggestion: [Crypt Champion](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/40/crypt-champion). The Champion works well with my current creature base, because all of the other creatures I have are three mana or less. In addition, he is very helpful against mass-removal - I lose guys to [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god), and suddenly I get two creatures back the following turn (The [Crypt Champion](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/40/crypt-champion) plus another creature) instead of just one.

Also, it was pointed out to me in the forums that I have a little too much black mana in the deck, and too little red mana. This is less-important now that I've zonked out the [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/120/rakdos-pit-dragon)s, but it's still a valid complaint - the deck has become more red heavy, and [Shadow Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/46/shadow-guildmage)/[Crypt Champion](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/40/crypt-champion)/Jaya Ballard all need red mana to activate.

I've had a few mulligans the last few games, so I think that after testing a lot, I just need to add in another land. I take out a [Crypt Champion](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/40/crypt-champion) to add a [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp). Also, I'm not happy with [Sudden Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/194/sudden-shock) (which I haven't really had a use for yet) or Hit/Run (there's a reason I yanked it from my deck to begin with!). So far, I've faced several decks that care about the graveyard. Because of this, I want a way to hose my opponent's bin. I choose [Cremate](https://scryfall.com/card/rtr/59/cremate) over [Withered Wretch](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/45/withered-wretch) and [Tormod's Crypt](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/235/tormods-crypt) - I think it's easier to get {B} than {B}{B} with this deck (especially if I need to [Cremate](https://scryfall.com/card/rtr/59/cremate) on turns 1 or 2), and [Tormod's Crypt](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/235/tormods-crypt) is a bit pricy right now. I also swap in [Phthisis](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/105/phthisis) for Hit/Run - this deck can often get to seven mana in a long game, and so I want a tool that will just outright kill my opponent if they have something like [Avatar of Woe](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/57/avatar-of-woe) (assuming I can deal a little extra damage) or an 11/11 [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll), rather than the tempo-jamming Hit/Run (I already have a lot of three drops).
