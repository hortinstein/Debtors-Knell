When I first got my hands on MX3SERZE's take on a black-white control deck, it broke Rule #1 (not enough lands—only twenty with the average mana cost of the deck coming in at a hefty 3.9), Rule #2 (63 cards—mathematically, you want to be at 60 unless you're playing [Battle of Wits](https://scryfall.com/card/m13/44/battle-of-wits). Otherwise, you have less chance to draw any given card in your deck), and Rule #4 (the deck didn't get started with an offense until turn four, at the earliest).

I fixed #2 (taking the deck down to 60 cards) and #4 (added in lower-drop cards like [Castigate](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/106/castigate), [Phyrexian Arena](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/180/phyrexian-arena), and Descendent of Kiyomaro), but I only partially fixed #1—I brought the land count from 20 to 21, but that's still not enough for this deck. As mentioned above, I really need to be able to drop a land every turn. [Orzhov Basilica](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/292/orzhov-basilica) is great for this, because it means I get three land drops out of two lands (first, I play a [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp). Then, I play the Basilica returning the [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp). Then I replay the [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp) again). Rather than up the budget of this deck with any more fancy lands that produce black and white, I just decide to add two [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)s and a [Plains](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/866/plains). I haven't particularly had troubles getting the *colors* of land I need in previous games, I just haven't gotten enough lands, period.

The original version of MX3SERZE's deck had seven [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) effects (one of them got left off the deck list last week, so it only showed six. Don't worry—this week's article is 300% more error-free than last week's!) The two games I lost so far this week (against beanie414's Sliver deck and Flywheel's Red-Green Beasts deck) highlight the need for some form of mass removal—*especially* in a black-white deck! Those two colors tend to get the most "kill everything at once" cards (as opposed to red, which gets the "deal damage to everything at once" cards).

[Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) and [Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation) are not budget cards, but there are several budget versions of Wrath—for instance, [Austere Command](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/121/austere-command) (1 ticket), [Kirtar's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/35/kirtars-wrath) (2.5 tickets), [Solar Tide](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/24/solar-tide) (0.25 tickets), Sunscour (0.75 tickets), and [Plague Wind](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/102/plague-wind) (0.25 tickets, though probably too high a mana cost to run). [Hour of Reckoning](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/120/hour-of-reckoning) was intentionally left off this list as it has very white-intensive color requirements compared to the other cards. This does not include cards like [Infest](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/114/infest) or [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter), which give a blanket -X/-X to all creatures.

If I were to further evolve MX3SERZE's black-white deck, I would likely move towards having at least a four-set of some [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) effect, and take out the [Hide // Seek](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/151/hide-seek)s entirely—while they are a really nifty trick, unless you're playing against an obscure combo deck that only has a one-of on certain win conditions, taking a single copy of a single card out of your opponent's deck isn't going to win you many games. There are already ways to gain life in this deck (Descendant, [Blind Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/166/blind-hunter)), so losing those three won't hurt too much.

Let's run this down my five deck building rules:

**Rule #1: Fix your Mana Base**

21 lands again seems low for a deck with an average mana cost of 3.5, and no mana accelerants at all. Without going into huge mathematical equations, one-third of the cards in the deck are land, meaning that you'll only see an average of about three lands in your first nine cards of the game. (Yes, I know this is not the *exact* odds, but it's close enough to make this argument). The average mana cost of the deck is higher than this amount, so often you'll be stuck with cards in hand (much less being able to play multiple cards a turn).

**Rule #2: Keep Close to 60 Cards**

Again, there are 63 cards in this deck. There's no reason to go above 60—all it does is decrease your chances of drawing the cards you need / want the most when you need / want them the most! I think we can find three cards to cut from this deck, just to get down to 60.

**Rule #3: Focus Your Goals, But Don't Be a Slave to Your Theme**

Flywheel has a really good Beasts theme going here—[Wirewood Savage](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/6/wirewood-savage) and Æther Charge are two particularly potent non-Beast cards in a Beast deck. I also like the utility present in [Hull Breach](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/185/hull-breach) and the removal spells, but I wonder if there are better on-theme choices for these cards? Also, [Steely Resolve](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/286/steely-resolve) seems a little unnecessary here—crunch my guys all you want, I'll make more!

**Rule #4: Have a Curve**

This deck starts at three mana and ends at five. In the meanwhile, there aren't many spells you can play early on in the game. This is fine, given that once turn three starts, you'll be dropping a creature a turn, and never stop putting on pressure from that point onwards. A little mana acceleration might go a long way here, just to throw that out in the open!

**Rule #5: Enchant Creature Cards Are Not Your friends**

Does not apply to this deck!

[![Contested Cliffs](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/c13/282?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/282/contested-cliffs)

This deck has a very solid base, but the above mentions are keeping it from reaching a higher potential. Here's the changes I made to the deck, with the reasons listed:

**Out:** 2 [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), 1 [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) 
**In:** 3 [Contested Cliffs](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/282/contested-cliffs)

Reason: In a 60 card deck, you want some degree of redundancy in your removal. The [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate)s and single [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) seem somewhat arbitrary in this deck—there isn't a great chance of drawing either one, and there doesn't seem to be a reason why there is one [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) over a third [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate)... or vice versa. If Flywheel was having problems with one-drops, more [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock)s would be the answer. If it was regenerators or three-toughness creatures, [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate). As it is, I'd rather run [Contested Cliffs](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/282/contested-cliffs) in the deck. It solves two problems at once—working more lands into the deck (three more lands, in this case), and it allows you to use those land slots as removal spells. [Krosan Warchief](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/155/krosan-warchief) can let you have your Beasts survive the Cliffs, if your Beasts aren't just outright bigger than your opponent's creatures to begin with.

**Out:** 3 [Hull Breach](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/185/hull-breach)
**In:** 4 [Indrik Stomphowler](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/297/indrik-stomphowler)

Reason: *Invasion* / *Planeshift* / *Apocalypse* cards are worth a premium on **Magic Online**, as those sets were not in circulation for a very long time. Because of this, [Hull Breach](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/185/hull-breach) goes for about a ticket. There are other options to destroy an artifact or enchantment (though not as many to destroy both at once), and these are options that cost a fraction of the amount—[Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize), [Krosan Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/198/krosan-grip), and the such. Well, this is a Beast deck—why not run [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize) on a 4/4 body in the inimitable [Indrik Stomphowler](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/297/indrik-stomphowler)? It servers a dual purpose in the deck—to budgetize the artifact/enchantment removal for the deck, and to add another Beast to the deck!

**Out:** 3 [Steely Resolve](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/286/steely-resolve)
**In:** 4 [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves)

Reason: [Steely Resolve](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/286/steely-resolve) is in the deck to keep a creature type untargetable. Well, it seems just as good to keep one creature at a time untargetable, especially since [Steely Resolve](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/286/steely-resolve) A) doesn't stop mass removal, and B) keeps you from targeting your own Beasts with things like [Krosan Warchief](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/155/krosan-warchief) and [Contested Cliffs](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/282/contested-cliffs). Giving your Beasts haste (especially since they are kind of slow to begin with) seems ideal, so [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves) is a great swap straight in for Resolve—haste is definitely a trade this deck wants to make in exchange for mass-untargetablility.

**In:** 4 [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth)
**Out:** 4 [Hunting Moa](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/60/hunting-moa)

Reason: If this deck needs acceleration, there are a lot of choices—[Wild Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/mkc/195/wild-growth), [Utopia Sprawl](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/159/utopia-sprawl), [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), Search for Tommorrow, [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach)—but in this deck I choose [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth). This is for two reasons: 1) This deck needs a lot of green mana—triple green for [Fangren Firstborn](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/75/fangren-firstborn) and [Beast Attack](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/43/beast-attack), green mana for [Krosan Warchief](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/155/krosan-warchief) to regenerate creatures, and [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth) definitely provides this green, and 2) I am considering playing a couple of copies of Warp World in this deck if it needs a big reset button against decks with a lot of mass removal, and [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth) is a great compliment to Warp World. I take out [Hunting Moa](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/60/hunting-moa) because I really don't want to have a creature that forces me to pay echo on turn three. I want to drop a three-drop, then a four-drop, not drop a three-drop, and then pay echo.

**In:** 1 [Fangren Firstborn](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/75/fangren-firstborn)
**Out:** 4 [Petravark](https://scryfall.com/card/tor/109/petravark), 2 [Pulse of the Tangle](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/80/pulse-of-the-tangle)

Reason: I needed to cut three cards from this deck entirely, to bring it down to 60 cards. I already added five Beasts ([Fangren Firstborn](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/75/fangren-firstborn) and 4 Stomphowler), so losing 10 Beasts (2 [Pulse of the Tangle](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/80/pulse-of-the-tangle), 4 [Hunting Moa](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/60/hunting-moa), 4 [Petravark](https://scryfall.com/card/tor/109/petravark)) seems all right for now, especially given that several Beasts let me play multiple other Beasts ([Avarax](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/112/avarax), [Beast Attack](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/43/beast-attack)). I really liked (feared) [Fangren Firstborn](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/75/fangren-firstborn) when I played against it, and it is even better with [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves) in the deck (speeding up not only the Firstborn, but every other attacking creature at the time). [Pulse of the Tangle](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/80/pulse-of-the-tangle) seemed like the weakest Beast in the deck—it would almost never "buyback," since this deck is never going to want to hold back creatures. For those not familiar, PDC stands for Pauper Deck Challenge. Pauper decks are all-common decks—no uncommons or rares allowed! In fact, if a card is not common in that format, you can't play it, even if it's common in other formats! (For instance—Tribal Flames is common in *Planeshift*, and "timeshifted" in *Time Spiral*. You can't play Tribal Flames in Standard PDC, because there is no common version in Standard. You can play either version ("timeshifted" or *Planeshift*) in Extended, since there is a common version that exists in that format).
