The Message Boards from [last week's article](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042253/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm127) were diverse enough that I honestly didn't know which card would get picked in the poll. I readied myself to play Bloodletter Quill in a Black/Blue life-drain deck. I dreamed of using Polymorph on a Genju for Mindleech Mass. I thought of [Cerulean Sphinx](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/36/cerulean-sphinx) in a dedicated Blue/Black control deck with (gasp!) counterspells. Spawnbroker readied his [Dizzy Spell](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/43/dizzy-spell) and Imaginary Pet. All offbeat ideas, and all ideas I was prepared to make work.

Instead, you all let me off easy...

I begin my exploration of House Dimir with [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps), a card just begging to be used in sick and twisted ways. [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) was the one card on the list that triggered multiple deck ideas for me (thank goodness Relentless Rats is no longer in Standard or there would be no controlling me), so I suppose it was inevitable that it would attract would-be deckbuilders to its cause.

For those of you new to the column, my goal is to slowly tinker my way through a deckbuilding exercise over the next three weeks. Usually when I engage in these deck evolutions, I begin with a preconstructed deck as a base and slowly change it until it's a fun, respectable budget deck. Sometimes I try different starting points, however, such as the time [I began with](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042253/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm114) the “cheap” rare [Blood Clock](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/153/blood-clock). Today is a lot like the [Blood Clock](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/153/blood-clock) experiment in that I'm starting from a single card as a foundation.

Specifically, here are my guidelines over the next three weeks:

- Start with a “cheap rare” (as defined by prices online) and brainstorm a first-draft, Standard, Blue/Black deck around that card.
- Don't make changes until playing the deck in at least five games.
- Change no more than five cards at a time.
- Keep four copies of the rare in the deck at all times.
- Build a respectable deck that's fun to play.
- Build an affordable deck.

As I've said before, these guidelines are meant to be broken from time to time (especially the “five cards at a time” one, which I seem to violate at least once per series). Mostly they're around to help ensure that I go slow, pay attention to the changes I'm making to my deck and understand why I'm making those changes. As I've also said, you should expect to disagree with me at several points along the way--It's my deck, and my goal isn't so much to give you a decklist to copy as much as it is to inspire you to build your own deck. What's important here is that we all enjoy the ride and that I end up with a budget deck that's fun to play and wins its fair share of games in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*.

With introductions out of the way, let's get to know my new features card...

##### [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps): An Overview

Here is how [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) looks straight out of the booster pack.

[![Followed Footsteps](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/cn2/111?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps)

Matt Cavotta [described](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042253/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/mc8) [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) as the elusive “home run,” a card where the mechanic, art, card name, and flavor text all align perfectly into a tasty package of, um, tastiness. Let's look beyond the flavor, though, and head straight to its deckbuilding potential.

What do we have here? First thing to notice is that it's an Aura generally and an Enchant Creature specifically. This almost always means that there is a possibility of losing two cards (the Footsteps and my enchanted creature) for only one card from my opponent (like, say, [Dark Banishing](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/123/dark-banishing)). A lot of Auras these days pack a bunch of side benefits to make this drawback less drawback-ish, such as [Genju of the Fields](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/5/genju-of-the-fields) (comes back to your hand), [Fists of Ironwood](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/139/fists-of-ironwood) (gives you creatures), [Indomitable Will](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/109/indomitable-will) (played at instant speed), and [Clinging Darkness](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/80/clinging-darkness) (effectively a creature removal spell). [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) has none of these benefits. It's simply an interesting effect that has just as many two-for-one dangers as a [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor) or [Spirit Link](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/29/spirit-link).

The other thing to note about [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) being an Aura, I suppose, is that I happen to be making a deck outside of the “typical” Aura colors. That is, White is into Auras all over the place via cards like Auratouched Mage and [Tallowisp](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/25/tallowisp). Green is into Auras via Enchantresses and really beefy Auras. Green/White is thus usually considered the “typical” Aura color combination. Neither Blue nor Black tend to have any card specifically geared towards making Auras more beneficial than normal. That's two strikes so far against my new feature card.

On the positive side, [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) has a really cool effect. Put it on any creature--mine or an opponent's--and I get a copy of that creature every turn. If I have a fattie like [Moss Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/228/moss-kami), I will eventually have an army of said fatties. If I happen to have an uber-fattie like [Verdant Force](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/187/verdant-force), well, things are about to get silly. Same thing is true if my opponent is packing big, mean creatures, with the side benefit that if my opponent's fattie happens to be a legend then [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is like delayed creature removal. This makes the Footsteps not only interesting but strategically versatile.

With such a cool effect, it might be difficult to understand why [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is a “cheap” rare. After all, how can a flavorful home-run with *break me* written across its forehead be relegated to the bargain bin? Part of the answer lies in the aforementioned fragility of Auras. The other part of the answer lies in the Footsteps' casting cost. {3}{U}{U} is hard for any Blue deck to muster reliably. Green and Red are flush with mana-acceleration, but Blue views five mana as just plain daunting. Add that to the fact that you won't see it start working until the turn *after* you've cast it, and [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) starts to look slow, fragile, and otherwise pretty dicey.

Still, those are some distracting words tattooed across its forehead. And it's so cute, too. Let's proceed.

As I ponder building a deck around a single card, I often brainstorm the generic implications for using it in a deck. Here are some quick observations as I think through [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps).

*Use creatures*

I know this sounds dumb, but Enchant Creature cards are a lot less effective unless you happen to have a creature on the table to enchant. As such, any deck packing [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) needs to have a fairly high quotient of creatures. Moreover, those creatures should generally be things you would like to multiply. [Verdant Force](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/187/verdant-force) is sure cool, but copying a [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) turn after turn is decidedly less cool. That brings me to my next observation...

*Some Creatures Are Better Followed Than Others*

[![Rootrunner](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/chk/237?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/237/rootrunner)

Let's continue with my mythical Green/Blue [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) deck. [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) might not be great targets, but what about [Wood Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/EXO-130/wood-elves)? [Carven Caryatid](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/250/carven-caryatid)? [Viridian Shaman](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/25/viridian-shaman)? [Scion of the Wild](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/160/scion-of-the-wild)? [Rootrunner](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/237/rootrunner)? [Hana Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/147/hana-kami)? You get the idea. The mental experiment is to try and imagine what sorts of creature you wish you could use more than four of in your deck. Comes-into-play and leaves-play critters are always good ideas, as are cards that benefit from having multiples in play like Zuberas.

*Fat = Phat*

Big, mean creatures are also good targets. An opponent may be able to handle one Ancient Silverback, but what one per turn? I think a lot of people look at [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) and think of Smashing their opponents into oblivion under a sea of fatties. Personally, I don't see anything wrong with this instinct. What you need to guard against is using [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) as a “win more” card that you could easily drop from your deck. That's why I tend to ponder the comes-into-play and Beast of Burden-type tricks more than the BOOM! CRUNCH! WHAMMO! ones.

*Legendary Fat = Not So Phat*

I mentioned before that copying an opponent's legendary creature is a decent way to kill it in a pinch. The same goes for your own legendary creatures, which turns out to be a much less clever play. The possible exception to this thought is Kokusho, the Evening Star, which I could imagine as a viable target for [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) even if it were sitting on my side of the table.

*Other Creature Duplicants Are Good*

Sakashima the Impostor

One way to really hammer home the idea of [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) as a feature card is to include other ways to mimic its effect. Creature reanimation is the obvious one, since a [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) is almost like a one-shot, delayed [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) for those yummy comes-into-play creatures or fatties. Try to expand beyond mere reanimation, though. Think bounce like [Peel from Reality](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/402/peel-from-reality), or shapeshifters like Sakashima the Impostor. These analogues not only tighten a deck's focus, they also make a deck less anemic if it happens to not draw [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps).

*Use Blue Mana*

Another obvious point? Maybe, but the problem with some feature cards is that people first start to brainstorm ideas outside of the card's color. Remember that [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) costs {3}{U}{U} to cast. If you don't have access to good dual lands, this means your deck should have more than a smattering of Blue mana in it. I happen to be starting off with a Blue/Black deck in mind so I may be safe here, but this paragraph should act as a word of caution for anyone tapping their lip about Auratouched Mage and [Yavimaya Enchantress](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/167/yavimaya-enchantress).

*Go Crazy. Have Fun.*

Finally, I think it's important to remember that [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is a weird card begging to be included in weird decks. It's not like [Savannah Lions](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/146/savannah-lions) where its purpose is bludgeoningly clear. It's also not a card to take the tournament scene by storm (though it does bear that break me tattoo for would-be Spike/Johnnies). It's a fun, funny card, a card meant to make your opponent grunt “huh,” when you play it then “huh” again when it starts rolling. In this way I think [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is a perfect entree into House Dimir because it's a tricky card meant to keep opposing decks off balance. It is the Footsteps' unpredictability that makes it almost as potent as its freaky cloning effect.

Now I'll try and put some of these observations to the test...

##### From Footprints to Blueprints

When I started my [Blood Clock](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/153/blood-clock) experiment, I brainstormed five initial decklists and had you choose one of them. This time I'm only making one deck. The disadvantage of this approach is that I might choose an initial path for the deck that you hate. The advantage is that I can start playing right away and continue playing after I submit the article. With only one deck, too, I can be pretty loose in my deck construction.

In fact, my intention is to take the words “first draft deck” to heart. I'm not going to worry about packing my deck with four copies of cards so much as trying lots of one-, two- and three-copies so that I'm able to try out a bunch of different ideas. I'm not worrying about card quality overmuch. If I'm interested in trying a card out because I've never played it, it's going in my deck. The result should be a deck somewhere between a typical preconstructed deck and a solid theoretical deck ready for playtesting.

Here is how I approach the deck:

*4 [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps)*

It's the feature card, and I promised four copies of it. My aim will be to justifiably keep four copies of [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) in the deck from start to finish.

*3 [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil)*

Thanks to the wickedness that is [Battle of Wits](https://scryfall.com/card/m13/44/battle-of-wits), [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil) is the only five-cost card with transmute. Still, a creature removal spell that can tutor for [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) using House Dimir's signature mechanic seems terrific to me. The downside is that I now have seven cards out of seven that cost five mana. Help!

*4 [Dimir Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/221/dimir-signet)*

Ahhh... That's better. Again, the emphasis is on House Dimir, which I like. It's also some of the only mana-acceleration available to Blue/Black decks in Standard right now. As a side benefit, I can see an opponent using an early [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize) on my Signet and not having it in hand when I finally cast [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps).

*3 [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction)*

[![Mark of Eviction](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rav/58?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction)

I swear, I'm listing these cards in the order that they occurred to me. I realized that I needed mana, so I added [Dimir Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/221/dimir-signet) and then scanned the lower cost cards available to Blue and Black. Lo and behold, there was [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction), a card that acts as a sort of analogue to [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) and can even help me reassign my Footsteps to another creature if needed. The defensive ability of [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction) is nice, too.

*2 [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes)*

Speaking of defense, I realized at this point that I needed to start loading up on creatures. [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) doesn't have any useful comes-into-play abilities, but it is a creature I can imagine wanting to duplicate turn after turn. Again, too, it's meant to attract [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize)-like effects away from [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps).

*2 [Drake Familiar](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/44/drake-familiar)*

I may be getting too cute here, but I figured that since I had seven Auras this might work out. Again, it's a way of reassigning [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) later in the game and can help slow down an opponent using enchantments.

*2 [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger)
 3 [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats)*

I'm committed to not having this deck degenerate into a tried and true reanimator deck. That said, [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger) is too cool of a [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) target in Blue/Black to pass up and [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats) is an all-around good early creature in a deck like mine. I'm interested to know whether the Rats' effect is worthy of me wanting to enchant it, so that's part of the reason for including it as well.

*2 [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)
 2 [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee)*

I like both and can't decide which I like better. I figure trying two of each will help me solve this mental quandry. With [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger) and these, my manacurve is again creeping skyward so I need to reign it in a bit.

*3 [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours)
 1 [Throat Slitter](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/37/throat-slitter)*

As much as this deck could become a straight reanimator deck, it could just as easily become a Ninja deck. That said, it's hard not to include at least a token Ninja pack since they interact so well with the other parts of the deck. I put a single [Throat Slitter](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/37/throat-slitter) in there understanding that, I suppose, I could tutor for it via [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil) if needed.

*2 [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)*

Does [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) enchanted with [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) make a new [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) each turn or the creature it [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)d? I don't know off the top of my head, but I aim to find out. Like [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction), [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger), and the Ninja, it's also a way to really focus on the key theme in the deck. I'm sort of maxed out on rares with the addition of [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), but I think it's still worth including in my deck for the chaos factor alone.

*1 [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber)*

I actually wanted to include more than one since [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) might be a key win condition for my deck. I don't see the room, though, until I start dropping some of the other four-cost creatures or simply culling the chaff from my deck. Eventually, maybe, this guy might make its way to center stage.

*2 [Surveilling Sprite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite)*

[![Surveilling Sprite](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ddh/43?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite)

##### First Footsteps

As always, I log my games in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*. My online handle is BuildingOnABudget, something I rarely think to mention yet a question that comes up weekly on e-mail. Anyway, please drop by to watch my games whenever I'm playing.

Speaking of playing, there is no better way to figure out changes to a draft decklist than playing said decklist. I assembled my [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) deck online, sat down at the first empty table I saw, and prayed my deck would do something cool.

*Game 1: Black/Green Golgari*

[![Highway Robber](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ddm/61?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber)

My opponent had a [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), [Elvish Skysweeper](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/104/elvish-skysweeper), and [Shambling Shell](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/57/shambling-shell). I had a bunch of Islands and no Swamps. On the fifth turn I found a Swamp to cast [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats), which made him discard Mausoleum Turnkey and eventually killed his Shell. I then got [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee) to kill one of his critters. My Banshee turned into [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours), allowing me to off a second creature. It's late and this article is already long, but I feel comfortable making one round of changes before signing off...

*OUT*: 2 [Surveilling Sprite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite)

Having a Turn 2 creature with evasion is good, and I often appreciated the card I got when it died. [Surveilling Sprite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite) makes my deck filled with just a few too many one-toughness creatures, though, and I always felt a little silly playing it. I think its biggest indictment is that I want to enchant it with neither [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) nor [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction). I still think it was a good call instead of a defender like [Minamo Scrollkeeper](https://scryfall.com/card/cns/101/minamo-scrollkeeper) or [Drift of Phantasms](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/42/drift-of-phantasms), but the Sprite has too little punch for this deck.

*OUT*: 1 [Drake Familiar](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/44/drake-familiar)

It was useful in one game for interesting Ninja tricks, and I like both its stats and the ability to reassign [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). With two I too often found myself with a [Drake Familiar](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/44/drake-familiar) stuck in hand, though. I'm keeping one in the deck (there's a method to my madness coming up, I promise), but only one.

*OUT*: 2 [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes)

I like the three toughness, I like the colorless mana cost, and once I even managed to get four [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) into play via [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). Truly, though, [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) is both a better lifegain option and better target for the Footsteps. If my deck was more focused on reanimation I could see [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) sticking around, but it's a nonsensical Ninja and [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction) target, which can't be said for [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber). Oh well... it was worth a try.

*IN*: 1 [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber)

[![Dimir Infiltrator](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/pca/86?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator)

See above. [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) might be my single best target for [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps), so I need to think really carefully about potentially adding two more to the deck. The problem is that my deck is now completely log-jammed in the four-mana slot thanks to the Robber, [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee), [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal), [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger), [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), and to a lesser extent [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours). I like all of these creatures so much that I'm not quite sure what to do about a deck trying to allow them all to coexist. This is one area where I would appreciate some Message Board feedback.

*IN*: 4 [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator)

I'm not entirely sure why, when making my first decklist, I avoided [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator). I probably thought it would be too hard to cast, which will still probably come up a lot. It's clear to me now, though, that the Infiltrator is a far superior Turn 2 creature compared to [Surveilling Sprite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite) and a perfect Ninja outlet. I can even--sort of--imagine enchanting this with [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) in a slow game or [Mark of Eviction](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/58/mark-of-eviction) to use the transmute ability for [Drake Familiar](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/44/drake-familiar) or [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats). Speaking of transmute, with four Infiltrators in the deck I need to pay close attention to two-mana cards to see if there are others I'd like to include in the deck.
