*I'm constantly looking for new ways to evolve Building on a Budget. Of all the columns on the site, BoaB is the one that has the greatest amount of reader input regarding content. This is intentional—I strive to incorporate as many reader ideas as possible, and to give you, the reader, the chance to influence upcoming columns.*

*The biggest problem I face is the ability to incorporate reader input in a timely fashion. This is because of my deadlines—articles that are posted at midnight on Wednesday are due in at 1 p.m. EST on Monday. This leaves me only five days to use poll results/forum ideas before my next article is due.*

*Ten Decks in Ten Weeks originated as an idea I pitched to Scott Johns and Kelly Digges—a way to give weekly continuity to the column, and to allow readers to determine deck focuses weeks in advance. I really, truly believed that 10 Decks in 10 Weeks would be the series within Building on a Budget that really made this column my own—something completely unique that neither Nate Heiss nor JMS had done before. I am thankful that Scott and Kelly let me run with this series, because it did indeed end up being the most popular segment of Building on a Budget from this past year. Here is the final part of the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks series, which includes links to the other parts.*

*While I probably won't do an exact copy of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks next year, I do have an idea for a continuous deck evolution that I think will blow people away. Look for it early next year!*

This week's article focuses around [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth), a rare from *Planar Chaos*. [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth) is part of the same cycle of creatures as [Aeon Chronicler](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler). Functionally, I could describe [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth) as the mismatched offspring of [Hunting Moa](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/60/hunting-moa) and [Emperor Crocodile](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/165/emperor-crocodile). Like the former, it gives +1/+1 counters to other creatures. Like the latter, if you control no other creatures, it dies.

10 Decks in 10 WeeksThat's the rub against [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth)—in almost all situations, it cannot be your only creature on the board. If you don't either suspend it put a counter on other creatures, or have other creatures in play with +1/+1 counters, it dies. In order to build a successful [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth) deck, you need creatures with +1/+1 counters—lots of them.

In order to get started, let's take out the black from last week's deck.

In: 8 [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island), 4 [Simic Growth Chamber](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/298/simic-growth-chamber)

I'm not going to lie—a lot of the green combo pieces from last week's deck aren't going to end up making the next cut—but I'm leaving them in to see what happens just in case. To start the deck off, I added in 4 [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu). This is one of the earliest drops that can get +1/+1 counters, and it works extremely well with [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth) and [Search for Tomorrow](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/137/search-for-tomorrow). Naturally, 4 copies of [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth) need to be in the deck, and I've returned [Draining Whelk](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/62/draining-whelk) from [A Blink in Time](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042754/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb107) to make a repeat appearance, four weeks later. I don't expect this version to play very well (there aren't enough creatures to reliably be able to play [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth)), but I want to see how the cards interact.

Game 1: Oldmanwinter (Mono-Blue Control)
 I get an early [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu) and follow it with [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth). He tries to [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) it, and I play [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), untap, and [Draining Whelk](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/62/draining-whelk). I then get double [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize), [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), two more [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth)s, and another [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest). He counters a couple of spells with [Muddle the Mixture](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/50/muddle-the-mixture) and [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang)s my Kudzu, but the Whelk goes all the way.
 Record: 1-0

Yeah, pretty much what I thought—I had lots of mana generation and barely any creatures. To fix the deck from this point, I took out a lot of the [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) engine pieces, and added in some good blue, green, and artifact creatures that deal in +1/+1 counters. [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/226/plaxcaster-frogling) is a 3/3 for three and [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/143/cytoplast-root-kin) is a 4/4 for four, and both give great benefits to other creatures with +1/+1 counters—they are auto-includes in this type of deck. I also added one each of four utility creatures—[Triskelavus](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/226/triskelavus) (a large flyer and tim), [Spike Feeder](https://scryfall.com/card/wc98/bs118/spike-feeder) (lifegain, and the ability to move around +1/+1 counters), [Spike Tiller](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/218/spike-tiller) (another Spike, and one that can make three more creatures with +1/+1 counters), and [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/23/cytoplast-manipulator) (a limited Control Magic).

I nearly beat him three times, once with [Triskelavus](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/226/triskelavus), once with [Spike Tiller](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/218/spike-tiller) plus [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth), and once with [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/143/cytoplast-root-kin) and [Draining Whelk](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/62/draining-whelk).

Game 5: hirohito_0 (Slivers)
 I get turn-two [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu), turn-three [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), turn-four [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/226/plaxcaster-frogling), turn five suspend [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth), turn six unsuspend, play [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/143/cytoplast-root-kin), attack with an 8/8 Kudzu and a 15/15 Behemoth.

I'll say this—when the deck works, it works fast. [Fungal Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/128/fungal-behemoth) virtually doubles the power I have on the board at the time, since almost all of my creatures are 0/0 dudes with +1/+1 counters.

To make the deck more explosive, I took out more engine pieces ([Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) was nice, but hardly necessary to smash down on an opponent), and added in [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse)s (there are great with [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu)—I play the Expanse, the Kudzu gets +1/+1, and I sacrifice the Expanse to get another land and another +1/+1 counter), [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) (to speed up the deck a turn, and to give me another creature for the deck), and a second [Spike Tiller](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/218/spike-tiller)—so far, I've been happy whenever I drew the first.
