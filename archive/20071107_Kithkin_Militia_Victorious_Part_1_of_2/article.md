I broke down the contents of the deck [a couple of weeks ago](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042841/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb135) and then held a vote to see how I should evolve the deck. So we'll be evolving the Kithkin Militia Theme Deck with the focus being mostly on Kithkin themselves. We want to turn our starting stack of sixty cards into a finely-honed deck capable of winning a majority of games. In order to get there, we'll definitely have to cut out some of the chaff and bring in substitutes from the sideline.

Well Zachary, you are correct—normally the fine folks at Wizards of the Coast provide my BuildingonaBudget account with a foursome of every card available online. Whenever I swap cards in and out of a deck, I check multiple sources online to see the price of the cards being changed, so that I am sure that the cards I am using remain in budget. However, you are also correct in saying that I normally do not have to put the *time* into tracking down these cards—they are already in my account.

Angry Mob So imagine my surprise when I logged into my account to start playtesting for this week, and found that, oops! someone had forgotten to load up any of the *Lorwyn* cards into my account! This left me with three choices:

1. Don't write my column this week, and let Scott Johns and Kelly Digges take turns playing "Human Piñata" with my strung-up body.
2. Switch this column with another one that didn't involve *Lorwyn* cards, which would make the "Reality Fracture" riots of 2006 seems like a cakewalk.

Although I knew that certain cards were not going to make the first cut out of the deck ([Goldmeadow Dodger](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/16/goldmeadow-dodger), anyone?), I wanted to get a feel for how the deck played, and to see if some of the marginal cards ([Kithkin Harbinger](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/26/kithkin-harbinger), [Surge of Thoughtweft](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/43/surge-of-thoughtweft)) were better than I gave them credit for in my initial evaluation.

**Game 1: LaurensDaddy (Blue-Black Tappers)**

He gets Royal Assassin and Frozen Solid, but I put the Assassin into the [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring), and start swinging with two-power Kithkin. He kills a couple of my guys with [Sudden Death](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/143/sudden-death) and [Tendrils of Corruption](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/145/tendrils-of-corruption). I then get [Kithkin Harbinger](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/26/kithkin-harbinger), and use it to fetch [Thoughtweft Trio](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/44/thoughtweft-trio) (championing the Harbinger itself). When the Trio bite the dust, I then can immediately fetch up a [Changeling Hero](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/9/changeling-hero). My Hero goes all the way.

Record: 1-0

**Game 2: OmniSemaj (Giants)**

He starts with a [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/119/stinkdrinker-daredevil) on turn three and starts dropping huge Giants from turn four onwards. I manage to stabilize the ground with [Thoughtweft Trio](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/44/thoughtweft-trio), [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn), and a [Knight of Meadowgrain](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/5/knight-of-meadowgrain) (9 points of first strike defense) until he gets out [Hamletback Goliath](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/332/hamletback-goliath) and follows it with a [Thundercloud Shaman](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/123/thundercloud-shaman) for five. This kills all of my creatures ([Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn) dies, making my [Thoughtweft Trio](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/44/thoughtweft-trio) a 5/5 with 5 points of damage, causing them to die). I manage to stay alive a turn by using [Crib Swap](https://scryfall.com/card/ecl/11/crib-swap) to take out his humongous [Hamletback Goliath](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/332/hamletback-goliath), but I die a short turn later.

Record: 1-1

**Game 3: Mossflowr (Elementals)**

He's playing every color except for white, and he gets down a Flamekin Spitfire on turn four. I have out [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn), [Kithkin Greatheart](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/21/kithkin-greatheart), and [Goldmeadow Harrier](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/3/goldmeadow-harrier) equipped with [Runed Stalactite](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/214/runed-stalactite). He starts dropping Æthersnipes, and I am forced to put Flameskin Spitfire into the [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring) to keep him from taking out my entire team (he is now at eight mana, and can start shooting down all my guys one by one).

I get my team back onto the board, and start racing him with [Kithkin Greatheart](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/21/kithkin-greatheart), now equipped by [Runed Stalactite](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/214/runed-stalactite) (making him a 5/4 first striker—2/1, +1/+1 and first strike because he is now a Giant, +1/+1 from the Stalactite, and +1/+1 from [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn)). [Goldmeadow Harrier](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/3/goldmeadow-harrier) keeps Mossflowr's Elementals from being able to attack or block effectively, and my small guys go all the way.

Record: 2-1

It's time for the first round of changes, and so far I know which cards have performed well—[Goldmeadow Harrier](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/3/goldmeadow-harrier) has kept my opponents from being able to engage in combat well. [Knight of Meadowgrain](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/5/knight-of-meadowgrain) has been a great weenie creature the time I've drawn him, [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn) has made my combat math work out terrifically, and [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring) is a "get-out-of-jail-free" card in case I'm stymied on offense.

To make room for them, I take out some of the more dodgy (get it! [Goldmeadow Dodger](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/16/goldmeadow-dodger)? Get it? Ok, put away those piñata sticks, folks) cards from the deck. None of my guys is really large enough to take advantage of [Battle Mastery](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/89/battle-mastery). [Kinsbaile Balloonist](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/23/kinsbaile-balloonist) is a Limited trick, and [Pollen Lullaby](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/26/pollen-lullaby) is more for a defensive deck. I also cut a [Plains](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/866/plains) (since most of my guys cost two or one mana), and a [Shields of Velis Vel](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/39/shields-of-velis-vel) ([Surge of Thoughtweft](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/43/surge-of-thoughtweft) seems much better for this deck, which is offensively oriented).

**Game 4: Thordonn (Merfolk)**

He keeps a one-land hand with a bunch of two-drops and never draws a land. This is why I try to mulligan any hands with only one land—even though the rest of your hand looks great, if you don't draw a land, you just plain lose. If you're running 24 lands in a deck, and you have 53 cards left in your deck (60 cards minus your original seven), you have less than a 50% chance of drawing a land on your first draw phase (53 cards left, versus 23 lands left). To make this point, I get [Goldmeadow Stalwart](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/18/goldmeadow-stalwart), [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn) and [Goldmeadow Harrier](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/3/goldmeadow-harrier), and they go all the way quickly.

Record: 3-1

**Game 5: chhaymam (White-Green Enchantress)**

He gets [Seal of Primordium](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/228/seal-of-primordium) and puts Griffin Guide on a [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger). I [Crib Swap](https://scryfall.com/card/ecl/11/crib-swap) the Ranger, which doesn't create a 2/2 flying token (since [Crib Swap](https://scryfall.com/card/ecl/11/crib-swap) removes from the game, and removed-from-the-game creatures don't hit the graveyard). He then puts [Verdant Embrace](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/173/verdant-embrace) on his 1/1 changeling token. I throw the token into the [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring), removing it from the game. He attempts to use [Seal of Primordium](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/228/seal-of-primordium) to kill my Ring, but this accomplishes nothing. Token creatures that are removed from the game cease to exist. This leaves him with only 2/2 [Yavimaya Enchantress](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/167/yavimaya-enchantress) (would have been 4/4 with the Seal and the Ring on the board), and I am able to run him over with my 3/3 guys (thanks to [Wizened Cenn](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/49/wizened-cenn)).

Record: 4-1

This change is easy—[Goldmeadow Stalwart](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/18/goldmeadow-stalwart) is essentially Isamaru, Hound of Konda for a Kithkin Weenie deck, and those two guys were an auto-include in any White Weenie deck of years past. To make room for them, I take out a pair of [Kithkin Greatheart](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/21/kithkin-greatheart) (which still are viable with the number of changelings in the deck), the [Kithkin Harbinger](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/26/kithkin-harbinger) (this deck is heading towards redundancy, and not a toolbox format, which makes tutoring for one specific Kithkin an ill-fated proposition), and the [Runed Stalactite](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/214/runed-stalactite) (if I want more equipment later, I'd probably be better off with the old standby of [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer)).
