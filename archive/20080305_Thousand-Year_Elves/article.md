# Thousand-Year Elves

**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** March 5, 2008  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb154>

---

Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! [Last week](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041549/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb153), I evolved a deck based around putting large amounts of Elves and Elf tokens into play, and then playing [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun). That deck did pretty well for testing, but I wanted the deck to have a little more ‘wow' factor while keeping it able to win games.

In the meanwhile, I was attending a Pro Tour Qualifier for Hollywood this past weekend (working, not playing!), and I had some time to chat it up with none other than [Bennie Smith](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041549/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/authorarchive), who many of you know and love from his column on this here website. Bennie and I were brainstorming ideas for Building on a Budget (sorry Bennie, no more [Momentary Blink](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/15/momentary-blink) decks for a while, even if they do involve Merieke Ri Berit), when Bennie mentioned that he had once read about a weird Japanese Elf deck that ran [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) and [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate)... but that he didn't know anything else about the deck.

Well, as those of you reading last week may recall, I tried out [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate) in my deck, but I took it out because as a 2/2 for four, it died the second it hit play without being able to pump any creatures. [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) would solve this problem—this artifact out of *Lorwyn* gives partial haste to creatures (they may use their tap ability the turn they come into play). In addition, [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) can untap any creature for the low cost of one mana, allowing you to reuse creatures with a tap ability a second time, or have a creature play offense and defense (much like a vigilance creature).

I took around the internets to see if I could see any mention of this Japanese deck, just to get ideas for this deck. I was completely unsuccessful in finding any information, but all that meant was that I would be building the deck just based on a rumor and heresy. The best place to start would be with my Overrun Elves deck from last week. I already had the deck built—it was just a matter of ripping out the parts, adding in new elements, and finding the smooth, smooth deckbuilding strategies to make the deck whole again.

Here's where I started:

The first thing I wanted to do was to add [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) and [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate) to the deck. I wouldn't need [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) anymore for the deck, so swapping those straight out for the Magistrates was an easy choice—they both serve the same purpose (pumping up my Elves for a lethal attack), but [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) wants a sheer number of creatures in play, gives a bonus for only one turn and distributes the +3/+3 and trample evenly, whereas [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate) wants Elves and only Elves, gives a permanent bonus, and can pump the same creature multiple times, if need be.

Out: 4 [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun)
 In: 4 [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate)

If I were going to run [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) and [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate), I'd want two types of cards in my deck—Elves and creatures with tap abilities. Thankfully, the deck was already chock-full of Elves—[Lys Alana Huntmaster](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/67/lys-alana-huntmaster), [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid), [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush), and [Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect). [Thelonite Hermit](https://scryfall.com/card/mkc/188/thelonite-hermit) is also technically an Elf, though he was in the deck to generate four Saproling tokens. Without [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) or the need for those four extra creatures, the Hermit was the next card I cut from the deck, in order to make room for the [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir).

Now, about those creatures with a tap ability—with [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir), I can drop any mana-producing Elves and chain them into play without a loss of tempo. For instance, say I get a first-turn [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), and follow them with a second-turn [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir). On my third turn, I can play [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid)s, and immediately tap the Druids for mana. Normally, the Druids would not be able to produce that mana the turn they came into play, meaning I would lose tempo on that mana I used to cast [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid) that turn—but with the Elixir, this was a non-starter. This also meant that [Yavimaya Dryad](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/247/yavimaya-dryad) wasn't as important, because I'd want to be dropping the Elixir or the Perfect on turn two (which would be a regular occurrence with eight mana Elves in the deck), and because my curve now topped out at four (the Magistrate) and not five ([Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun)).

Out: 3 [Yavimaya Dryad](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/247/yavimaya-dryad)

Since I wanted creatures with tap abilities, I made up a list of all the green creatures in Standard that could tap to generate an effect. The first one that leaped to my attention was [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library). One of the problems with the entire Magus cycle is that while most of them have very powerful effects, the ones that need to tap tend to die before they ever have a chance to lose summoning sickness. [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) would negate this drawback, and it would allow me to “double-dip” the Magus by tapping it, playing a card, using the Elixir to untap it, and then tap it again to draw a third card for my turn.

Another problem with [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) is that pesky “draw a card only if you have seven cards in your hand” clause. If I played a land and an Elf on turn one, [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) on turn two, and [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) on turn three, I would have, at most, five cards in hand.

Turn one (on the draw): Seven cards to start the game. Draw a card (8). Play a [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) (7) and [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) (6).

Turn two: Draw a card (7), play a [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) (6) and a [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) (5).

Turn three: Draw a card (6), play [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) (5).

In order to counteract this, I decided to up the number of [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger)s into the deck. [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger) already was serving the purpose of allowing me to untap creatures with a tap ability (allowing me to get extra effect out of [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid) and [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate)). Not only could it allow me to tap my Magus twice, but it would let me return unneeded lands from the board to my hand. That served a secondary benefit of getting the number of cards in my hand up for the Magus.

Even though [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) is a rare and [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize) is an uncommon, the former (the Magus) usually runs at three for a ticket right now (.33) whereas [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize) is usually 1.5 tickets each! I swap out the [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize)s to make room for three [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) and a [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger), and then put in a [Fa'adiyah Seer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/160/faadiyah-seer) (to draw extra lands, as a tap ability), [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush) (to up my Elf count), and a [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) to take the place of the [Yavimaya Dryad](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/247/yavimaya-dryad)s.

Out: 3 [Yavimaya Dryad](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/247/yavimaya-dryad), 4 [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize)

In: 1 [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger), 3 [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library), 1 [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush), 1 [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest), 1 [Fa'adiyah Seer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/160/faadiyah-seer)

So far, I've been stuck with [Lys Alana Huntmaster](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/67/lys-alana-huntmaster) in my hand every single game, and the tempo of my deck doesn't really allow me to drop it and then play Elves. While in theory it is a great card for this deck, in practice it just hits play at the wrong time. Rather than have it clogging up my hand, I take it out of the deck.

If you want to talk pace of the deck though, let's talk [Llanowar Mentor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/216/llanowar-mentor). With [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/271/thousand-year-elixir) in play, I can discard a card to [Llanowar Mentor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/216/llanowar-mentor) immediately, put a Llanowar Elf token into play, and then immediately tap that Elf for mana! This seems almost too perfect for the deck—while I have to lose cards in hand, I get two Elves in play for one mana (to play the Mentor), one mana (to use it), and one card (to discard). This pushes me even closer to getting turn-four kills off of the Magistrate, because now I have a higher Elf count and more mana (thanks to the [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) tokens) to play threats.

In order to make room for four [Llanowar Mentor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/216/llanowar-mentor), I take out the [Fa'adiyah Seer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/160/faadiyah-seer). Although I had never drawn it yet, it was the card I could most afford to lose to try out the Mentor. Maybe I'd add them back in later—but I'd rather up my chances to draw a Mentor and see if it works well in the deck, rather than draw the Seer, and get a free land a third of the time.

The deck was performing admirably in Standard practice, so I thought to myself: why not take it to Extended? There are a TON of Elves with tap abilities in Extended, whereas the Elf-tapping pool in Standard is a bit more limited. So I pulled up a list of all Elves in Extended, and decided to start by adding [Sylvan Messenger](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/75/sylvan-messenger) (to keep my hand constantly filled with Elves), [Timberwatch Elf](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/76/timberwatch-elf) (Magistrates #5-7) and [Seeker of Skybreak](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/268/seeker-of-skybreak) ([Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger), as an Elf) to the deck. I also wanted to try out [Bloodline Shaman](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/249/bloodline-shaman) (since I am running to many Elves, it will hit a good portion of the time) over [Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library) (which needs seven cards in hand, and is not an Elf).

I also cut [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush) from the deck, since I now have a lot more Elves in the deck ([Magus of the Library](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/134/magus-of-the-library), a [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger) and a [Moonglove Extract](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/222/moonglove-extract) were out), and less need to generate two (relatively) vanilla Elf Warrior tokens to up my Elf count.

Thousand-Year Elves and Overrun Elves both posted a great record in the casual room. Both were mono-green, were centered around attacking, and wanted to pump smaller creatures into much larger monsters. However, I can say without a shadow of a doubt that Thousand-Year Elves is a thousand times more fun to play than Overrun Elves. All right, maybe not a thousand times more fun, but that's a great tag line, isn't it? Regardless, it is much more satisfying to tap [Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect) three times in one turn and then follow that with fifteen +1/+1 from [Immaculate Magistrate](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/234/immaculate-magistrate) pointed in clumps of five thanks to the Elixir and [Scryb Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/227/scryb-ranger), than it was to drop out five Elves by turn four, and then play [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun). Don't ask me why this is—I'm not a certified psychologist, I just know that I myself had a lot more fun with this version of the deck than last week's! You should try this out and make your own tweaks—there are lots of other Elves with tap abilities in Extended (anything going on there, Mr. [Wirewood Channeler](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/79/wirewood-channeler)?), so there is a lot of customization that could be had here.
