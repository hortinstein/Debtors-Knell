10 Decks in 10 Weeks! Each week, I evolve a two-color Standard-legal deck that costs 30 tickets or less. At the beginning of the next week, I keep all of the cards of one of the two colors, and then switch to a different color combination, and begin Building from there! Week 1: G/W - The Two Ladies Week 2: G/R - A Wild Pair Week 3: R/U - Aeon in Flux Week 4: U/B - Grim Outlook Week 5: B/W - Rescue Me Week 6: W/U - A Blink In Time Week 7: W/R - Sheer Pandemonium Week 8: B/R - Madness Week 9: B/G - Profusion Confusion Week 10: G/U - Fungal Behemoth

Hello, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! In this, the next-to-last installment of the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks experiment, we will be taking out the Red from last week's Madness deck, and building around the remaining Black cards from that deck to build around [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion).

The inspiration for my build of Profusion Confusion comes from [Week 2](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042030/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb103) of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks. If you check out my game against Gurgi (Game 7), you'll see that I got worked over by his green-black-red [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion). His deck featured [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion), [Utopia Sprawl](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/159/utopia-sprawl), [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) and [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom), and ended with [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot) for the win. While I wouldn't want to end with [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot) (this is a two-color build, unlike his three-color build), I wanted to base my deck around the engine I saw in his match.

[![Null Profusion](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/plc/89?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion)

In short, here's how the deck would play out. I would use mana acceleration to get up to six mana on the board. Then, I would drop [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion). On the following turn, I would play a ton of lands using [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom), untap them all several times using [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) and [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), and then eventually have a ton of mana left to kill my opponent with.

My kill card of choice? [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit). But how would I make sure that I have both enough green mana in play to play the majority of the spells in my deck early, yet have enough black mana later in the game to win with a [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit)? I decided to splash in a single [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/287/urborg-tomb-of-yawgmoth) into the deck. Urborg is selling for two tickets online, so I wouldn't want to add in a whole playset—but one to two copies seemed fine for a budget build. Plus, they are legendary—I really only want to draw one per game, and only when I'm about to win.

Now, there were a lot of acceleration choices for this deck. I wanted to go with cards that work well with [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) and [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom), since these are the main parts of the engine to get the [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) combo working. This meant that creature-based acceleration ([Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves)) or artifact-based acceleration ([Golgari Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/246/golgari-signet)) were off the menu. This does allow for any spell or ability that would put a land directly into play ([Wood Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/EXO-130/wood-elves), [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth)), or an enchantment that accelerates my mana ([Utopia Sprawl](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/159/utopia-sprawl), [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth)).

In the end, here's what I came up with for the cards to add to the deck:

In: 4 [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), 3 [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), 3 [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom), 4 [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), 2 [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit), 4 [Chromatic Star](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/263/chromatic-star), 4 [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize), 2 [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing)

[Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest): The main engine for the deck. This allows me to untap my lands, which can allow for a [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) a couple of turns early (with the help of [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth)), and allows me to keep untapping my lands once [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) is in play.

[Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim): [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) is a nice, cheap spell that can recur any given card from my graveyard. This is especially important once I get [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) in play, because it allows me to keep getting [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)s.

[![Summer Bloom](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/2x2/160?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom)

[Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom): With [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) in play, this allows me to both draw a card and to play three additional (untapped) lands. One of the dangers of [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) is getting stuck with two lands in your hand, having already played a land. [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom) circumvents that problem and helps boost my mana.

[Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth): The cheapest, most reliable sorcery-speed mana acceleration in Green. [Search for Tomorrow](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/137/search-for-tomorrow) is also a consideration for this deck, and I might end up adding it in to give myself another set of accelerants, plus a turn-one play.

[Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit): The kill card. I don't want too many of these, because my deck is primarily Green, so I won't really be able to use it effectively unless I'm about to kill my opponent.

[Chromatic Star](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/263/chromatic-star): This is in here for two reasons. First, it helps color-fix the deck. Second, it is a fail-safe for [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) against discard. If I play a [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion), and my opponent makes me discard my hand, I lose the game (I'll have no cards in hand, and no way to draw new cards). If I leave a [Chromatic Star](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/263/chromatic-star) on the board with a [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) in play, I'll be able to draw at least one card on my turn.

[Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize): The best card drawing spell in green-black right now. I don't have many early plays, and the ones that I do have can involve me emptying my hand ([Chromatic Star](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/263/chromatic-star), [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth)). This helps me to draw more cards, and drawing more cards in a combo decks means drawing more pieces to help you get your combo going.

[Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing): These were added to complement the [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim)s. Also, if I get down to one card in hand with [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) in play (either due to discard, or by being forced to play [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) with only one other card in hand), this lets me return key spells to my deck (usually [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) or [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)) plus draw two cards (one from the Profusion and one from [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing) itself).

In: 9 [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest), 4 [Golgari Rot Farm](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/151/golgari-rot-farm), 1 [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/287/urborg-tomb-of-yawgmoth)

The [Golgari Rot Farm](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/151/golgari-rot-farm)s are another failsafe in case I end up with one card in hand.

With the other commons and uncommons added, this deck hits right about at the 30 ticket mark. Let's see if all of these rares are entirely necessary!

Game 1: Fiss (Mono-Green Aggro)
 I die to [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) and a Moldvervine-Cloaked [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise) the turn before I'm going to go off with [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion). On my next-to-last turn, I could have played [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) to drop an early [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) and had some mana up, but instead I played [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) (hoping to save [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) for the following turn) and ended up dying to his creatures. 
 Record: 0-1

Game 2: GattoV (R/W/G Slivers)
 He double mulligans and then beats me to death with double [Sidewinder Sliver](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/43/sidewinder-sliver), [Two-Headed Sliver](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/883/two-headed-sliver), and a late Sinew Sliver. I fizzle while going off by drawing a billion lands and only one [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) or [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom). I also am reminded that while [Golgari Rot Farm](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/151/golgari-rot-farm) is good mana fixing, you can't untap it with [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)—the Harvest only untaps basic lands!
 Record: 0-2

No offense to Gatto V, but it is *never* a good sign for your deck when your opponent double mulligans, and then kills you with a trio of 1/1 creatures. Usually I'll wait a few games to tinker with a deck, but Profusion Confusion needed help, and stat.

Golgari Rot Farm with a large red XFirst off—[Golgari Rot Farm](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/151/golgari-rot-farm) had to go. The entire deck is reliant on untapping lands with [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), and the Rot Farm did not work in this paradigm. I could definitely replace it with basic lands without any problems.

Secondly—Haakon and [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/139/smallpox) didn't really have a place in this new deck. [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/139/smallpox) was great when I could discard madness cards, but Profusion Confusion doesn't want to lose cards in hand or lands on the board. Aside from [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/139/smallpox) or reaching three cards in hand with [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) on the board, I didn't have a way to reliably get Haakon into the graveyard. Plus, the deck was either going to win with a massive [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit) to the noggin', or not at all—attacking for three wasn't going to make a difference.

With those ten cards out of the deck (and with the deck down 10 tickets in price, now at a much-more sleek 20 ticket cost), I set about adding more pieces to the combo. I wanted another [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom) and [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), to make sure I didn't get stuck with lands in hand, or without a means to restock my [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest). I also found this to be a great opportunity to add in a playset of [Search for Tomorrow](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/137/search-for-tomorrow). These would compliment the [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), would give me another turn-one play for the deck, and would allow me to have eight cards that facilitate a turn-three [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize). Last, I added in two [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest)s and a [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), plus a third [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit), just in case I needed an early life buffer against a weenie rush.

The deck is definitely working better at this point (and how could it not after a 0-2 start?), but I've had two straight close calls where I almost ran out of gas on the way to comboville—one in which I won, and one in which I lost.

To make the deck more consistent, I decide to add in a pair of [Diabolic Tutor](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/54/diabolic-tutor)s. First, if I don't draw [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion), I can't win. [Diabolic Tutor](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/54/diabolic-tutor) lets me get that [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) before turn four/six (depending again on acceleration), and it also lets me search out the missing combo piece ([Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit), Urborg, [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom)) once I start going off with [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion).

I also grab a piece of the playbook from wayyyyyyyyyy back during *Odyssey* Block Constructed, and add a single [Mirari](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/246/mirari) to the deck. [Mirari](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/246/mirari) is going for 1-2 tickets these days on **Magic Online**, so it's not big strain on the budget to pick up a single copy. [Mirari](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/246/mirari) lets me copy all of my essential spells ([Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), [Search for Tomorrow](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/137/search-for-tomorrow), [Summer Bloom](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/160/summer-bloom), [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize)) at a time when I have mana to spare, which will help me from fizzling. While I don't draw an extra card from [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) off of a copy of a spell from [Mirari](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/246/mirari), this sometimes works in my favor. For instance, I can [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) a card, have the [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) trigger go on the stack, and then copy [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) to put, say, [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) on my deck. On resolution, [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) returns to the top of my deck, I draw it, and then the second card goes on top of my deck—which I'll draw once I play [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)!

At this point, I start looking around for solutions to large hordes of two-power critters. In virtually all of my losses, an Infest or Hideous Laughter-type card would have pulled my fat out of the fire. Unfortunately, there are literally no global "-X/-X" Black spells available in Standard right now! My choices for dealing with hordes of creatures in black-green are: [Crime // Punishment](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/150/crime-punishment), Hail Storm, [Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation) or [Plague Wind](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/102/plague-wind). [Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation) is ideal, but it's so far off the budget radar that it's not even a consideration. [Plague Wind](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/102/plague-wind) is the other type of "too expensive," and [Crime // Punishment](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/150/crime-punishment) is too conditional. My choices are either Hail Storm, or to push my deck in the other direction—make it even more focused on the combo, at the expense of getting disrupted by discard and land destruction. I opt for the second choice—I'd rather go down swinging than sit there with a dead Hail Storm in hand, wishing I had a combo piece.

To speed up the deck, I dredge out those old [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth)s I had considered earlier. I don't have many turn-three plays (excepting [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize) with [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth) or [Search for Tomorrow](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/137/search-for-tomorrow)), so this will let me ramp up to [Null Profusion](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/89/null-profusion) two turns early. I go down to one [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit)—either I win with the one copy after drawing my entire deck, or I lose!
