# Grand Spree

**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** May 22, 2006  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb60>

---

*Editor's Note: This was the article Ben submitted for his audition article three months ago, so if things don't look quite how the current Building on a Budget columns look, that's why.*

Mycosynth Lattice is one of those cards that tugs at my [inner-Johnny](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041313/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/mr11b) heart strings. This *Darksteel* rare changes the rules of the game in multiple ways, and therefore is perfect for building a deck around. Let's examine, quickly, exactly what it is that the Lattice does.

1. It turns all permanents in play into artifacts. This has twofold usefulness – turning all of your creatures, lands and enchantments into artifacts, or turning all of your opponent's creatures, lands and enchantments into artifacts.
2. It fixes the color of mana. With the Lattice in play, you (and your opponent) can use any mana as any other color of mana. This is especially useful for multi-colored spells (think Nephilim) or spells which require a large colored-mana commitment ([Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit)).
3. It turns all non-permanents colorless. Admittedly, the first two abilities of the Lattice are the more interesting ones to build around, although this colorlessness does have usefulness.

[![Shattering Spree](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/gpt/75?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree)

Prior to the release of *Guildpact*, there wasn't really a good way to take advantage of turning your opponent's permanents into artifacts. Sure, you could run a deck with lots of artifact destruction – but why run a six-mana artifact just to turn [Viridian Shaman](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/25/viridian-shaman) into [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)?

Enter [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree).

When I first saw [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree), I immediately knew it was going to be a powerhouse. It is one of those spells that looks fairly innocent on the surface, but has a lot of potential to do evil things. It's cheaper than [Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/rix/114/shatter), plus it has replicate. Unlike spells like Granulate or Purify, [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree) can destroy multiple artifacts with pinpoint accuracy. Plus, thanks to replicate, you can target the same artifact multiple times with one Spree – making the spell effectively uncounterable by Blue mages.

Some people saw [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) and thought, “Man, this makes my affinity cards amazing – plus now I can hit my opponent with a Feedback Bolt for fifteen!” Others looked at the Lattice and mused, “With a Lattice in play, I can play all five colors in my deck and not worry about color requirements to cast my spells!”

I thought to myself, “Self, I wish there was a card that would allow me to take advantage of turning all of my opponent's permanents into artifacts.” Two years later, the good folks at Wizards of the Coast finally gave me my wish – targeted, one-sided mass destruction and chaos.

I set out to build a Lattice/Spree deck to play on ***Magic** Online*. I faced two decisions when laying out the beginnings of the deck:

1. What did I want the deck to do?
2. What colors did I want in the deck?

1) What did I want the deck to do? Answer this question was fairly simple. I wanted to play [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) to the board, and then pummel my opponent's entire side with a [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree). The Lattice helped in two ways towards achieving this goal – it both turned all my opponent's permanents into artifacts so that they could be destroyed by the Spree, and it allowed me to use mana of any color to replicate the Spree multiple times.

2) What colors did I want in the deck? This was a much tougher question, as the Lattice, once in play, allows me to play all five colors of **Magic**. However, the Lattice was sure to be at the top end of my mana curve, so I didn't want to risk building a deck where I'd be stuck with cards in my hand, unable to cast them until I played the Lattice. I saw two ways to go about the deck:

- Red and Green. No matter which colors I used in the deck, Red was the only color that just had to be in the deck, in order to facilitate use of [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree). Red and Green are the two colors which are the absolute best at killing artifacts – between [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree), [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize), [Wear Away](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/250/wear-away), [Viridian Shaman](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/25/viridian-shaman), [Tin Street Hooligan](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/78/tin-street-hooligan), [Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami) (among others), I would have a plethora of artifact-killing options to choose from. This was also the problem with choosing Green for the deck – it didn't add any function to the deck that Red didn't already provide. The problem would be getting the Lattice into play each game, and neither Red nor Green has an easy way to search out the Lattice. I briefly considered combining Green dredge cards with [Trash for Treasure](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/148/trash-for-treasure), but instead I opted for the other plan.
- Red and Blue: Now we're talking. Blue has several cards which interact well with artifacts, without actually destroying said artifacts. Blue can steal artifacts, tutor for artifacts, turn artifacts into creatures – in short, Blue is awfully tricksy when it comes to artifacts.

So it was decided and so it shall be – Red and Blue are the colors for me. Cue the deck list, Maestro!

The deck is built around getting a [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) into play and then blowing up everything your opponent controls using [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree), so those two cards are self-explanatory. Let's take a look at the other choices for this deck.

[Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate): I wanted a way to be able to directly get [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) in the case that I hadn't drawn it early in the game, and [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) was that way. There were a couple of other options I could have used here – such as Ethereal Usher to transmute into Lattice – but [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) is simple, and versatile. I could use it to get an artifact land if I was short on mana, or to get Guardian Idol if I needed an attacker.

[![Thirst for Knowledge](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/msh/79?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge)

[Thirst for Knowledge](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge): [Thirst for Knowledge](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge) is one of the best card-drawing spells ever printed. It works well with the number of artifacts in this deck (sixteen), and allows me more of a chance to see a Lattice or a Spree in any given game. I just had to remember that Lattice turns the cards in my hand colorless, but not into artifacts – there's a difference – so if I had a Lattice in play, I still had to discard an artifact or two non-artifacts if I cast Thirst.

[Neurok Transmuter](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/27/neurok-transmuter): I didn't want to have to rely solely on [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) to make my [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree)s useful, so I added in [Neurok Transmuter](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/27/neurok-transmuter) as a way to force my opponent to play with artifacts.

[Guardian Idol](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/467/guardian-idol): Six mana is a lot, and I didn't want to be stuck with a Lattice in my hand. [Guardian Idol](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/467/guardian-idol) allows me to play my signature spell on the fifth turn, plus it also gives me an offensive threat.

[Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami): A third offensive threat for the deck, and one that is capable of killing artifacts as well.

[Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast): [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) is a very potent direct damage spell, and one that I thought would work well with the Lattice – suddenly, anything I had on the board could be sacrificed to deal a quick five damage.

[March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines): [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines) doesn't play well with the [Great Furnace](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/161/great-furnace)s or [Seat of the Synod](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/176/seat-of-the-synod)s in my deck – with a March out, those artifacts immediately get sent to the graveyard. For the same reason, [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines) works great with [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) – when both hit the table at the same time, all lands in play are destroyed, and you're left with a 6/6 monster.

[Great Furnace](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/161/great-furnace) and [Seat of the Synod](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/176/seat-of-the-synod): These two artifact lands work well with [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) and [Thirst for Knowledge](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge), so I included them in the deck.

[Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks): The Boilerworks produces both colors of mana needed to power this deck. It also serves the purpose of allowing me to bounce a [Seat of the Synod](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/176/seat-of-the-synod) or a [Great Furnace](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/161/great-furnace), so I can discard one of those to a [Thirst for Knowledge](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge).

With deck in hand, I hit the open play room looking for some Extended matches. I was a bit nervous about taking the deck out for a spin – would I get stuck with too many conditional cards in my hand? Would I have Sprees and Kamis without any way to turn my opponent's guys into artifacts? Would I shame the Lattice?

**Game #1: Senhordapedra (Black/Blue Discard)**

[![Guardian Idol](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/jmp/467?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/467/guardian-idol)

My first opponent was Senhordapedra. He countered my third turn [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate), and then proceeded to hit me with a Mind Rot, causing me to lose a second [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) and a [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast). Luckily, I didn't need either [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) as I had started the game with a Lattice in hand, and was trying to use the first [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) to get an artifact land. It didn't matter, as I played [Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks) and [Guardian Idol](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/467/guardian-idol) on the fourth turn, Lattice on his fifth turn, and [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree) for all of his permanents (two Swamps, two Islands, and an Abyssal Specter) on the sixth turn. Senhordapedra conceded on the spot.

*Record: 1-0*

That win put a big smile on my face – my first game, and already the deck was performing as intended. Was this luck, or had I built my deck to have game?

**Game #2: Doc_Gibbs (Madness/Tog/Dredge)**

Doc_Gibbs ran a deck with over 100 cards, and he warned me that his deck was slow. As it turns out, he had a [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/96/life-from-the-loam)/[Psychatog](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/292/psychatog)/Flashback deck that kept getting massive amounts of card advantage through cycling lands. I slowed him down in the early game by using [Neurok Transmuter](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/27/neurok-transmuter) and two [Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami)s to kill a [Wild Mongrel](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/194/wild-mongrel) and a [Psychatog](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/292/psychatog). I dropped a Lattice, and then Doc tapped everything except one Island to cast a second Mongrel and a [Roar of the Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/49/roar-of-the-wurm) from his graveyard. I untapped, played my third [Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami) of the game, destroyed his Island (I did not want to lose to [Circular Logic](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/42/circular-logic)), and then proceeded to blow up his entire board with [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree).

Unfortunately, the Lattice worked against me as Doc_Gibbs was able to use a Swamp to cast [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/70/careful-study), getting back [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/96/life-from-the-loam). He proceeded to recur all his lands with Life, and dropped a [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator), which was enchanted by a [Moldervine Cloak](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/150/moldervine-cloak). I could not find a second Spree, and died in short order.

*Record: 1-1*

When that second game ended, I had multiple [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice)s and [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate)s in my hand. I realized that I really didn't have anything worth tutoring for with [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) in my deck except for Lattice, and made a mental note that it might need to be replaced.

**Match #3: Extractor1 (White Weenie)**

He opens with [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/298/aether-vial), and follows it with [Lantern Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/32/lantern-kami), [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem) and [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem)! Luckily for me, Extractor1 hadn't drawn any other creatures, and so when he cast a [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice), I was able to have my [Neurok Transmuter](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/27/neurok-transmuter) turn itself into an artifact, so that I could sacrifice it to [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) to kill his Kami. I played [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice), and he then played Skyhunter Skirmisher, and equipped it with the [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice). I had no fear, since the Lattice turns all of my spells colorless, and I proceeded to kill every permanent he has with [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree). [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines) on the following turn seals the game.

*Record: 2-1*

**Match #4: CygnusSphere (Red/Green/White Beasts)**

CygnusSphere starts the game with an early [Contested Cliffs](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/282/contested-cliffs), but seems to have trouble getting colored mana. He taps out to play [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum), and I play [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice). He follows with [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), and I finish the game with a sixth turn [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) to the Birds/[March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines) to destroy everything on his side except for the Simulacrum.

*Record: 3-1*

**Match #5: Zachman69 (Black/Red Aggro)**

[![Ronin Houndmaster](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/chk/184?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/184/ronin-houndmaster)

Zachman69 comes out swinging fast with a third turn [Ronin Houndmaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/184/ronin-houndmaster). His aggression ends there, and I lay a fifth turn Lattice at a safe fourteen life. He casts [Distress](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/94/distress), but my hand is double [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree), [Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami), double [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines). He knocks a Spree out of my hand, but I proceed to destroy his entire side of the board the following turn, earning a win via concession.

*Record: 4-1*

At this point, I've gotten a pretty good feel for how my deck works. I definitely like being able to drop a fifth turn [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice), and so I decide to add more artifact acceleration to the deck. This allows me to have another set of cards to pitch to [Thirst for Knowledge](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/79/thirst-for-knowledge), and another set of artifacts to animate with [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines). [Izzet Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/320/izzet-signet) seems like a good candidate for this slot.

I'm also unhappy with [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate). In theory, [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) allows me to have eight [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/241/mycosynth-lattice) in my deck. In practice, I never really needed [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) when I drew it, and I was already having some problems when I'd draw two Lattices a game – anything past the first is going to be a dead draw.

In place of [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate), I add in [Thoughtcast](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/242/thoughtcast). With the Signets added to my deck, I'm now running twenty artifacts, giving me a respectable number to run with [Thoughtcast](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/242/thoughtcast). Plus, [Thoughtcast](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/242/thoughtcast) only costs a single Blue with the Lattice on the table, allowing me to cast Lattice a little more freely without a [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/75/shattering-spree), since I can cheaply dig for a Spree with [Thoughtcast](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/242/thoughtcast).

**Out**: 4 Fabricate,1 [Hearth Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/171/hearth-kami), 1 [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast), 1 [March of the Machines](https://scryfall.com/card/arc/6/march-of-the-machines), 1 Mountain

**Match #10 - Marillon33 (Angels)**

I play out an early [Guardian Idol](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/467/guardian-idol) plus a Transmuter – and he kills them both with one Orim's Thunder. Ouch! That's not very nice now, is it? He plays [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) to power out a very early Silver Seraph, which is too large for the [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) in my hand to kill. I'm forced to cast a second Transmuter, turn his Seraph into an artifact, and Spree it.
