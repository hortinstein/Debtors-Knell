# Followed Footsteps: Transmutastic

**Author:** Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar  
**Date:** December 12, 2005  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm130>

---

It's shame that I've been so busy in my non-**Magic** life of late, because there has been a tremendous outpouring of feedback on the Message Boards, feedback to which I'd like to respond. There isn't enough room in today's article to address all the suggestions brought up over the past two weeks, but I'll try today to cover what I consider to be the key discussion points.

What? Just joining the column now and not sure what the heck I'm talking about? See, I wanted to build a fun, respectable House Dimir budget deck. The readers chose [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) as the card around which I would build [my deck](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm128). [Last week](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm129) I continued the evolution of the deck by settling on a “toolbox” transmute deck that can do lots of weird and unexpected things. Here is the deck as I left off last week:

Some people objected to the fact that it looks like a Ninja deck, or a comes-into-play deck, or a transmute deck, or a Blue/Black aggro-control deck. My response has been twofold. First, it is all of those things *in addition* to being a [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) deck. Second--and this will always be my trump card in this column--it's my deck, and I'm building it the way I want to build it. If you want your own [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) deck to be based on Spirits or Zombies, or to use a different second color, then I say spread your wings and fly, baby. These series have never meant to supply an off-the-shelf decklist so much as they have been to provoke you into wanting to build your own decks. At every turn I'm making choices that you might handle differently, and that's great. Post your decklists on the Message Boards and let everyone enjoy your creativity.

I'll come back to this point once more today, but for now let's see how version 1.4 plays in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*...

*Game 26: Green/White Selesnya*

[![Emperor Crocodile](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ema/165?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/165/emperor-crocodile)

His deck was different from my [Selesnya Conclave deck](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm126), but he was packing [Emperor Crocodile](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/165/emperor-crocodile), which made me happy. Anyway, he started off with [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) and [Centaur Safeguard](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/244/centaur-safeguard) while I had [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard) to ninjutsu into [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours), then I played [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats). My Rats and Ninja held off his critters, even after he enchanted the Safeguard with [Fists of Ironwood](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/139/fists-of-ironwood) and got out [Selesnya Evangel](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/220/selesnya-evangel). As I was just about to transmute for [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee), I instead drew it and killed his Evangel. A second Ninja let me replay the Banshee, which was good because he played [Veteran Armorer](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/34/veteran-armorer). [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil) killed his [Siege Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/155/siege-wurm), then we stared at each for a while. Things got a little desperate once he played Crown of Convergence with me at eight life, but I luckily drew into [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) and [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). Robber tokens kept his forces at bay while I rebuilt my life, then I drew Honden of Seeing Winds and used my House Guard to fetch [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach). I quickly took control of the game after that, killing his main threats, ignoring his tokens, and cruising in for the win.

*Game 27: Black/Green Golgari*

Might as well make my way through the other guilds, right? He had a quick [Shambling Shell](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/57/shambling-shell) that I blocked with [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber). I used [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp) to kill his [Golgari Brownscale](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/166/golgari-brownscale), then [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee) killed his [Golgari Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/194/golgari-guildmage). He tried [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu), but I had [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) on my Banshee to send it to the graveyard. He recycled his Brownscale to gain some life, but I put [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on my Banshee to ensure that I would kill whatever else hit the table (and I could always target the Brownscale in a pinch). I attacked twice more with flying Banshee and Banshee tokens, then [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger) found [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) to win me the game.

*Game 28: Blue/Black Dimir [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps)*

Mindleech Mass

A mirror match! Well, sort of. His deck used [Clutch of the Undercity](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/197/clutch-of-the-undercity), along with a full compliment of Dimir Doppelgangers, [Dimir Cutpurse](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse)s, Dream Leashes, and Mindleech Masses... so maybe not so mirror-ful after all. Anyway, I'm not entirely sure how to summarize this game. Suffice it to say, he bounced and stole my early threats (which included a [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) and a [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)d [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber)), then started to get his rare critters going. I got [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours) and [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee) into play, putting him into single digits of life while I still hovered around twenty. He got [Dimir Cutpurse](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse). I put [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on it. He put [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on it, too. I got two tokens then killed it with [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp). He got Mindleech Mass, I put [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on it and managed to get both Honden into play. His hand emptied, mine didn't, and I won easily with only twenty cards left in my library.

*Game 29: Blue/Black Dimir Doppleganger*

When my opponent transmuted a [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth) on the third turn for another, I knew something funny was going on. I figured it must be a reanimator deck of some kind. I attacked with a [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours) and a [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator) for a while, putting [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach) into play. I was aware that the Honden might be helping his cause, but I hoped to make the discard eventually hurt. When he tried a Dimir Doppelganger and activated it to remove a [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth), I responded with [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp). Plink, plink, plink went my attack. A second Doppleganger turned into a second [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth), but I drew and played [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) to kill it. That was pretty much game, as my discard finally put him into topdeck mode while I drew an extra card per turn. Just in case, I put [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on my Ninja, triggering a concession from my opponent.

*Game 30: Five-Color Hondens*

[![Nullmage Shepherd](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/khc/70?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/70/nullmage-shepherd)

My opponent had a [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) and [Civic Wayfinder](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/5/civic-wayfinder) while I got a [Dimir Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/221/dimir-signet) and third-turn [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours). I killed his Wayfinder with [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil), then [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/50/vinelasher-kudzu) with [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp), allowing my Ninja to draw me plenty of cards. He played [Honden of Seeing Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/54/honden-of-seeing-winds), and I transmuted a [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard) into [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach). Then I transmuted another [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil) for my own [Honden of Seeing Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/54/honden-of-seeing-winds), destroying his. [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) on my [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours) gave me three Ninja tokens. My opponent tapped out for [Honden of Life's Web](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/172/honden-of-lifes-web) and [Nullmage Shepherd](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/70/nullmage-shepherd) (with only two creatures on the table). I smiled, played [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) to get my own [Nullmage Shepherd](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/70/nullmage-shepherd), then [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) to kill his Shepherd, then I destroyed his Honden with my (new) Shepherd. On the next turn I tapped creatures to kill his [Genju of the Cedars](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/13/genju-of-the-cedars), attacked with my Ninja tokens, and my opponent conceded.

Now that's more like it. My games are going long, which means that [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is becoming a much bigger part of my victories. I'm drawing enough cards to reliably find the Footsteps, and I'm able to transmute [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil) for it thanks to the rest of my early removal. The transmuting has been fantastic, and along with the [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)s I find myself with a deck that's really difficult to predict. In short: Me likey. It's worth noting, though, that I've consistently been able to beat slower decks while having trouble with faster decks. It's too bad that I couldn't slip a Boros beatdown deck in there to test my mettle (and give me a full set of guild match-ups).

##### Feeling Transmutastic

I could probably stop now, shoring up the deck's weakness to aggressive decks in the sideboard. I've seen several suggestions on the Message Boards that I've liked, though, so I'll make a few minor tweaks to my deck and really embrace the toolbox feel.

*OUT*: 1 Island

*IN*: 1 [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)

[![Fellwar Stone](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/soc/347?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)

A few folks suggested that perhaps I could drop two [Dimir Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/221/dimir-signet)s for two [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)s, allowing me to use activated abilities of creatures I copy via [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) and [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). Recall from my first article that I bemoaned the loss of [Star Compass](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/229/star-compass) without realizing that [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) was sitting around in *9th Edition*. As you can imagine, I love the idea of [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone). The reason I'm only adding one is because I also love the fact that my deck has run into relatively few mana issues through thirty games. Usually a two-color deck without good multicolor lands is in trouble, yet thanks to the Signets I can almost always cast my [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator)s, my {B}{B} cards like [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee), [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal), and [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber), and my {U}{U} [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). If anything, my deck has a slight tendency towards mana-flooding, which is the reason I feel comfortable dropping a land for a non-land mana source. If worse comes to worse I can transmute an Infiltrator for Fellwar Stone if the off-color mana is going to be relevant.

*OUT*: 1 [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)

*IN*: 1 [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard)

I love [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) and have never been sad to draw it, so this change hurts. Still, I see the wisdom of dropping the second copy to maximize my transmute options. The deck has a whopping ten possible targets for [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard)'s ability, plus it's often a solid creature on its own. In order to reliably be able to transmute on Turn 3 and then play my chosen card on Turn 4, I think four House Guards make sense. Even still, I'll miss my second [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone).

Speaking of mana: If you were wondering about the lack of three-mana cards in my deck, the House Guards, Infiltrators, and [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil)s effectively act as a third-turn play. I think most people knew this, but the question came up a few times on the Boards and over e-mail.

*OUT*: 1 [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats)

[![Nezumi Graverobber](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/cm2/71?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/71/nezumi-graverobber-nighteyes-the-desecrator)

It's funny that when I had four [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats) in the deck, people decried my lack of creativity. When I dropped two of them for [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach) and [Honden of Seeing Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/54/honden-of-seeing-winds), people argued that I should add more Rats into my deck. I tend to think of [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats) as the most expendable card still in my deck, due in large part to [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach). I'm keeping one Rats around because I've frequently found the situation where my opponent is clinging to one card in hand and I'm about to make an alpha strike, so it does end up being a fairly good transmute target (and it's always been a good target for [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours)).

*IN*: 1 [Nezumi Graverobber](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/71/nezumi-graverobber-nighteyes-the-desecrator)

Speaking of good transmute targets, I have been resisting this addition since my first idea of using [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) with comes-into-play creatures. Nezumi Graverobber was obviously a centerpiece of [my Ratimation deck](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm103), and I worried that adding him to this deck pushed the deck into more of a reanimator feel than a cloning feel. What I have come to realize is that the Graverobber's graveyard-stripping ability is often as important as its Nighteyes the Desecrator ability thanks to dredge, soulshift, and reanimator decks seemingly everywhere in the Casual Decks room. This lone copy becomes an important target for [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator)'s transmute ability against graveyard-focused decks and is never a wasted draw against other decks. The reanimating ability is a bonus, something my deck can certainly appreciate but one I don't expect to use overly much.

When I say “final version,” I mean that I'm not intending to work on the deck any longer. Now that I'm done, this deck more than any other I've made seems to beg the question...

##### Did I Succeed?

So, let's remember my goals for this deck: My first goal was to make a fun deck. Is this deck fun? This is a sort of a trick question, since what's fun to one player is like gouging out one's own eyes for another. For me, though, I can say unequivocally that this deck is fun to play. It's strategically complex, rewarding me for outthinking my opponent. It's versatile, allowing me to never truly lose hope in a game. It does wacky things, frequently eliciting “oohs” and “ahhs” from spectators. It is, in fact, one of the more fun decks I've made in this column.

[![Followed Footsteps](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/cn2/111?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps)

My second goal was to make a respectable deck. As several people have noted, the deck wins a good number of games. My record in playing version 1.5 so far is 17-7 in the Casual Decks room. The deck can still be too slow against super aggressive decks, and I can easily lose to [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte). Still, it has beaten plenty of non-budget decks and has very few drastic losses. Here too I would say I met my goal.

My third goal, of course, was to make a budget deck. The deck uses five “bargain bin” rares, several decent uncommons (many of which are single copies), and a pile of commons. It falls well below the “30 tix” limit that Nate Heiss once employed in the predecessor to this column. Clearly this is a budget deck.

My fourth goal was to make a House Dimir deck. Although people grumbled that my initial decklist wasn't very “Dimir,” I think those voices have quieted. The deck is Black/Blue to the core, using no less than eleven cards with Dimir's signature mechanic of transmute. It's sneaky and it's tricky and it's underhanded. No, it doesn't mill away opponent's libraries, but [as Aaron pointed out](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/af92), that's just one path to victory for House Dimir. Fourteen of the cards even have “Dimir” in their card name, for crying out loud. Yes, I would say the deck met this goal as well.

The fifth goal was to build a deck around [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps). Here, I think, lies the biggest uncertainty. On the upside, I kept four copies of [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) in the deck throughout this experiment, and Footsteps in no way contradicts what the deck is trying to do. In fact, if you look through my game logs you'll see that, in the longer games, [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is often a key to my victories. Certainly this is a deck that can take full advantage of Footsteps without batting an eye, thanks mostly to a host of juicy comes-into-play creatures.

That said, several folks have pointed out that [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) isn't exactly the central card of the deck. Imagine, for example, dropping three copies of [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) for a fourth [Brainspoil](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/78/brainspoil), one Dream Leash, and one Evacuation. The deck pretty much performs the same way, with [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) still a viable option as a path to victory, yet opening the door for other pieces to the deck's “toolbox.” I don't think I would enjoy such a deck as much as the one I have, simply because I've found [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) to be a) fragile enough to warrant multiple copies, and b) fun as all get-out. Still, I think it underscores the argument that perhaps [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is not the centerpiece of my deck. Indeed, I'm not sure the deck I've made *has* a true centerpiece.

It's possible that [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) is not a natural fit for House Dimir, and belongs instead in a Blue/White or Blue/Green deck. It's also possible that I simply went about the whole centerpiece thing the wrong way by not focusing on either immobilizing my opponent's creatures via [Clinging Darkness](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/80/clinging-darkness) and [Dehydration](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/78/dehydration), or using big beefy Footsteps targets like [Air Elemental](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/44/air-elemental). Or maybe five goals is too many for one deck evolution. Whatever the case, this fifth goal is the one in which I have serious doubts about my own success.

I hope, though, that those last two paragraphs don't detract from the very fun, respectable, budget, House Dimir deck I've built. The deck is terrific, and I will argue that four [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) belong in the deck until I'm blue (and Black) in the face. To my mind, this experiment was definitely a success, both in its final product and as a learning experience.

It's also a deck that seriously needs a name. Speak up on the Message Boards with suggestions, and in my first article of the New Year I'll officially dub the deck. Remember that I like my deck names to be both catchy and to reflect the overall mechanics/strategy of the deck. Look back at past deck names to get an idea of what tends to make me smile.

##### Speculative Sideboard Time!

One of the things about a toolbox deck is that their sideboards look... weird. The idea is usually to add all of the tutor-able options that are good in some match-ups but less than ideal in others. The result is a lot of one-ofs and two-ofs in the sideboard, just as in the maindeck. I haven't had time to try out this deck in matches that use sideboards, so take my musing here with a large grain of salt. The sheer number of options available for a toolbox sideboard is such that you should really do some testing to figure out what cards you do and don't need in your toolbox. With those caveats in place, if I were piloting this deck into a Friday Night **Magic** or eight-person online tourney, I might start with a sideboard along these lines:

*Sideboard*: 2 Shred Memory

Shred Memory

One option against Golgari and reanimator decks is to add more [Nezumi Graverobber](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/71/nezumi-graverobber-nighteyes-the-desecrator)s. I'm less enamored with this idea than Shred Memory, a card much more efficient than Graverobber at killing graveyards and that can transmute for other options (like [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp)) in my deck when needed. Honestly, I wish I could use three in the sideboard but I just don't have the room.

*Sideboard*: 2 [Mnemonic Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/59/mnemonic-nexus)

I find that if I'm facing off against a dedicated milling deck that uses cards like Glimpse the Unthinkable, then I can only win the race about half of the time. If the opposing deck is creatureless (as opposed to using things like Soratami Mindsweeper and Vedalken Entrancer), then I usually lose the race. At least in theory, [Mnemonic Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/59/mnemonic-nexus) helps me restock my library and win the game with damage before my library is depleted. Like Shred Memory, I wish I had more than two copies because there is a real danger that both of these copies will get milled into my graveyard before I can ever use them.

*Sideboard*: 1 [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)

To be honest, I would usually rather have a 2/2 flier than a 2/1 first striker. That said, there are some games in which [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) is simply a better creature than [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee), and in those match-ups I imagine that the Banshee would come out for her scimitar-wielding cousin.

*Sideboard*: 1 [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee)

...Whereas against Monoblack or mostly-Black decks, [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee) would come in for [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal).

*Sideboard*: 1 [Manriki-Gusari](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari)

[![Manriki-Gusari](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/sok/156?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari)

As a budget player, I pretty much loath [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte). I've been decently successful up until this point by killing all of my opponent's creatures before they can become equipped with the Jitte. That's a pretty dicey strategy, though, and I would rather a true anti-Jitte answer was sitting in my deck waiting to be sought out. Of course, [Manriki-Gusari](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari) also helps against any deck that relies heavily on [Sunforger](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/473/sunforger), [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer), [Tatsumasa, the Dragon's Fang](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/270/tatsumasa-the-dragons-fang) or any other pieces of equipment.

*Sideboard*: 1 [Cruel Edict](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/48/cruel-edict)

I'm mostly thinking [Kodama of the North Tree](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/222/kodama-of-the-north-tree) here, but I suppose there are other cards like [Kuro, Pitlord](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/44/kuro-pitlord) or somesuch that would be immune to most of my other removal (aside from [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), the Legend Slayer).

*Sideboard*: 1 [Eradicate](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/65/eradicate)

This is another tool against reanimator decks or against decks with really annoying creatures like [Loxodon Hierarch](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/15/loxodon-hierarch). I appreciate that [Eternal Witness](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/286/eternal-witness) is no longer in Standard, but I still take comfort in this fellow sitting in my sideboard.

*Sideboard*: 1 [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter)

It's nice that both [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard) and [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator) survive [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter). The problem is that most of my other creatures don't, which is why I don't use Laughter maindeck. That said, my deck is vulnerable to quick creature rushes. Against any Selesnya Conclave deck, for example, Hideous Laughter is going to hurt my opponent a lot more than it hurts me.

*Sideboard*: 1 Shifting Borders

Shifting Borders

I don't relish the idea of giving someone two of my land, even if it does make [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) more reliable. The reason I think it might be necessary, though, is because of cards like [Quicksand](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/245/quicksand), [Svogthos, the Restless Tomb](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/270/svogthos-the-restless-tomb), [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/324/sunhome-fortress-of-the-legion), [Vitu-Ghazi, the City-Tree](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/272/vitu-ghazi-the-city-tree), [Mikokoro, Center of the Sea](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/217/mikokoro-center-of-the-sea), [Miren, the Moaning Well](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/163/miren-the-moaning-well), or any other opposing legendary lands. I've especially seen where this could be wonderful versus Svogthos and Miren. It sounds like merely a “neat trick,” but if timed correctly I think that Shifting Borders can be a game-winner.

*Sideboard*: 2 [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach)

*Sideboard*: 2 [Honden of Seeing Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/54/honden-of-seeing-winds)

A “transformative sideboard” is one that allows a deck to change its core strategy and/or identity between games within a single match. Against some decks, chunks of my deck are going to be nearly useless. I'm thinking for example, of [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp) versus creatureless decks or [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) in a deck stuffed full of creature removal. In these match-ups, I think it makes sense in turning the deck into a Black/Blue Honden toolbox deck. It's a theory that I would need to test, but I like the idea of a transformative sideboard as yet another one of the deck's tricks. It's so House Dimir.

##### Adding Money To The Deck

One of the cool things about this deck is that I rarely experience myself as playing a budget deck. That is, the deck performs well without sacrificing a great deal of power or tricks for its low monetary cost. Even still, there are a few rare cards that could potentially find their way into the deck. If you have access to any of the cards below and like the rough skeleton of a deck like mine, I would highly recommend trying them.

*[Watery Grave](https://scryfall.com/card/eoe/261/watery-grave) and [Underground River](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/321/underground-river)*

I said the same thing with Empire Maker, and I made my point explicitly in [August](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042357/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm115). I imagine that I'll begin to repeat myself quite a bit in these last installments of deck evolutions. The most important rares in Standard right now are the rare dual-colored lands. *Ravnica's* are slightly better than those in *9th Edition*, but both are awesome. If you have one, throw it in. If you have eight, throw them all in. Doing so would also allow you to use [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) more liberally than [Dimir Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/221/dimir-signet), freeing up at least one spot for another tutor-target.

*[Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte)*

Again, I daresay you've heard this before. The so-called “Fork of Doom” is indeed a harbinger of doom versus many decks (including this one). If you are using creatures to win the game and have access to [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte), the only reason not to use it is because your friends will hate you. Besides, it's a transmute target and works so well on either [Dimir Infiltrator](https://scryfall.com/card/pca/86/dimir-infiltrator) or [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard).

*[Dimir Cutpurse](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse)*

[![Dimir Cutpurse](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ddh/49?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse)

I said earlier that the deck didn't really need a third-turn play because it can (and will) often transmute during the third turn. Here, though, is a card worthy of Turn 3. [Dimir Cutpurse](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse) is a superb [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) target and its discard/draw ability both helps protect the Footsteps and ensures you'll draw one. Just remember that adding this before you add something like [Watery Grave](https://scryfall.com/card/eoe/261/watery-grave) is flirting with potential mana problems.

*[Cranial Extraction](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/105/cranial-extraction)*

Nothing says “mind invasion” quite like [Cranial Extraction](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/105/cranial-extraction). In this deck, the Extraction helps get rid of cards that would kill [Followed Footsteps](https://scryfall.com/card/cn2/111/followed-footsteps) and is a darned good target for [Dimir House Guard](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/73/dimir-house-guard)'s transmute ability. Like the Jitte, [Cranial Extraction](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/105/cranial-extraction) seems to have that “buzz kill” quality with friends, but if you're making a more tournament-focused deck I don't see why this card would stay out of either your maindeck or sideboard.

*Spiffy One-Ofs*

Speaking of transmute targets, there are a number of cards that I think could add to or supplement the deck's existing toolbox. [Nezumi Shortfang](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/131/nezumi-shortfang-stabwhisker-the-odious) is likely going to be better than [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats). Dream Leash can dramatically swing a game, as can [Meloku the Clouded Mirror](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/399/meloku-the-clouded-mirror). I'm not entirely sure that [Sickening Shoal](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/82/sickening-shoal) is worth a shot, but I do like that its converted mana cost is two and could replace a [Last Gasp](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/86/last-gasp) for added flexibility. There are certainly other two-, four- and five-mana rares worth exploring, but this list can get you started.
