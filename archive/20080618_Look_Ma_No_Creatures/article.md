# Look Ma, No Creatures!

**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** June 18, 2008  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb169>

---

Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget, the column dedicated to building decks that cost 30 tickets or less using **Magic Online** prices! Last week, I held a vote to see which Giant deck you wanted me to build, but I said that this week would be an interlude into anti-*Legions* land, while we tallied the votes using Gleemax-o-matic 3000. And what is an anti-*Legions* deck? Well, since *Legions* was the set with 100% creatures, this deck will have no creatures at all!

Back in the day, there was a deck called [Prison](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041509/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/44). Prison was a white-green deck that locked people down by playing Winter Orb, [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator), and Armageddon to deny the opponent mana, while using [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing) to keep the Prison player from being decked. This, in fact, was the deck I brought to the first Pro Tour–Chicago in 1997. And while Winter Orb and Armageddon don't exist anymore, we do have [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) as an approximation of Armageddon.

So assuming that the deck is green-red (for [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing) and [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust)), what cards would you want in a creatureless deck? This deck was made pre-*Shadowmoor* (the last one in this column, I swear!), so the available card pool is *Tenth Edition*, *Coldsnap*, *Time Spiral*, *Planar Chaos*, *Future Sight*, *Lorwyn*, and *Shadowmoor*.

To start, I went through the list of all noncreatures in red, green, lands, and artifacts, and threw all of the cards that looked interesting into one big deck list. This is just like someone taking their collection of cards, pulling out all the cards they are interested in, and whittling the list down to a finely tuned 60-card pile. The next thing I did was look up the ticket cost of these cards—this *is* Building on a Budget, and there's no sense considering cards that would completely wreck the budget.

The first three cuts were [Highland Weald](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/147/highland-weald), [Molten Disaster](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/117/molten-disaster), and [Coldsteel Heart](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/301/coldsteel-heart), for budgetary reason. When you're working with a 30 ticket budget, there's no reason to spend a third of that budget on two-mana-cost artifacts, when much more affordable options ([Mind Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/352/mind-stone), [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens)) are present. The same goes for [Highland Weald](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/147/highland-weald), and to a degree [Molten Disaster](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/117/molten-disaster); there are lots of red spells that can wipe the board, and it's not essential that [Molten Disaster](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/117/molten-disaster) be in this deck.

Out: 4 [Coldsteel Heart](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/301/coldsteel-heart), 4 [Highland Weald](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/147/highland-weald), 4 [Molten Disaster](https://scryfall.com/card/c13/117/molten-disaster)

On the second runthrough, I realized that [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze) would be a much cheaper alternative to [Disintegrate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/58/disintegrate) (even though [Disintegrate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/58/disintegrate) is strictly better), and I certainly didn't want them as a four-of in a deck designed to blow up all the lands. I also cut one [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) (since drawing more than one a game is too many), a couple of the Vivid lands each (since I need my lands to generally come into play untapped, so I don't fall behind on the curve), and a [Crucible of Worlds](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/303/crucible-of-worlds).

Out: 2 [Vivid Crag](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/276/vivid-crag), 2 [Vivid Grove](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/445/vivid-grove), 4 [Disintegrate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/58/disintegrate), 1 [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust), 1 [Crucible of Worlds](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/303/crucible-of-worlds)

On the third go-round, I took certain cards to more manageable levels. Since there was a lot of redundancy in direct damage, I took out singletons of [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm) and [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast), plus two of the Beacons of Destruction and all of the [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt)s. I also yanked three of the [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault)s—the subtheme of "[Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust), [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault), and [Crucible of Worlds](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/303/crucible-of-worlds)" was very costly, and I didn't think I wanted the deck to go in that direction. I wanted it to be more of a board-control deck! I also killed a [Creeping Mold](https://scryfall.com/card/kld/150/creeping-mold) and a [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing), because technically two Blessings is all you need to never be decked, assuming your opponent doesn't have graveyard removal, or key counterspells.

I also added in the basic lands for this deck. This deck was certainly going to be a land-heavy deck, with additional mana coming from artifacts.

Out: 1 [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm), 1 [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast), 1 [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing), 2 [Beacon of Destruction](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/35/beacon-of-destruction), 4 [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt), 1 [Creeping Mold](https://scryfall.com/card/kld/150/creeping-mold), 3 [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault)

At this point, I decided to completely kill the "Crucible with [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust)" plan. I needed to streamline the deck to be more effective, and that sort of cute trick wasn't going to cut it. I also took myself down to a single [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze)—if I was to truly use [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) to lock down the game, I wouldn't be able to [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze) effectively —but it couldn't hurt to try one in there, just because A) I have so many lands, and B) I'm not going to be able to play [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) every single game. I take out two [Creeping Mold](https://scryfall.com/card/kld/150/creeping-mold)s, as it was there just as a utility spell (and I want to keep one in just to see how it does, when I draw it), plus a [Beacon of Destruction](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/35/beacon-of-destruction), a [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm), a [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast), and an [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade). Last, I go down to six two-drop artifacts by killing double [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens), finish off Chandra as a three-of, and go down to the two [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing)s that I mentioned above. Below is the deck I started playtesting!

In this deck, the goal is to build up mana (both lands and artifacts) and control the board via direct damage. [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade)s are a recurring source of damage, as is Chandra, and both are key, because the goal is to blow up the world with [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust), leaving me with multiple ways to kill/neutralize creatures ([Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade), [Chandra Nalaar](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/34/chandra-nalaar), [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator)) and my opponents hopeless while I slowly take away their life. [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing) lets me recur threats, and keep myself from being decked in the case that the game comes down to a sheer lockdown with no point of return for either me or my opponent. How did the deck perform? Let's see!

**Game 1: Goetia (Mono-Red Burn)**

Goetia aims double [Tarfire](https://scryfall.com/card/ddt/55/tarfire) and [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt) at my head, and follows it with [Browbeat](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/123/browbeat) (I eat the 5 life rather than let Goetia draw three cards), leaving me at 8. I kill a Soulbright Flamekin with [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), suspend [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade), and put [Chandra Nalaar](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/34/chandra-nalaar) on the board. Goetia concedes when I get Chandra up to nine counters.

Record: 1-0

**Game 2: Lordpenguin4 (Black-Red Land Destruction)**

[![Chandra Nalaar](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/jvc/34?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/34/chandra-nalaar)

I get a [Mind Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/352/mind-stone) and a [Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens) early, which is good, because Lordpenguin4 leads with triple(!) Rain of Tears and follows up with Shivan Wumpus. I get [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator) and lock down his creature, and LordPenguin4 plays [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust), leaving his board empty, and me with two artifact mana, and the Icy. I get more lands and start to play [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize) to build my hand. Eventually, he gets two lands in play, and I play [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust). This leaves him with a perpetual eight cards in hand, and I am able to suspend two [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade)s, which slowly chip away at his life total. He starts building up lands again, and I play [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing) twice, putting [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) back into my library and drawing a copy again. After a long, twenty-six-turn game, I kill him with seven [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade)s, an [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), and a [Beacon of Destruction](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/35/beacon-of-destruction).

Record: 2-0

**Game 3: mykbuckley (White-Green Aggro)**

Mykbuckley leads with [Safehold Elite](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/220/safehold-elite), and then [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) and Wilt-Leaf Liege. I kill the Liege with Chandra (which, in turn, is killed by [Safehold Elite](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/220/safehold-elite)), and then blow up his world with [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm). He gets Medicine Runner to put his [Safehold Elite](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/220/safehold-elite) back to full strength, and then puts Gaddock Teeg down. I kill Teeg with [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) and then drop another Chandra, taking down the Medeicine Runner. He plays [Spectral Procession](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/33/spectral-procession), and I play [Gaea's Blessing](https://scryfall.com/card/dom/161/gaeas-blessing), putting [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm), my first Chandra, and [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) back, drawing another Clasm to blow up the board. He drops Old Ghastbark, and I put [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator) down, and finally finish off his Elite. Chandra dies keeping various smaller Elves out of play, and I drop a third Chandra, and then play [Mind Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/352/mind-stone) and [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) to lock down the board with a fully charged Chandra. He concedes.

Record: 3-0

At this point, I've drawn [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault) in two games, and it's been pretty bad in both of them. That's because this deck wants to hold (and/or play) every land it gets, either to ramp up to [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust) or to have mana available *after* [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust). Either way, [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault) needed to go.

I also took a lot of early damage from the aggressive decks, and I don't want cards in the deck that will hurt me further while sweeping the board. I take out a pair of [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast)s for the much more life-friendly [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm). If I were playing with *Shadowmoor*, I would find a way to include four copies of [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout), as it is very suited for this deck.

Out: [Seismic Assault](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/146/seismic-assault), 2 [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast)
 In: [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade), 2 [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm)

**Game 4: MuseOfMors (Mono-Black Fling)**

MuseOfMors drops [Nantuko Husk](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/109/nantuko-husk), which I kill with [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm). He then drops double Sootstoke Kindler, which I kill with a second [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm). His fourth creature of the game (in as many turns) is a Waning Wurm. I have the perfect answer in [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator), but even though I tap down his 7/6 Wurm, I eat a 7-point Rite of Consumption during his turn. I then drop a second Icy and a [Chandra Nalaar](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/34/chandra-nalaar), and start locking down the board. MuseOfMors drops a pair of [Whispersilk Cloak](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/225/whispersilk-cloak)s, but I draw and immediately play [Boom // Bust](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/156/boom-bust), locking down the entire opposing board. I have more lands in hand, and start drawing artifact mana, all the while activating Chandra every turn (including her 10-point special). Eventually, after reaching eight cards in hand, MuseofMors concedes.

Record: 4-0

**Game 5: chessmack (Mono-Red Burn)**

Chessmack burns me down quickly with Shard Volley, [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock), double [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast), and [Keldon Marauders](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/136/keldon-marauders). I get down Chandra to keep his board clear and use an [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade) to kill an Emberwilde Augur. I then start chipping away at him little by little with Chandra and [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade), with his own [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast)s taking him to 14 to start out. I drop double [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) on his head and win the game at 4 life.

Record: 5-0

**Game 6: Rhiox (Red-Green Warriors)**

Rhiox drops a [Bramblewood Paragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon), and I kill it with [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate). He drops a second, and that one eats a [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm). He then drops [Brighthearth Banneret](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/88/brighthearth-banneret) and [Evolution Charm](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/202/evolution-charm), bringing back one of the Paragons. I make them both eat consecutive [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade)s, which is a fine meal of red hot magma! I then play Chandra and two [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator)s, tap down both his sources of red mana for several turns, and eventually kill him thanks to double [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade) plus Chandra's ultimate ability.

Record: 6-0

Boom/Bust

**Game 7: Prana ([Stuffy Doll](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/875/stuffy-doll))**

Prana opens with [Ancestral Vision](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/144/ancestral-vision)s on the first turn, and I build up a lot of artifact mana. Prana plays [Stuffy Doll](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/875/stuffy-doll) and then plays [Cryptic Command](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/48/cryptic-command) to counter an [Arc Blade](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/152/arc-blade). Overall, a decent record and a fun deck to play. To a degree, land destruction is frowned upon in the casual room, but I think there's a difference between "Turn one [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) / turn two [Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain) / turn three Avalance Riders / turn four [Plow Under](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/272/plow-under)" and "Play a control deck until it's time to just blow up all the lands at once." The first is just a mana denial strategy. The second encourages people to play better **Magic**. Too many people get in the habit of just blindly laying down their lands the second they draw those lands, and that's bad for multiple reason: the fewer cards you have in hand, the less chance you have of bluffing your opponent, the more open you are to losing key spells late-game to discard, and (in this case), the less chance you have of recovering if your opponent suddenly decides to blow up the world.
