When *Time Spiral* was first released, there was a card that I immediately wanted to play with. This card is not the most flashy of cards, or the most desirable (good for the budget!), but it works well with a card from **Magic's** past. That card?

[![Vesuva](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/tsr/289?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva)

[Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva).

Its partner?

[![Cloudpost](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mrd/280?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)

[Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost).

Back in the day, the French developed a [Tooth and Nail](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/327/tooth-and-nail) deck revolving around [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s. They would use [Sylvan Scrying](https://scryfall.com/card/bfz/192/sylvan-scrying) plus [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow) to get multiple [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s into play, and use them to fuel an entwined [Tooth and Nail](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/327/tooth-and-nail). They named this deck Twelve-Post, although they could only actually have 4 [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) out at a time.

Enough of the history lesson! [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva) allows you to copy any land, and that includes [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost). This means that if you play with four [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s and four [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva)s, you have access to a potential *eight* [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)! What does this mean for your mana?

One [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 1 Mana
 Two [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 4 Mana
 Three [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 9 Mana
 Four [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 16 Mana
 Five [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 25 Mana
 Six [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 36 Mana
 Seven [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 49 Mana
 Eight [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = 64 Mana
 Nine [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) = Either you're playing the mirror match, or you're a dirty cheater!

Compare this to the Urzatron, and you'll see that a focus on the locus comes out way ahead! If you're playing [Urza's Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1051/urzas-mine), [Urza's Power Plant](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1052/urzas-power-plant), and [Urza's Tower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1053/urzas-tower), you need to get three different lands out at once, and then they produce seven mana. You only need one [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) to get things going, since any combination of [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s and [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva)s past the first will act identically to one another. Three [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s equals nine mana - outpacing Urza lands by two mana.

Now, let's say you build a deck around [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) and [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva). What do you do with such an engine? Let's take this step by step.

##### Step One: Searching for Land

In order to make sure you get [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s and [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva), you need ways to search for these lands. Technically Eight-Post could start as any color deck, as [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost) produces colorless mana. Green has the best cards to search for non-basic lands, and the original duo of [Sylvan Scrying](https://scryfall.com/card/bfz/192/sylvan-scrying) and [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow) seem fine for use as ways to search for [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost).

##### Step Two: Searching for Land, Part Two

One old tried-and-true trick for setting up your draws is to run [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) in a deck that uses multiple shuffle effects. I've already got eight in the deck. [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) is a natural fit in this deck. Without [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), this deck can get stuck not having enough colored mana sources. With [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), the deck can play turn-one [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost), turn-two Forest, and then have access to two colored mana the rest of the game while dropping [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s/[Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva)s every turn.

##### Step Three: Finding a Use for 64 mana (a.k.a., Profit!)

You've got eight [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s in play. Now what? The skies are the limit, my friend! Use [Wurmcalling](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/234/wurmcalling) to make 60/60 Wurms each turn! Cast and activate a turn-four [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver)! Wipe the board clean with [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), using only one land!

I took a look at the cost of these cards on ***Magic** Online* and found that they were all surprisingly cheap – perfect for a budget deck. Let's look at a breakdown for the deck.

The total cost on four [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost), four [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva), four [Sylvan Scrying](https://scryfall.com/card/bfz/192/sylvan-scrying), four [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), three [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver), four [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), three [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), and two [Wurmcalling](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/234/wurmcalling)? 25.5 tickets. This leaves us with 4.5 tickets to work with, and 32 more cards to fit into the deck.

Since we're running [Sylvan Scrying](https://scryfall.com/card/bfz/192/sylvan-scrying) and [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow), it's easy to fit in any other non-basic lands that might tickle our fancy – they are easy to search out! As a kill condition, I added in the mother of all mana-intensive lands, [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths) (30 mana? No problem – that's only four [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s over two turns!)

I also decided that I could easily support a second color of mana. In particular, the ability to recur [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver) appealed to me, so I put in the blue and added a single copy of [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins). Once I got up to thirteen mana (three [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s, an Island, an [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins), and any other two lands), I would be able to completely lock down the game with this artifact. I could activate [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver), put it back on the top of my deck, and control every turn my opponent had for the rest of the game.

Once I had added [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins), I also wanted a B-plan for killing my opponent with a recurring artifact. I settled for [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion). The Blue also enabled me to add in another spell which takes advantage of having a trazillion mana: [Spell Burst](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/88/spell-burst). Once I get multiple [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s into play, I can essentially counter a spell a turn for each Island I have in play.

Card | Cost in Tickets
13 Forests/3 Islands | Half a ticket

With one [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion), two [Spell Burst](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/88/spell-burst), four [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow), one [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths), one [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins) and the basic lands, I was up to 53 cards and 29 tickets. For the last ticket worth of cards, I went with three [Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace) (to stave off weenie rush) and four [Krosan Tusker](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/302/krosan-tusker) (another shuffling/land search mechanism), bringing the total cost of the deck to thirty tickets.

How did the deck do? Better than I could ever imagine in my wildest dreams. When I play in Extended [Pro Tour Qualifiers](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042552/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/ptq) in January, I'm going to use this deck as the starting point for qualification. Let's take a look at the game logs.

**Game 1: Brothwin (W/G Beatdown)**

He gets quick Phantom Centaur, and I get [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost), [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva), then [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow) for a third [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost). He swings in for 10, but I get [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow) (destroying one of his lands) for an [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins) to recur [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), and lock him under [Spell Burst](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/88/spell-burst). He concedes before I can find a [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver).

*Record: 1-0*

**Game 2: Zeffrin (R/W/U Good Stuff)**

I drop [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost). He drops [Bloodstained Mire](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/216/bloodstained-mire) and sacrifices it for [Sacred Foundry](https://scryfall.com/card/eoe/256/sacred-foundry). I drop [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva), play Top, and set up another [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost). He drops Hallowed Fountain and [Silver Knight](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/8/silver-knight), then [Steam Vents](https://scryfall.com/card/grn/257/steam-vents) and Chrome Mox, powering out a quick Lightning Angel. I play and activate a fourth-turn [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver).

He plays [Fact or Fiction](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/91/fact-or-fiction) for 0 (thanks to my [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver) turn!), and I [Reap and Sow](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/81/reap-and-sow) for [Academy Ruins](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/309/academy-ruins) on my turn. He attacks, and I [Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace), drop [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion) and a [Vesuva](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/289/vesuva), and put [Mindslaver](https://scryfall.com/card/som/176/mindslaver) back on top of my deck. He [Absorb](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/186/absorb)s it on my next turn, but I put it back on top of my deck, activate [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) to draw it, and replay/use it for the second time that turn. He concedes, since that allows me to lock him down for the rest of the game.

*Record: 2-0*
