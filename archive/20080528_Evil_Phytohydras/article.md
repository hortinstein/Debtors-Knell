Let me explain the ins and outs of this-here 25-0 masterpiece, because I'm sure that you all can't fully appreciate the genius that went into this deck.

[![Sprouting Phytohydra](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/dis/95?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/95/sprouting-phytohydra)

First off, I got [Sprouting Phytohydra](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/95/sprouting-phytohydra) in this deck because it's a [Sprouting Phytohydra](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/95/sprouting-phytohydra) deck. Duh. Then, I got [Ashes of the Fallen](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/152/ashes-of-the-fallen) and [Haakon, Stromgald Scourge](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/252/haakon-stromgald-scourge) because so few of you care that it's a Plant Hydra. We'll make it a Knight, cause that'll make 98.1% of you unhappy that I'm focusing on creature type, and 1.9% upset that I'm making sure it doesn't matter that our Plant Hydra is a Plant Hydra!

8.8% of you want proactive hand removal, so [One with Nothing](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/84/one-with-nothing) it is. You think I want [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress) or [Cabal Therapy](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/83/cabal-therapy) or [Mind Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/77/mind-shatter)? Your gotta prove yourself to use cards like that, you punks. Instead, papa Evil is gonna lay a whoppin' on his own hand, and then beat you down anyways despite the 'look ma, no hands!' approach to this deck! I don't want a win—I want a humiliation.

[Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation) is there because people like playing with creatures, and I don't want them to have fun playing against me. If I kill all their creatures, maybe they'll disconnect and I'll just count the game as a win for this column. Or something. I'm just going to say I won every game anyhow, and edit out all the losses. 25-0 record, here I come!

[Fecundity](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/165/fecundity) lets me draw my entire deck and get my hand stacked for [Spiraling Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/116/spiraling-embers) / [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/96/rite-of-flame) / [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/190/simian-spirit-guide) at the end, so I can do my hand's worth of damage to an opponent. Someone emailed Good Ben or posted in the forums about the Embers, but I'm taking credit for the suggestion, because this here column is my column this week, and not theirs.

I bet you wanna see how this did. I'll oblige, but only because I'm the Evil Genius, and you are not.

**Game 1: Korn1399 (Elves)**

This guy drops [Wellwisher](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/166/wellwisher) on turn two, and then puts double [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor) on her. The nerve! What am I supposed to do other than drop [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/190/simian-spirit-guide) to chum- block, and then [Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation) his board out from under him? Then I get [Fecundity](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/165/fecundity), which bites a chunky [Tranquility](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/333/tranquility). [Tranquility](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/333/tranquility)! That could wreck this whole deck! Well, not if I have a second [Fecundity](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/165/fecundity), Æther Flash, and [Sprouting Phytohydra](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/95/sprouting-phytohydra). At 6 life, I draw the majority of my deck and give him the big ol' how-do-you-do known as a 26-point [Spiraling Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/116/spiraling-embers).

korn1399: i think that was the cheapest lamest way to win

I yam what I yam!

Record: 1-0

[Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie) works well with Æther Flash, and [Wall of Roots](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/278/wall-of-roots) can kill itself with [Fecundity](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/165/fecundity). It all comes together!

**Game 4: tenshi9 (Spirits)**

A couple of quick Æther Flashes lock down the board, and a stray [Jugan, the Rising Star](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/172/jugan-the-rising-star) is dealt with via [Spiraling Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/116/spiraling-embers). The combo then goes off, and I bring myself down to two cards, play double [Bitter Ordeal](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/80/bitter-ordeal) to bring tenshi9 down to three cards left in library... and then finish him off with [Spiraling Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/116/spiraling-embers) anyhow. I pulled out my pocketwatch from the pouch I keep 'neath my horned exterior—and found that it took six minutes to draw most of my deck, four minutes to remove thirty-eight cards from tenshi9's deck, and three seconds to click on [Spiraling Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/116/spiraling-embers) to deal more than his 20 life points. Evil Ben enjoys wasting those ten minutes when only three were needed, at most.

Record: 4-0
