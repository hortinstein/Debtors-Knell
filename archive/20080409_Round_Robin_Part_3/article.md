Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! We're on part three of the first BoaB Round Robin, wherein I started with a deck suggested by a reader, evolved it for a bit, and then jumped into playing with a deck used by one of my playtest opponents! When we left off last week, I had just posted a 4-1 record with LordofDucks U/R [Rites of Initiation](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/ODY-217/rites-of-initiation) deck (you can read about it [here](https://web.archive.org/web/20090528195653/http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb158).)

I was looking for some cards to change up to make the deck more consistent. I was finding that often I would struggle to get enough creatures onto the board for a game-ending Rites. Remember, this deck is a Pauper deck, meaning that only commons are allowed (as opposed to the normal 30-ticket-or-less budget afforded to other decks in this column).

I used the **Magic Online** filter to show me all common cards in Extended that were Red, Blue, Artifact or Land. Here's the list of possibilities I came up with to add to the deck.

***Card Drawing*:** [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis), [Fathom Seer](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/68/fathom-seer), [Ideas Unbound](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/40/ideas-unbound), Word of Wisdom

These were the cards that would leave me up at least one card whenever they were cast. The more cards I draw, the more cards I have in hand for Rites, and the more spells I can cast overall. Keep in mind, however, that the deck is consistently killing on turns 4 through 6, so I don't want to necessarily lose a full turn tapping out just to draw a couple of cards.

***Cantrips*:** [Aether Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/456/aether-spellbomb), [Forgotten Cave](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/289/forgotten-cave), [Lonely Sandbar](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/166/lonely-sandbar), [Obsessive Search](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/61/obsessive-search), [Peek](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/94/peek), [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb), [Serum Visions](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/112/serum-visions), [Sleight of Hand](https://scryfall.com/card/woe/67/sleight-of-hand), [Think Twice](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/787/think-twice).

Cards designed to draw one card, either attached to a minor effect ([Peek](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/94/peek)), in lieu of a medium effect (the Spellbombs) or with the addition of greater card selection ([Sleight of Hand](https://scryfall.com/card/woe/67/sleight-of-hand)). These would help me smooth out my early game (remember, I'm killing rather quickly with this deck!), without giving up cards in hand to rites...or by giving me flexibility with the option of cycling.

***Reusable Card Draw*:** [Merfolk Looter](https://scryfall.com/card/afc/86/merfolk-looter), [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger), [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier)

Same as above, except with one card at a time over multiple turns. These serve the same purpose as [Looter il-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor), except without evasion.

I went back and forth for a while deciding which direction I'd want to take LordofDucks PDC Rites deck – did I want to move more towards upping the storm count, by focusing on [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens)? Were evasion creatures the way to go? Did I want to stuff my hand and get all combo-y with [Tolarian Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/105/tolarian-winds) and [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis)?

In the end, I decided against all of these except upping the number of storm enablers. The deck was 4-1 so far, and in the games I won I was crushing my opponents (with a deck that costs virtually nothing, natch). If the deck was working great, why mess with how it works? Instead, I looked to possibly swap out similar-functioning cards with possible upgrades.

[![Lava Dart](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mh1/134?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart)

In place of [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), I put in [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) – [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) was a swap-in for [Skred](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/97/skred), but if I wanted to up Storm Count, [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) was better than either! For the cost of R, sac a [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), I could deal two damage (divided how I choose among any number of creatures and/or players ;), plus up my storm count by two. That goes a long way for one mana, plus it allows me to kill off annoying early utility creatures that my opponent might start the game with.

In place of [Spiketail Drakeling](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/89/spiketail-drakeling), I added in Wee Dragonauts. I never really used the sacrifice effect on the Drakeling, and this deck is very offensively oriented – ie, I must hit in order to kill. On the turn that I win most games, we're looking at casting [Keep Watch](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/10/keep-watch) plus [Rites of Initiation](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/ODY-217/rites-of-initiation). Even without the Rites itself, we're looking at a 5-point swing from the Dragonauts just from casting two instants/sorceries, not to mention a turn where I double-[Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart)/[Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens), or any other shenanigans. I saw Wee Dragonauts as a way to speed up my clock considerably.

I took out [Sparksmith](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/235/sparksmith), which never really worked well for me, and would work even less well once I focused more on making 6+ goblins per cast with [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens). In its place, [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter) – the same mana cost, with the ability to whittle the opponent down early game before my Rites-fueled kill turn.

While I didn't change the focus of the deck with any of the major cards listed above, it was good to have a list of cards to know that I could swap in or out on a moment's notice. Did I want to run [Fathom Seer](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/68/fathom-seer)? I would need more [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island)s. [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) a good trick? I'd probably want [Tolarian Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/105/tolarian-winds) and more [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) effects.

**Out:** 4 [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate), 3 [Sparksmith](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/235/sparksmith), 3 [Spiketail Drakeling](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/89/spiketail-drakeling)
**In:** 3 Wee Dragonauts, 3 [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter), 3 [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart), 1 [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens)

Game 6: TheSteelCricket (W/G Control)
 I drop a [Mogg War Marshal](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DMR-131/mogg-war-marshal) turn two, and don't pay echo so I can drop another on turn three. He only plays [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest)s and [Plains](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/866/plains), and when I attack on turn four (with just a Red open), he casts [Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace) before damage (so I hold a [Rites of Initiation](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/ODY-217/rites-of-initiation) in my hand – I don't think I would have cast it there anyhow, in fear of any disruption spell – [Condemn](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/14/condemn), Wing Shards, etc, making me auto-lose the game). He does, however, tap out to play Commander Eesha the next turn. **Game 7: tkennan (W/G Tokens)**

He drops first and second turn [Llanowar Reborn](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/341/llanowar-reborn), making a 2/2 Squirrel token with Chatter of the Squirrel on turn two. I cast [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/159/ponder) turn one, setting up [Mogg War Marshal](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DMR-131/mogg-war-marshal) turn two and a second [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/159/ponder) into [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) on turn five. By then, he's cast [Sprout Swarm](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/138/sprout-swarm), Mosswart Bridge, [Sprout](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/221/sprout), and flashed back the Chatter. I go down to seven on his attack, and then swing in with my five Goblins (one died to his [Sprout](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/221/sprout) plus [Llanowar Reborn](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/341/llanowar-reborn) earlier). He casts [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush), but I have [Keep Watch](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/10/keep-watch) and Rites to kill him regardless. He shows me his hand, and it contains double Angel of Salvation and Murgana Petroglyphs.

Record: 6-1

**Game 8: Bagyvon (R/G Non-Budget)**

[![Keep Watch](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/hop/10?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/10/keep-watch)

Over the course of the game, he plays double [Stomping Ground](https://scryfall.com/card/eoe/258/stomping-ground), [Karplusan Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/164/karplusan-forest), double [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), [Grim Lavamancer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/126/grim-lavamancer), [Flametongue Kavu](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/120/flametongue-kavu), [Magma Jet](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/346/magma-jet) and [Sun Droplet](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/268/sun-droplet). **Game 9: o Psilocybe o (W/G/B Control)**

He casts [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), double [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer) and double [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress). His first [Duress](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/606/duress) hits nothing – my hand is [Mulldrifter](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/67/mulldrifter), [Looter il-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor) and [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter) (which takes down his entire board). I get down Ninja of Deep Hours and start drawing three cards a turn (discarding one of them because of [Looter il-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor)). I keep a grip with [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) and plenty of action in case he has a mass-removal spell – as is, my board is winning the game, so why overextended? He Mortifies my Ninja, so I drop [Mogg War Marshal](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DMR-131/mogg-war-marshal) down. On my attack, I draw a second Ninja after dealing damage with my Looter, so I ninjutsu the War Marshal back to my hand (remember, even after damage, there's still a window for using instants and effects, so you can get the Ninja on the board, just to get it on the table), allowing me to recast the War Marshal. He concedes on his next turn.

Record: 8-1

**Game 10: Shruktus (Black/Blue Control)**

He hits me with [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats), and drops [Dimir Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/186/dimir-guildmage), Urborg and [Quicksand](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/245/quicksand) on the board. I lose three creatures to Death Pulse, but manage to get [Mulldrifter](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/67/mulldrifter) cast three times with double [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours). After a long war of attrition, I finally land an [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) with two storm count (making six Goblins) allowing me to cast double [Keep Watch](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/10/keep-watch) to find a [Rites of Initiation](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/ODY-217/rites-of-initiation) for the win.

Record: 9-1

**Game 11: Eagleye (U/G [Doubling Season](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/216/doubling-season))**

He gets down a [Doubling Season](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/216/doubling-season) early, and proceeds to drop [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion) (which kills [Mogg War Marshal](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/DMR-131/mogg-war-marshal) and his two tokens, plus [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter) and two [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) tokens), [Beacon of Creation](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/82/beacon-of-creation) (for eight guys) and Pentavite (which makes a ton of flyers). I knock him down to twelve with a [Looter il-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/102/looter-il-kor) on the table and two [Rites of Initiation](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/ODY-217/rites-of-initiation) in hand, but he gets [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate) for [Coat of Arms](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/58/coat-of-arms) to kill me with Pentavite tokens.

Record: 9-2

This deck would be an interesting play for me, because this isn't the type of deck I would have built if left to my own devices. I wanted to approach evolving this deck the way I would a theme deck – even though there are some cards in here I think (on paper) are sub-optimal, I wasn't familiar enough with how they actually played in-game to see if they were good or not! In particular, I wasn't a huge fan of Leyline of the Meek (which I viewed as a very-expensive [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem) more often than not, when it wouldn't be in my starting hand), Angel of Salvation (will this deck get to eight mana between lands and convoked creatures, and not want to swing?), or Mosswort Bridge (how often will I have ten power in tokens on the board?)

**Game 1: DedalusEx (Affinity)**

I get down a 2/2 Squirrel token (thanks to [Llanowar Reborn](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/341/llanowar-reborn)), bounce the land with [Selesnya Sanctuary](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/246/selesnya-sanctuary), play [Raise the Alarm](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/41/raise-the-alarm) and [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/214/gilt-leaf-ambush), drop Leyline of the Meek, and then clear his entire board with [Hour of Reckoning](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/120/hour-of-reckoning). Garruk Wildspeaker

I'd like to take a minute to say a quick word about Garruk Wildspeaker. Right now, he is definitely *not* what I, or anyone else, would consider a budget card – six tickets is a lot to trade for any one given card, and a playset of Garruks would require me to take out around four tickets worth of rares from the deck. I would note, though, that the rule of Building on a Budget is that I build decks that cost 30 tickets or less using Magic Online prices. Garruk would definitely be a great addition to this deck – untapping comes-into-play tapped lands, putting a 3/3 vanilla beast into play (which would work great with both Petroglyphs and Leyline of the Meek) and [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) (which, again, would be a great finisher with the amount of tokens this deck pumps out).

While I could safely add three to this deck, and be under budget (by two tickets), instead I want to keep this deck at a lower cost. Basically – if you have some extra tickets, or you want to push the thirty-ticket limit (and remember, this is a limit – I don't always have to hit it, just when necessary....and yes, this is foreshadowing for my next deck in this round robin!), Garruk would be awesome for this deck.
 As-is, I decided to go with [Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity) – a cheaper way to pump all of my guys, and believe me this deck can pump out guys! I decided against going with the vanilla bruisers ([Watchwolf](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/220/watchwolf), [Savannah Lions](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/146/savannah-lions)) because the token theme was working out well so far – but [Watchwolf](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/220/watchwolf) would be the absolute next card I'd add to the deck (or put in place of [Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity), if it didn't work out). Even without any pumping, 3/3 for two (with no drawbacks) is huge – I'd have to work [Raise the Alarm](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/41/raise-the-alarm) plus Petroglyphs (turn four) to equal what I get, raw, out of [Watchwolf](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/220/watchwolf) on turn two.

Call of the Herd was another hard card to not add to the deck – it fits great on the curve (I'm low on three-drops pre-[Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity)), but I couldn't fit both [Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity) and Call of the Herd into the same deck and keep a budget. This was an either/or situation based on budgetary constrictions, and if [Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity) didn't work out, I would likely yank both it and one of my token creature generators (probably [Sprout Swarm](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/138/sprout-swarm)) in exchange for a package of [Watchwolf](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/220/watchwolf) and Call of the Herd.
