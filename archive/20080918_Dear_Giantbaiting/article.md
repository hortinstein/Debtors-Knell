# Dear Giantbaiting

**Author:** Jacob Van Lunen  
**Date:** September 18, 2008  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/boab/4>

---

So I was sitting earlier this week at my desk with some commons that I wanted to build a deck with. I awkwardly rocked back and forth with my knees and elbows pressed together. I glanced over at [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting). I quickly looked down and thought to myself, "Was it looking back at me? All my friends think [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) is ugly. What would they think?"

[pause]

"It doesn't matter, Jake," I told myself. "You can't live a lie forever."

[![Giantbaiting](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mm3/208?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting)

I begin writing a note, occasionally glancing over at the [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) with my sexy look (known to insiders as T.N.I.B.S. or The New and Improved Blue Steel).

Dear [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting),

I've had a crush on you for awhile now. I just didn't know what to say. Sometimes I see people using you as a coaster and I want to cry. To me, [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting), you're beautiful. This may be a little forward, but... do you think I could play with you?

Love,
 Jacob

*I pick up the [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) and gently sway it back and forth like a newborn kitten. The air conditioner under my desk suddenly comes on and blows it out of my hand.*

"Noooooo!"

*As I lean down to pick up the [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) I realize that it has fallen on top of another card on the floor. I pick up both cards and hold one in each hand. [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) wants me to play with its friend, too.*

[![Bramblewood Paragon](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mor/115?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon)

Just let it sink in for a second. Yep, that's right, with [Bramblewood Paragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon), [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) puts two 5/5 creatures with trample and haste into play. That's a lot of damage, and anyone who knows me well knows that the only thing I love doing more than playing awkward midrange control decks with late-game combos is playing ridiculously fast aggressive decks.

Now what other cards should I put in here? I'm playing with [Bramblewood Paragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon), so I probably want to play a good number of Warriors. I can actually put [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe) in the deck and have an interesting Warrior theme. After a quick gatherer search I have a nice big list of green Warriors and am ready to get into building.

##### The One-Drops

[![Llanowar Elves](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/fdn/227?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves)

Most decks don't have the luxury of playing more than eight one casting cost creatures because they won't have much clout later on in the game and they'll weaken late game topdecking. A deck like this is special, though; [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe) makes my opponent's Wrath effects a lot less impressive. I can draw any creature, play it, equip it, and bash for a significant amount of damage that very same turn. A deck like this can try to maximize its aggressive draws by playing at least twelve one-drops. Even when unequipped, my one-drops can conspire [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting).

[Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) seems like an obvious inclusion. It accelerates the deck into powerful three-cost cards like [Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect) and [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe).

[Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid) will serve very well as [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) five through eight.

[Essence Warden](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/106/essence-warden) is good against decks with red cards, it also helps win the race against other elf decks.

[Elvish Hexhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/shm/226/elvish-hexhunter) will allow me to remove pesky [Oblivion Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/29/oblivion-ring)s and [Story Circle](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/49/story-circle)s that might otherwise put the bad in badtle.

[Heritage Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/171/heritage-druid) seems like it could make for some pretty amazing starts. I've lost a few games in my day to very fast draws involving this gentleman.

##### The Two-Drops

Normally a good aggressive deck has anywhere from eight to sixteen two-cost cards. My deck can fall on the bottom half of that range because it has eight turn-one mana accelerators.

[Bramblewood Paragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon) is one of the major reasons I'm playing this deck. He'll have amazing synergy with my [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) and the other warriors I decide to include.

[Wren's Run Vanquisher](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/447/wrens-run-vanquisher) is absolutely insane. The ability to play a 3/3 with a relevant ability on turn two is something we **Magic** players wouldn't have dreamed up until recently. Sometimes a 3/3 on turn two is enough tempo to win a game all on its own. Auto four-of.

##### The Three-Drops

Usually when you're building a good aggressive deck you don't want more than eight cards that cost three mana. The reason I can get away with playing sixteen three-drops is the same reason I can get away with playing so few two-drops; [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) and [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid) allow me to accelerate into some these cards with relative ease.

[![Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mor/144?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe)

[Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting), oh how I love thee. This card will enable a lot of turn-four kills with a deck like this and I'm pretty sure that's good enough for the pro tour, let alone the casual room. It's important to note that it isn't wise to just play a turn-two [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting) because you played a mana accelerator on your first turn. [Giantbaiting](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/208/giantbaiting)s should be held until you can conspire them, preferably with a [Bramblewood Paragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/115/bramblewood-paragon) in play, unless you are swinging for lethal damage.

[Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe) gives this deck the longevity to overcome powerful board sweepers like [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout) and [Damnation](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/73/damnation).

[Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect) is an automatic four-of. Think about this: turn one [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) or [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid), turn two [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe), turn three play [Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect), equip the Battle-Axe for free, make a token, equip the Battle-Axe for free, swing for 4. Seems fine to me.

Boggart Ram-Gang isn't a card I expected to see when I gathered up a list of warriors, but the Ram-Gang can do exactly what this deck wants it to. I envision myself playing a turn-two Paragon into a turn-three Ram-Gang and swinging for huge amounts of damage. It's important to note that I can't cast these gentlemen with the colorless Mana produced by [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid). So if I'm presented with the option of [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) or [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid) on the first turn, it's generally correct to play the [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) even if I don't have the Ram-Gang in hand. I never know what might be waiting on the top of my library.

##### The Top of the Curve

It's unusual for a deck like this to play many expensive spells, and this one is no exception. I want my cards that cost more than three mana to have a huge impact on the game. Oftentimes, by the time a creature-based aggressive deck is playing four- or five-cost spells, the opponent, armed with a control deck, has already started to take over the game.

[Hunting Triad](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/116/hunting-triad) is great wrath effect insurance to hold in my hand in conjunction with an [Imperious Perfect](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/719/imperious-perfect). When battling other creature based decks it does some pretty exciting stuff. If I have a Paragon in play it will make a bunch of able and willing fighters. If I have just played my fourth land and I attack with Ram-Gang, my opponent may sense some urgency and decide to [Nameless Inversion](https://scryfall.com/card/ecl/113/nameless-inversion) the Gang. BAM!! "How does 9 damage feel?!" Yeah, it's pretty exciting.

[Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) is no stranger to winning games that are otherwise impossible. In creature-based matches, this card will break stalemates quickly and decisively.

Playing a single [Pendelhaven](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/244/pendelhaven) doesn't seem to have any drawback, and it might come in handy to get an extra point in now and let my tokens fight with [Blood Knight](https://scryfall.com/card/plc/115/blood-knight)s and live to tell the tale.

We're going to have to make some cuts if we want this deck to run smoothly. The number of one-drops seems very excessive right now, so I'll start by trimming off some of that. [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), [Boreal Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/105/boreal-druid), and [Heritage Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/171/heritage-druid) seem way too good in a deck like this to even consider cutting them. I think we can get away with only playing twelve creatures in our one slot this week, so I'm going to move the [Elvish Hexhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/shm/226/elvish-hexhunter) and the [Essence Warden](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/106/essence-warden) to the sideboard. It's important to remember that these cards are very good in particular matchups.

Now I can toy with the rest of the numbers a bit. I really like [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/mor/144/obsidian-battle-axe), but drawing more than one seems like it would be pretty miserable. I think it'll be safe to play three of this awesome Equipment. [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun) is an amazingly powerful spell, but I would really hate to have multiple five-cost spells clogging up my hand. I think I'll just play one or two. The rest of the deck seems awesome. I think I have a battle-worthy maindeck; now I just need a good sideboard.

I've already explained that [Essence Warden](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/106/essence-warden) is good against red and other Elf decks and that [Elvish Hexhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/shm/226/elvish-hexhunter) is a really good answer to otherwise insurmountable enchantments like [Story Circle](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/49/story-circle). I'll put four of each of these cards in the sideboard, and I'll probably take out the [Heritage Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/171/heritage-druid)s if I'm planning on bringing one of them in.

I'm really worried about getting [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout)ed, so I should probably have an answer to that in the sideboard. It may seem narrow, but I think Wrap In [Vigor](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/215/vigor) is a great counterspell effect when fighting a [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout) deck. Again, I'd probably take out my [Heritage Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/171/heritage-druid) if I was planning on siding these in. I doubt I'll get to activate the ability when I'm having my board wiped by [Firespout](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/190/firespout) anyway.

The last three spots should probably be [Squall Line](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/222/squall-line); this card is great against pesky Faeries and should be sided in against most control decks as a green [Fireball](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/175/fireball) of sorts. It's a rare, though, and might be hard to come by. Some [Nettle Sentinel](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/182/nettle-sentinel)s or another aggressive card might be a good replacement if you can't find enough of these.
