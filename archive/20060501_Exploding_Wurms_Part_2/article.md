Welcome back to my second go-around with my Exploding Wurms! deck from [last week's column](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041258/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb56). This column probably won't make a whit of sense unless [you click on this link](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041258/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb56) and read last week's column first. Seriously! [Clickity click click!](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041258/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb56).

Thank you to those who clicked on one of those links – but I have a feeling that some newcomers might be a little lost. That's all right! Let me summarize last week's article. I built a Red/Black/Blue deck that uses the combination of [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) and [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) to deal a quick 15 damage to my opponent's dome. The deck had a lot of synergy, such as [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) plus discarding fatties plus [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), and [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) plus dredge plus [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), and [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) plus [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top).

The deck had a strong showing last week, and I heard back from several players who had built the deck – so far it seems to be a runaway success! Here's a copy of last week's last copy of the last decklist, when last we left off:

As you'll remember, the first version of the deck was Black/Red and featured very few ways to get to [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), to get creatures into the graveyard to reanimate, or to win in general. The revised version added in Blue for card drawing and discard effects.

This week, I'm going to approach the evolution of the deck a little differently. You see, when I first built the deck, I had two different versions of the deck in mind – both of which operated on the same principle of [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames)/[Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm), but each of which took vastly different approaches in reaching that goal. By the end of this column, we'll have two decks that, while they have the same basis, operate in vastly, vastly different ways.

Before we get to the parallel-universe Exploding Wurms! deck, let me say that the feedback I've received, both in the forums and through e-mail, have been invaluable towards improving Building on a Budget. One of the most important parts of the deckbuilding process is being able to keep your mind open to ideas – not only in regards to which cards you use, but from suggestions that others throw out there.

In that spirit, all deck changes this week were taken directly from suggestions from you, my readers, and have been sprinkled with a dusting of Bleiweissian sensibility (or should that be in-sensibility?)

Zombie Nixon said: “I like the deck a lot, but it occurs to me that there are still changes to be made. Like this one: drop Black altogether. Drop it like de-pinned grenade. Make the deck straight U/R. The only two black cards you use now are zombify and stinkweed imp, and while they've probably proven their worth, they'd be better off as additional burn, [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak), and some Ethereal Ushers to transmute for flames.”

So that's just what I did.

**Out**: 3 [Dimir Aqueduct](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/270/dimir-aqueduct), 4 [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), 4 Swamp, 4 [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify)

This was a change I had contemplated during my first round of testing. Wasn't the main point of the deck to blow people up using [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames)? Blue had proven a lot more useful than Black as far as getting cards into my graveyard for reanimation, but what if I did away with the reanimation theme entirely?

The first change was to the mana base – seven Black producers out, and seven Blue/Red producers in. I re-added [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) to the deck, as my focus was now to get to the [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) combo as quickly as possible. [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) also allows me to power out a quick [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (which, did you know, costs eight to cast?). [Telling Time](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/75/telling-time) served the same purpose – it digs three cards deeper into the deck, plus it can set up [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) if you've got eight mana to spare the turn you cast the Flames.

Unfortunately, this version of the deck didn't work very well. Witness:

**Game #1: Punjoke (Mono-G Snake beats)**

This was a rematch from last week. This time around, Punjoke was running mono-Green beats. He got down a couple of early creatures, put [Moldervine Cloak](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/150/moldervine-cloak) on a bloodthirsted Skarrgan Pit-Skulk, and then cast [Strength of Cedars](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/245/strength-of-cedars) to make it freaking' huge. I was left at two life when I drew [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (you know, the eight-mana creature?). Unfortunately, Punjoke had used [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) to get back the Strength, so I was unable to cast the (eight-mana) Colossus, and was too low in life to use the (converted-mana-cost eight) Colossus to clear the board.

If the [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song)s in my hand had been Zombifies, I could have gotten the Colossus (the guy from Apoclaypse who costs Red, Red, Six) into play on turn 4, making this a much different game. Alas, this was not to be.

*Record: 0-1*

**Game #2: MadPiper05 (W/R Searing Flesh/Samurai)**

Here's a little factoid – it doesn't matter how well you build your deck, if you play horribly, you *will* lose. Don't ever let yourself go on autopilot like I did this game – I cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), doing fifteen with [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm). I reordered the top of my deck, and put the second [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) in the wrong place. Instead of killing MadPiper05, I did two damage to him with a Flamed-[Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm), and then drew Wurm #2. He then proceeded to kill me with [Honden of Cleansing Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/13/honden-of-cleansing-fire) and Searing Flesh over multiple turns – I never got enough damage out of my deck to keep up from that point onwards.

*Record: 0-2 (Though I should have been 1-1, but it was my bad.)*

**Game #3: Goldenfoon (R/W JMS's Lifegain Thingy)**

The non-Black version of this deck is seriously lacking in a plan B, as I am able to discard Wurm with [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), but I end up getting bashed too quickly by a [Sunhome Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/233/sunhome-enforcer) with a [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar) on it. To add insult to injury, Goldenfoon had an active [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/324/sunhome-fortress-of-the-legion) that ended the game before I could get mobilized at all.

*Record: 0-3*

This is where I stopped with the non-reanimation strategy. Unless I got [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), my deck had no chance to win – which made all of my large creatures drawn into virtually dead draws, just like in the first version of the deck (remember, the Black/Red one). The only use for a drawn [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) is an easy discard choice when I tap [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) – and that minimizes the overall utility and combos possible with the deck.

[![Congregation at Dawn](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rav/198?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn)

The first time around, I had addition through addition – the power of the deck went up when I added a color. The second time around, I had subtraction through subtraction – the power level of the deck went down when I cut out one of the deck's two roads to victory.

Ok, so detour #1 was a bust – no biggy! If you don't try, you never know – and I definitely felt that the B/R/U version of the deck was superior to both the B/R and U/R versions of the deck.

Quite a few people made the suggestion that I shift the deck away from B/R/U and shift it towards G/W. The reason they gave? [Congregation at Dawn](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn). Congregation lets me theoretically set up the kill as a two-card combo. Here's how it works:

1. Cast [Congregation at Dawn](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn) at the end of my opponent's turn. Grab three creatures – two [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm), and one of anything else. Put the anything else on top of my library, with the two Wurms underneath it.
2. Draw the random creature, and then cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) for 15.
3. [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) triggers during my upkeep, doing another 15 damage from the other Wurm.

This approach had a lot of merit, plus who am I to argue with my readers? I rebuilt Exploding Wurms! from the ground up. Here's what I came up with!

*(Full decklist with sideboard: `decklist_3_Green_Exploding_Wurms_10.txt`; priced breakdown: `decklist_3_Green_Exploding_Wurms_10_priced.md`)*

The core of the deck remains the same – cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) to throw [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) (or other large creatures/spells) at my opponent. To this effect, many cards in the deck remained unchanged – [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm), [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) (to order cards both before and after the Flames) are all still present.

The rest – a mystery to be unraveled!

The first additions I made to the deck were [Congregation at Dawn](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn). This was the most suggested card to add to the deck, and it'd be pretty silly if I changed colors to accommodate Congregation, and then didn't play it! My playtesting with the non-reanimator version of Exploding Wurms! made me gun shy about removing ways to get creatures from the graveyard to play, so [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) stayed in the deck as well. I also liked the interaction with [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top)/[Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), plus the Imp is a way to stall the game a turn or two in order to get the combo going.

This presented a problem though – suddenly the deck was four colors. In fact, not only did it require four colors, but some of the mana requirements were getting out of hand. [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) requires double-Red mana, [Congregation at Dawn](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn) double-Green and single-White. Well, I'm playing Green now, right? Time to add in a budget deck-builder's favorite mana fixers: [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) and [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach). These two cards should help smooth the mana base a lot, plus they work well with [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top). If I want to see three new cards, these two mana-fixers/accelerators allow me, at worst, to shuffle up my deck.

I also wanted to change up the creature base slightly. The last version ran six creatures – three copies of [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) and three copies of [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (for a total of twenty-four mana of Bloodfire Colossi!). Since I was now playing a lot less Red and a smattering of White, I figured I could add in a couple of guys that I had a possibility to cast in a long game. Out went the (eight mana) [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus), and in came two copies of [Blazing Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/8/blazing-archon) and one copy of [Razia, Boros Archangel](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/92/razia-boros-archangel).

Why Razia and Archon? One of the strengths of [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (in its eight-mana glory) was its dual ability to both clear the board, and get my opponent within a single [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm)-fuelled [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) in one fell swoop. Both Razia and Archon discourage or outright prevent my opponent from attacking. In addition, Razia allows me to attack the turn it is reanimated, making for a similar lifeswing to a reanimation/activation of [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (which, instead of costing nine mana for casting and activating, costs five mana instead thanks to [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify)).

One of the major problems I found with [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) (who I hear costs eight) was that it was a popular target for [Pillory of the Sleepless](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/213/pillory-of-the-sleepless) and [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters). This kept it from being able to block, with the latter of the two forcing an activation of the Colossus in most cases. [Blazing Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/8/blazing-archon) can be neutralized all day long by auras, but as long as it is in play, my opponents can't attack. Plus, it's a 5/6 flyer, making it a short clock in a short game.

The initial build of the Exploding Wurms! deck, back when it was R/B, had two major weaknesses – it was vulnerable to a weenie rush, and it had no way to discard cards. Blue solved that problem thanks to [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier), and [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm), but the Green Exploding Wurms! deck wasn't going to have access to Blue mana. In addition, access to Red was a lot more limited, so [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm)s would be less reliable in the new build. How could I solve these problems?

[Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) helps a little, because it can usually block for a turn, stalling at least one creature. I still have [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp)s as well, but neither of these guys solves the getting-creatures-I've-drawn-into-the-graveyard-to-be-reanimated problem I originally faced. I did a search of every card available in Standard right now, and found my solution:

[Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind).

[Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind) was a perfect card for this deck – it allows me to get my creatures into the graveyard for [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify). I extends my life total by three for each card in my hand, allowing me more of a chance to survive an early creature rush. It enables me to toss [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) into my graveyard in case I need to dredge. Best of all, it gives me something to do with the cards in my hand after I've cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) – I can still discard each card I've drawn for three life, since activating [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind) is not a spell.

The only other change I made to the deck was to swap Signets for [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone). I figured that since I was four colors now, [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) would have more general utility than any one specific Signet. In addition, it let me be able to have the play of turn 1 [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), turn 2 [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)/activate Top – Signets are not capable of this start.

Before I shuffled up for my first game, I had three major questions about this deck:

1. Would the mana base be able to support the deck? You'll notice I'm running all basic lands. This is because I want to be certain that I can search out the lands I need with [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) and [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder). However, Black/double-Green/White (or triple-White, if you count [Blazing Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/8/blazing-archon))/double-Red might be a bit much to ask from a single deck.
2. Would [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind) be enough of a substitute for both [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) and [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier), as far as discard goes? Would [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) plus [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) be sufficient replacements for [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) and [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) as far as accelerating the deck goes? The old version accelerated card quality through card drawing – the new version accelerated through mana development.
3. Would [Congregation at Dawn](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/198/congregation-at-dawn) be any good, given that it's almost useless in this deck except as a combo enabler?

**Game #1: Cixelsid (R/U [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode)/Wee Dragonauts)**

Wee Dragonauts

I accelerate my mana with [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) and [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach), drop [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind), and cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), backed by [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), on turn 5. Cixelsid gets out [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) and Wee Dragonauts, and proceeds to have the following turn:

Shoot with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) (1 damage)
[Lava Spike](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/136/lava-spike) (4 damage, [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) untaps)
 Shoot with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) (5 damage)
 Tap Plains with [Gigadrowse](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/26/gigadrowse) ([Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) untaps), shoot with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) (6 damage)
[Glacial Ray](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/115/glacial-ray) (8 damage, [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) untaps), shoot with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) (9 damage)
 Attack with 7/3 Dragonauts (16 damage)

Come on people! I'm the one who is supposed to be doing fifteen damage in one turn, not my opponents!

Thankfully, I'm in the mid-twenties life total before the attack, thanks to [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind). I kill Dragonauts with [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) (off the Flames), and then discard [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) to gain three life and dredge away my top cards. I'm at five life, with only the Imp in my hand, when Cixelsid untaps, and plays [Ire of Kaminari](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/109/ire-of-kaminari) for six. He then shoots me twice with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode).

I still win this game. I activate [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) three times – once to order the top three cards of my library, in response to draw a card, and in response to order the top three cards again. This enables me to look at my library to see if I want to dredge [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) (I did), dredge the Imp (to discard to [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind) and to clear the chaff off of the top of my library), and then bury the now-on-top-of-my-library Top further down in my library so it wouldn't get picked up by [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) the following turn.

Let me just state for the record that the B/R/U version of Exploding Wurms! would have lost the game on that turn. That doesn't necessarily make either version of the deck better or worse, but I definitely made note that I had many, many more options with the R/G/W/B version.

I end up winning a couple of turns later, as I can gain three life a turn with [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind), which keeps me out of burn range.

*Record: 1-0*

**Game#2: Jypsum1 (B/W utility)**

This is the game when Green Exploding Wurms! went all funky. Grok this:

Turn 1: Land (1 mana)
 Turn 2: Land, [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) (3 mana)
 Turn 3: Land, [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone), [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) (6 mana)

One of the best things that can happen when you build a new deck is to be surprised by the potential plays the deck can pull out. Never did I expect to be casting a nine-drop creature with triple-White in its mana-cost on turn 5. But yet here was [Blazing Archon](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/8/blazing-archon), stopping up the board.

Unfortunately, AJ_Roach had gotten out a [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), so the Archon sat there unable to attack. The game ends up grinding to a halt – I get down [Peace of Mind](https://scryfall.com/card/emn/36/peace-of-mind). Razia enabled the kill, since I could redirect the [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) damage from my Archon to one of his creatures, both saving my Archon and killing his creatures (remember, redirected damage remembers its source, so the Imp would still trigger even after having its damage moved from my Archon to his [Golgari Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/194/golgari-guildmage)).

*Record: 6-2*

Even though both versions of Exploding Wurms! (B/R/U and G/W/R/B) are based off of the same principle, they both play extremely differently, yet both are very competent, fun, competitive decks. This tells me that [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) + [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) + reanimation is definitely a strong theme to build around. If you like playing combo decks that can kill on turn 5 or 6 reliably, or sending a fifteen point Wurm sandwich into your opponent's kisser, play the B/R/U version. If you like having more utility, more interaction, and the possibility of casting 9/14 creatures without cheating through reanimation, then the G/W/R/B version of Exploding Wurms! would be your cup of tea.
