I also closed my last column with the notion that I would be adding three new cards to the deck: [Sulfurous Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/262/sulfurous-blast), Take Possession, and [Riddle of Lightning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/183/riddle-of-lightning). Well, this was not to be. Why not? The budget, that's why not!

Here's the current cost of the deck, as is:
[Delay](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/61/delay): 0.75 Tickets Each (3)
[Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon): 1 Ticket Each (4) 
 Jhoira: 2 Tickets Each (8)
[Deep-Sea Kraken](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/3/deep-sea-kraken): 0.5 Tickets Each (2)
 Miscellaneous Commons/Uncommons: 1 Ticket Total
 Total: 18 Tickets

The original deck clocked in at eighteen tickets. When I went to add Take Possession to the deck, I found that they are currently selling for a whopping **SIX** tickets each! Now look, I've been known to fit in a slightly higher-dollar card into my deck if it's going to be the most important card I can have—but Take Possession was going to be a fun offshoot of a card, and not one that I would throw in a deck on a whim at 24 tickets a playset. Heck, at the current configuration, if I added two Take Possession to the deck, that would entirely kill the budget of the deck!

Along the same lines, I dismissed [Aeon Chronicler](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler) in my initial build due to budgetary constraints. When I used [Aeon Chronicler](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler) for two of the decks in my 10 Decks in 10 Weeks experiment (both in my [Aeon in Flux](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042726/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb104) and in my [Grim Outlook](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042726/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb105) decks), this guy ran about half-a-ticket. Nowadays, his price is up to a hefty four tickets.

Still, the majority of e-mails I received regarding this week's column asked why/if I could add my main suspend man to the Jhoira deck. "But Ben," you said, "it's perfect with Jhoira! You spend two mana and you get four cards. You'd have to spend {7}{U} to get that same effect without Jhoira! It's a perfect fit for this deck! It's card advantage!"

I agree! When faced with a choice between a card that can be central to the deck as both a card-advantage engine and a finisher—one that has great synergy with multiple cards in the deck—and an offshoot two-of, I'll choose the multi-purpose, synergistic card any day.

[![Aeon Chronicler](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/tsr/51?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler)

In short, sorry Take Possession—maybe after the Block Constructed Season cools off and you're at the 1-2 ticket price you deserve to be at, I'll give you a whirl. Until that happens, make mine Chronicler!

In: 3 [Aeon Chronicler](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler) (+12) (30 Tickets)

On the other end of the spectrum, there's [Riddle of Lightning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/183/riddle-of-lightning). This *Future Sight* common is a great fit for a budget deck, and especially one that is running so many high-mana cost spells. [Riddle of Lightning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/183/riddle-of-lightning) for [Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon) or [Deep-Sea Kraken](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/3/deep-sea-kraken)? That's 10 to the dome!

In: 3 [Riddle of Lightning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/183/riddle-of-lightning)

This certainly isn't the way I started last week, and I can pinpoint my problems to two sources—not enough mana, and not the right colors of mana. In both games, I've been stuck on one color for far too long to make a go at the game, and I have been too short on mana to manage cards like [Aeon Chronicler](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/51/aeon-chronicler), and the such. In order to fix this problem, I head for the color-fixing land of budgetary choice—[Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse).

In: 4 [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse)

The Jhoira Avatar is running only 2 tickets each right now from most people, so I would suggest picking it up now—it's a ton of fun running a game where you sling sorceries and instants back and forth! Here's some tips that I've gotten so far from playing a few Jhoira games:

1. You almost always want to go with the sorcery option. They tend to be more powerful, and there are several sorceries that can almost outright win the game ([Wit's End](https://scryfall.com/card/m13/117/wits-end), Crush of Wurms), whereas instants are best saved for digging for a desperation [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) against your opponent's game-ending sorcery.
2. You want to work up to six mana (for two sorceries a turn) in most games, and then sit there. If you end up with a few card-drawing spells ([Concentrate](https://scryfall.com/card/ddt/4/concentrate), [Harmonize](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/175/harmonize)), head for nine so you can drop three spells a turn.
3. Land destruction/denial is key in this format. There are a ton of [Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain) variants, and cards such as Temporal Spring work just as well for denying your opponent mana. Unless someone gets a huge early creature with a lucky sorcery, the name of the game is mana and card advantage.
4. The correct configuration of lands for your deck, as I've found, is 20 [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp), 10 [Plains](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/866/plains), 10 [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), 10 [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest), and 10 [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island). You want to be able to take advantage of Domain spells such as Tribal Flames or Allied Strategies. You also need to acknowledge that black has the most "land-type" matters sorceries out there, such as [Corrupt](https://scryfall.com/card/bro/88/corrupt) or [Mind Sludge](https://scryfall.com/card/zen/102/mind-sludge).
5. If you get the option to put down a creature early, even if it's a Chatter of Squirrel token, take it (unless there a *much* better option available). This opens the door for a quick win with [Biorhythm](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/231/biorhythm), because there aren't many quick wins in this format otherwise!
6. If you pull up three stinkers with your Jhoira pull, you don't have to choose any of them! Simply click "[Cancel](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/505/cancel)" and all three choices will be discarded, and you will do nothing for that spell. It's better to swing and miss than to [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm) away your own [Grizzly Fate](https://scryfall.com/card/plst/JUD-119/grizzly-fate) tokens. (Yes, I did this before the cancel button was brought to my attention).
