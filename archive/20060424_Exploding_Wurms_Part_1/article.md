Erratic Explosion

Speaking of Worms….

Ever since the dawn of Magickind, the Red mage has had one goal – blow things up real good. First came [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/806/lightning-bolt) and [Fireball](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/175/fireball), and then came [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) and Kaervek's Torch. But lo, that was not enough. Red wanted fireworks, flames and great balls of fire, not great [Fireball](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/175/fireball)s. So Wizards of the Coast printed a feisty little number known as Erratic Explosion.

All this was fine and groovy, but big pappa Bleiweiss wanted more. More explosions! More drama! More damage straight to the dome! Erratic Explosion came, went, and was promptly forgotten. Years later, the direct ancestor of Erratic Explosion saw print, and was completely ignored. This was the card of my dreams: [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames).

[Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) solved one of the problems of Erratic Explosion – once you deal your damage, how do you kill your opponent? Simple – by copying [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) every turn for the rest of the game! I wanted something really big to throw at my opponent. No, bigger than that. Seriously, I needed a huge monster to throw across the table, one that would make my foe fall from their chair and crash to the floor.

[Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), meet [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm). [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm), meet [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames).

I spent all day scheming about this deck, with visions of fifteen to the dome dancing in my head. What else would work well in the deck? Well, [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) was a must, since I'd want to set up my draws after I cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames). Remember, even though you can't cast any other spells for the remainder of the game after you cast an Epic spell, you can still use effects. I also wanted [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) – it is early defense, and a way to dredge past undesired cards from the top. Black would also give me access to reanimation spells – [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) and [Vigor Mortis](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/74/vigor-mortis) to be exact! Man oh man, this was getting better and better. Not only could I fling for fifteen, I could also come across the red zone with a 9/14 trampler!

A little muss and a little fuss later, and here was my result. Please note that this deck was built for Standard play.

I decided on [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) as my second fatty of choice for multiple reasons. First of all, he's one of the highest mana-cost creatures in Standard. Second, he's 6/6 for six. That's nothing to sneeze at. Third, I can reanimate him and clear the board – so as early as turn 4 (with a [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) or [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)) I can pop him and use him as an Inferno. Fourth, I can hard-cast him, even without reanimation. [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) also helped in this regard.

[Darkblast](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/82/darkblast) was a second way to get cards into my graveyard – I envisioned that I could kill an early creature, and then dredge to drop cards in the bin. [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) was for general acceleration, as well as allowing for a fourth-turn [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames). I really wanted to get the ridiculous draw of turn 1 [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), turn 2 [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone), active Top, turn 3 [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song), [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) for 15, turn 4 kill my opponent.

Did this ever happen? Did I kill my opponent on the fourth turn with this deck? Did my dreams of exploding wurms come true?

The answer was an emphatic no.

I felt a sickening, sinking feeling in the pit of my stomach, but I refused to give into despair. This was not going to be a bad deck. I had dreamt about [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) for months – the blueprints of Flames/large creature/Reanimation had been rolled over and over in my mind. I would make it win, even if I had to reach across the computer screen and unplug my opponent's computer!

**Game #4: RavenFamiliar (B/R [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger))**

I'm running twenty lands, four [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song)s, and four [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone)s. *Record: 1-3*

It's time to add more land to this deck. I can't even tell if the deck is good or not, because I can never cast my spells! I remember an important lesson from days gone by. It was back in 1995, and I was playing against my friend Neil. He had a R/G aggro deck, packed with [Kird Ape](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/137/kird-ape)s, [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), Grizzly Bears, Ball Lightnings, and burn. I was running R/G/B land destruction, with [Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain), Ice Storm, Sinkhole, creature removal, Nether Void, and Jade Statues. I beat him in ten straight games, and in each game he basically lost after I killed his first creature or first land. Frustrated, he threw his deck on the table.

“I can't believe it! I have twenty-three mana in this deck and I keep getting mana screwed!”
 “Neil, how many lands are you playing?”
 “Eight – but I have four [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), four [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), five Moxes, a [Black Lotus](https://scryfall.com/card/ced/233/black-lotus), and a [Sol Ring](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/211/sol-ring)”
 “Yeah, but if you don't draw a land, you can't cast your Elves or Birds – plus if I kill them or your lands, you don't have enough left in your deck to cast any of your spells.”
 “But it's twenty-three mana!”

My deck was having the same problems – sure, I had twenty-eight mana sources, for four of them were useless without two lands on the table, and four of them were one-shot spells that couldn't be used to cast half the other spells in my deck. I needed even more land.

**Game #6: 6angashe (B/G Dredge)**

For the first time, my deck gets humming! I get an early [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone), use [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) to set up my early draws, and get everything ready for the kill. I set up the deck perfectly – [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) on top, then [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), then [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) underneath, and land beneath that. All I need to do is draw the Flames, cast it, and I've virtually won…except that I forgot that activating [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) would put it on top of my deck.

You see, in the rush to get to the Flames, I had activated the Top on my own turn, and then I used it to get that Flames on top of my deck. Do you see where this is going? I cast Flames, doing one damage with the Top. I then did four with [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), and drew my [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm). The original plan had been to wait a turn, draw the Flames, reorder the deck, cast Flames, and then win. I got so excited about finally getting the deck to work that I blew this game on multiple levels.

*Record: 1-5*

I logged off ***Magic** Online* to lick my wounds. Not only was the deck not working, but I was thoroughly demoralized. I had a few deck ideas floating around in my head, but I wanted none to work more than this bad boy! Why wasn't the deck working? I slept on that very question, and awoke the next morning with some answers.

1. I didn't have a way to reliably discard. This is very important, because without a way to reliably discard, every large creature I draw is essentially a dead draw. I don't want to end up with [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) in my hand, because I can't cast him at all!
2. I had more reanimation spells than creatures I'd want to reanimate – and no way to tutor for those creatures. Without a card like [Buried Alive](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/273/buried-alive) or Entomb in Standard, I couldn't reliably use one of my reanimation spells. The last thing I'd want to do is cast [Vigor Mortis](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/74/vigor-mortis) on [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp).
3. My land count was a little wonky. I still was having mana problems. My deck needs to get to six mana to really get moving, and most games I was getting stuck at between two and four.
4. Aside from [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top), I couldn't control my draws. My deck is combolicious, but I'm completely at the mercy of top-decking each and every turn.

To solve these problems, I required a radical shift in the deck's design. Unlike my [B/U discard deck](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041212/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb53), I did not want to fundamentally change the nature of this deck. My goals were to:

1. Chuck a gigantic Wurm at my opponent's head,
2. Swing in the red zone with a gigantic Wurm, or
3. Do both.

I did an exhaustive search of all the cards in standard, and found that Black and Red didn't have many efficient self-discard spells. I didn't want to play something wacky like [One with Nothing](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/84/one-with-nothing), because it doesn't help further my game plan, past initially discarding my hand. I needed a card which let me discard while furthering my undying goals of [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames). In the end, I reached a conclusion:

I needed Blue in the deck.

Blue has many self-discarding cards that would fit well in this deck. These include, alphabetically, [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), [Dimir Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/186/dimir-guildmage), [Drowned Rusalka](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/24/drowned-rusalka), [Ideas Unbound](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/40/ideas-unbound), [Lore Broker](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/57/lore-broker), [Sift](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/69/sift), [Sift Through Sands](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/9/sift-through-sands), Soratami Seer, and [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier). I only really needed to discard a couple of cards at most each game – my large guys – so I didn't really need something as drastic as Soratami Seer. Since my deck was going to be triple-colored, I cut any double-Blue spells ([Sift Through Sands](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/9/sift-through-sands), [Ideas Unbound](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/40/ideas-unbound)), and ruled out [Drowned Rusalka](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/24/drowned-rusalka) due to a lack of creatures to sacrifice.

In the end, [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) and [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) seemed ideal for the deck. I didn't want my opponent drawing cards this time around, so [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) is pretty strictly better than [Lore Broker](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/57/lore-broker). Likewise, [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) was a turn faster than [Sift](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/69/sift), which makes a big difference when you're trying to get a combo off as quickly as possible. For that same reason, I didn't even bother with [Dimir Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/186/dimir-guildmage) – I wanted to be setting up for the kill on turn 4, not drawing one card, or making my opponent discard one card.

I had high hopes for the revised version of this deck. I cut all of the double-Black spells that remained in the deck, switched to a mostly-Blue mana base, added in bounce lands to help smooth my mana issues, and changed out [Fellwar Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/347/fellwar-stone) for [Izzet Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/320/izzet-signet). The result? Exploding Wurms! And if you've been following my column, you know this much to be true – when I give a deck a real name, it's a gooder.

**Game #1: EwokOutlaw (W/G Control)**

Oh man, oh man, oh man. Oh man. Oh, man. Man, oh man. Turn 2 [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier), turn 3 Divining Top, turn 4 [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), discarding [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), turn 5 dredge (to get past bad cards on top of my library), turn 6 [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song), [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) for 15, turn 7 [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus) for 8. My dreams have come true – Exploding Wurms! Exploded wurms!

*Record: 1-0 (I'm restarting the record here, because the deck is so changed from the B/R version that it's virtually a different deck.)*

All pistons were firing now. Not only did I win, but my deck performed the way I had imagined it would work. It was a thing of beauty – all the parts came together and formed a single gun, with two bullets, aimed with lethal precision. And this was no fluke.

**Game #2: Dudjiro (B/G Weenie)**

My opening hand had two [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus), but I didn't care – I also had a [Thought Courier](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/104/thought-courier) and a [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research)! Opening with large guys was no longer a mulligan – it was simply an easy choice about which card to discard first.

As it turns out, I get the “B” plan this game – I draw two early [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song)s and cast a fourth turn [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus). It hits him once before he casts [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) – but I simply blow up the Colossus, sending him to six (he had taken two from his own [Elves of Deep Shadow](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/47/elves-of-deep-shadow)). I cast [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) and [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), and he eats six from the top of my deck, courtesy of a second [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames).

*Record: 2-0*

Please don't let it all be just a dream.

**Game #3: Yingyangwizard (R/W Samurai)**

I take a little too much damage before stabilizing, and have to use [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) to kill all his creatures as he plays them. I get to hit him a couple of times with the Flames, but end up dying to [Rain of Embers](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/138/rain-of-embers).

*Record: 2-1*

**Game #4: Smogan (Mono-Black Control)**

I get a triple Stinkweed draw, and he gets [Festering Goblin](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/85/festering-goblin) and [Keening Banshee](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/44/keening-banshee). However, my [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) allows me to control my draws for the entire game – any time I don't like my top three cards, I dredge a dead Imp to get past them. I cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) on turn 6, hitting him with [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus), then another [Bloodfire Colossus](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/62/bloodfire-colossus), and finally with [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm).

*Record: 3-1*

**Game #5: .QDSH (G/W Weenie)**

Nothing doing this game – I couldn't draw any cards to discard the [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm) in my hand, I didn't reach six mana for [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), and I get run down by his creatures.

*Record: 3-2*

**Game #6: Basuta (Mono-Blue lockdown)**

Basuta is on the play, and bounces my first land on his second turn. He then plays [Temporal Adept](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/102/temporal-adept) on turn 3, and things look grim – until I top deck a signet! This allows me to continue to develop my board, as he doesn't have quite enough gas to bounce multiple lands/signets a turn. I eventually draw into multiple Signets, he can't keep up with his land-denial strategy, and he concedes the game, without me ever having cast a non-Signet spell. Just for the record, I would have been able to cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) the turn after he conceded.

*Record: 4-2*

**Game #7: Cianan (U/B mill)**

He gets down a pair of [Dimir Cutpurse](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/49/dimir-cutpurse), and proceeds to tear my hand apart with them, while furthering his draws. However, I manage to keep afloat thanks to [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), and cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) as the last card in my hand. I kill a Cutpurse (to keep from losing a damage race), and then he drops Kokusho with me at 10 life. It's time to race. I flip up [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify), sending him to sixteen life, and then get [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm), bringing him to one the turn before I die.

*Record: 4-3.*

If I had hit a Colossus or an [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) or another Wurm on my first Flames, I would have won this one. Even though I'm batting around .500 right now, It's been close in the games I've lost. Thanks to [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames) and Wurm, I can completely change the tide of the game in a single spell.

**Game #8: Punjoke ([Thief of Hope](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/100/thief-of-hope)/Arcane)**

He gets [Thief of Hope](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/100/thief-of-hope), casts [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) splicing [Kodama's Might](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/224/kodamas-might) (5 damage), then casts [Unchecked Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/148/unchecked-growth) splicing [Kodama's Might](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/224/kodamas-might) with a hard-cast of [Kodama's Might](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/224/kodamas-might), bringing me down to three life. I cast [Undying Flames](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/119/undying-flames), killing his [Thief of Hope](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/100/thief-of-hope) – and I follow it with two [Autochthon Wurm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/191/autochthon-wurm)s to his head, ending the game almost immediately.

*Record: 5-3*

It's time to address the weakness this deck is showing – I am vulnerable to an early weenie rush. Against the Samurai, Spiritcraft and U/B mill deck, I lost to a bunch of 1/1 and 2/2 guys. My deck is very capable of killing on the 6th or 7th turn, so all I need to do is buy a turn or two in the middle game, and I'm good to go. [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm) seems like a perfect fit here, so I add it in, taking out [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song). The Songs were good for getting a god draw, but the deck has proven consistent enough with the addition of Blue that I don't really need explosive anymore – unless, of course, explosive involves a wurm being removed from the game.
