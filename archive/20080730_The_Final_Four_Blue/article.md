Hello, and welcome back to [Building on a Budget](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041413/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/columnarchive)! This is the column dedicated to building decks that cost 30 tickets or less using **Magic Online**. I'm Ben Bleiweiss, and this is part two of my series in which I pick a color (in this case, blue), consult my handy-dandy list of rares that cost half-a-ticket or less using **Magic Online** prices, randomly put 35 of them in a deck, and playtest it to see what fits, and what does not. Before we get to this week's deck, I have an announcement to make.

Many have speculated about why I've titled this series "The Final X" (last installment was five - this installment is four). And I'm here with the truth - I'm pregnant. That's right, just like [Arnold Schwarzenegger in Junior](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041413/http://ww2.wizards.com/Site/LeavingWizards.aspx?url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0110216/&origin=), I've inseminated myself with an experimental process that will make me the first man to give birth! How else would you explain my gut?

What's that?

Oh, I'm not eating healthy. Frown!

Seriously though, these are my final five columns - I will be hanging up my Building on a Budget quills to concentrate on my Insider Trading column at StarCityGames.com. It's a project that I've wanted to work on for a long time - a column dedicated to discussing the financials and values of **Magic** cards. I've spent six years working with this end of the industry (I am the General Manager at StarCityGames.com, and am responsible for pricing all of the cards on that website), so I hope that I can shed some insight into a part of the industry that isn't really discussed much!

Yes yes, I will be sad to leave, and yes yes, I will miss you all. I cry myself a river, and that's not just a clever segue to this week's deck evolution. Ok, it is a clever segue. Here's the list of the blue random rares that are currently available for Extended-format use in **Magic Online**:

An interesting mix of cards!

**Game 1: rocketeer88 (Bears!)**

[![Leyline of Singularity](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/gpt/29?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) 
*Singular indeed.*

Rocketeer88 starts with Grizzly Bears, [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth), Bearscape and [Coat of Arms](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/58/coat-of-arms). I start with [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) in play, and proceed to use [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment) to kill the [Overgrowth](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/157/overgrowth) (thanks to the Leyline). I drop a morph (which is [Maelstrom Djinn](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/39/maelstrom-djinn)), and my opponent casts [Nourish](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/78/nourish), and makes a bear at end of turn. An [Alpha Status](https://scryfall.com/card/scg/110/alpha-status) on Grizzly Bears lets it swing as a 5/5 - but I block and morph with my Djinn.

Surprise!

But rocketeer casts Giant Growth! Thankfully, I have [Deflection](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/74/deflection) and make my Djinn an 8/9, saving it. I then drop [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/23/cytoplast-manipulator) and [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) (which can kill any creature an opponent drops, thanks to the Leyline), winning me the game.

Record: 1-0

Keys to the game: [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity). Man, the wheels are turning here - blue has a lot of strengths as a color: card drawing, flying creatures, stealing, and, of course, copying things. What happens when copying goes from getting equal time to what an opponent has to playing massive creature removal? That'd be a question for a [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) deck, and an intriguing one at that!

**Game 2: PrincessScratchyBeard (Black-Blue-Green Mill & Vulture)**

[![Vulturous Zombie](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/cm2/170?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie) 
*(Flying) Brrraaaiiiinnnssss...*

I seem to have all the answers in this long, drawn-out game. I answer a second-turn sacrificed [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) with [Shadow of Doubt](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/253/shadow-of-doubt). PrincessScratchyBeard has out an [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island), two [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest)s and a [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp) when I drop my fifth land and cast [Planar Overlay](https://scryfall.com/card/pls/28/planar-overlay), sending three lands back to their hand. I then drop [Dire Undercurrents](https://scryfall.com/card/shm/159/dire-undercurrents), [Thought Reflection](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/72/thought-reflection) and [Delusions of Mediocrity](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/70/delusions-of-mediocrity), and start throwing any random creature I draw to the board - many of which eat [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) or [Putrefy](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/131/putrefy). My opponent finally sticks a [Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie), and then casts Haunting Echoes on me - which is virtually useless except for the Mists of Stagnation in my hand. I drop Ixidor and [Maelstrom Djinn](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/39/maelstrom-djinn), chump block, and drop [Tidal Kraken](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/105/tidal-kraken) and [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth) to the board. ScratchyBeard drops a second [Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie), and I [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment) the [Delusions of Mediocrity](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/70/delusions-of-mediocrity) so that I don't die in a single swing. My opponent then casts Traumatize, but I have [Breaking Wave](https://scryfall.com/card/inv/48/breaking-wave) to tap down both of the opposing [Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie)s, and then I swing with my team of monsters to win the game!

Record: 2-0

Keys to the game: card advantage, life gain. I drew about double the number of cards that PrincessScratchyBeard drew, which let me have just what I needed at several points in the game. Moreover, the [Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie)s grew huge early on, but my large army of creatures kept my opponent from being able to attack without being counterattacked for lethal, in part aided by my (eventual) doubling of [Delusions of Mediocrity](https://scryfall.com/card/7ed/70/delusions-of-mediocrity). Blue isn't known for life gain, but that 20 life kept me from losing.

**Game 3: makoreactor (Elfball)**

My hand is Arcanis, [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans), [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth), and [Magus of the Future](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/75/magus-of-the-future). Makoreactor plays [Heritage Druid](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/171/heritage-druid), [Elvish Harbinger](https://scryfall.com/card/evg/4/elvish-harbinger), [Wirewood Channeler](https://scryfall.com/card/khc/79/wirewood-channeler), [Wirewood Lodge](https://scryfall.com/card/evg/27/wirewood-lodge), Oracle of Nectars and [Mirror Entity](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/83/mirror-entity) by turn four, and swings in with multiple large creatures, drawing my concession.

Record: 2-1

Keys to the game: Every card in my hand was either useless on its own ([Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans)) or cost too much to mount an early defense. I was quickly run over by weenie creatures without any chance to make a defense.

**Game 4: gob of spit (Yore-Tiller Nephilim)**

I was facing a turn-four Yore-Tiller Nephilim, aided by a Signet. I get down [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/23/cytoplast-manipulator), and then gob evokes Faultgrinder, killing two of my lands (one on the evoke, and one on returning it to play with the Nephilim). I graft on the second attempt, and wait for my opponent to untap to try to steal it. A [Momentary Blink](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/15/momentary-blink) targeting the 4/4 takes out a third land of mine. I cast a morphed Djinn which is removed with [Faceless Butcher](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/84/faceless-butcher). That merits my concession.

Record: 2-2

Keys to the game: Same as the game before - everything costs too much, and there's not enough of a focus in my deck. Time to make the first round of cuts!

[![Hunted Phantasm](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rav/55?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm) 
*When is a downside an upside?*

I was really enamored with the idea of a [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) deck - and while I didn't immediately want to go straight for Hunting Phantasm (a typical partner of this particular Leyline), I wasn't averse to ending up in that situation. To begin though, I wanted to bring in a lot of cards that would make my spells double as removal. To this effect, I added in three Leylines of Singularity, three [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer)s (which could become any other creature on the fly!), and four [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone)s.

I also wanted to toy around with [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans) in this deck - with [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) out, two [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans) equals six cards (since they kill each other). While these wouldn't be fantastic odds in a sixty-card deck on their own, I also added in three copies of [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment), so that I'd effectively have more copies of [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans) once a Leyline was in play.

The cards I took out were ones that didn't really fit this theme, or that overall weren't good utility blue monsters.

**In:** 3x [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), 4x [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), 3x [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment), 3x [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans), 3x [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer)
**Out:** [Breaking Wave](https://scryfall.com/card/inv/48/breaking-wave), [Crystal Spray](https://scryfall.com/card/inv/50/crystal-spray), [Govern the Guildless](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/25/govern-the-guildless), [Grozoth](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/53/grozoth), Ixidor Reality Sculptor, [Mind Bend](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/93/mind-bend), Mists of Stagnation, [Moonlace](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/68/moonlace), [Myojin of Seeing Winds](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/75/myojin-of-seeing-winds), [The Unspeakable](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/13/the-unspeakable), [Sprite Noble](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/73/sprite-noble), [Temporal Cascade](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/52/temporal-cascade), [Trade Secrets](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/64/trade-secrets), [Tidal Kraken](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/105/tidal-kraken), [Denizen of the Deep](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/47/denizen-of-the-deep), [Novijen Sages](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/53/novijen-sages)

**Game 5: b3koo4 (2003 *Mirrodin* Block Mono-Red Burn)**

I was hit with a turn-two [Magma Jet](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/346/magma-jet), [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song) into [Arc-Slogger](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/85/arc-slogger) on turn three, [Molten Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/103/molten-rain) on me turn four, and then got slogged four times with a Flamebreak on the untap to win.

**Game 6: grubbaboom ([Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit))**

I get down [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) to start the game. Grubbaboom starts with [Safehold Elite](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/220/safehold-elite) and Glen Elendra Liege. I get [Riptide Mangler](https://scryfall.com/card/lgn/51/riptide-mangler) and [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), which eats a Torture. I then put down [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/23/cytoplast-manipulator), which grabs Mephidross Vampire. My opponent's attempt to play [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit) is hit with my [Disrupting Shoal](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/51/disrupting-shoal). Grubba then drops another persister, and I play [Maelstrom Djinn](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/39/maelstrom-djinn) face down, only to turn it face up in time to eat the Glen Elendra Liege. This makes the Djinn 6/7 and allows me to fly over quickly for the win.

Record: 3-3

Keys to the game: [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), which wisely died so that it couldn't take out any creature at random. [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/23/cytoplast-manipulator), because it's awwwwwfully good against a deck that wants to play around with +1/+1 counters to begin with.

**Game 7: stixdww (White Weenie Equipment)**

I start with [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) in play, and drop a second turn [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer). Stixdww drops [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves) and Leonin Shikari, making it so that I can never successfully target anything on my opponent's board! I then get stuck at two lands anyhow, and end up losing the game pretty quickly to a cacophony of creatures and equipment ([Leonin Skyhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/7/leonin-skyhunter), [Sword of Light and Shadow](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/298/sword-of-light-and-shadow), and so on and so forth).

Record: 3-4

[![Clone](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/m14/47?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) 
*Clone doesn't target.*

Keys to the game: [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves) plus Leonin Shikari. That just shuts down any sort of targeting spell, and this entire deck is based around being able to target. The only out I had was [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), since [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) does not target, but unfortunately I couldn't get to four mana. Still - important to note - [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) doesn't target. Good to know, in case this comes up again.

**Game 8: SpookiQ ([Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit))**

SpookiQ is playing a deck based around getting counters off of [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths), using cards like Aether Snap and [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit). I drop an early [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) and two [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans), while Spooki gets a ton of mana acceleration ([Into the North](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/111/into-the-north), [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach)es for all!) along with [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths). I draw and play [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), drawing me six cards. I follow it with Arcanis, and SpookiQ pops the [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths). I kill the Marit Lage token with my [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), and SpookiQ returns [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths) with Petrified Field and plays it immediately with Aether Snap. I drop another [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) and kill it again. My opponent plays a third one, and I drop [Riptide Mangler](https://scryfall.com/card/lgn/51/riptide-mangler) and Aboshan. When the [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/244/dark-depths) is popped a third time, I make my Mangler 20/3, copy it, and then drop a third [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) and kill Marit Lage again. SpookiQ casts [Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace) on my attack, and then drops a fourth Marit Lage. I kill this one with [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone), swing through, and kill my opponent a turn later.

Record: 4-4

It's a squid! It's an octopus! It's... *gurgle, munch, gulp*

Keys to the game: [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), because man, there's not many other ways that I can deal with a 20/20 indestructible creature other than by killing it as a state-based effect thanks to the legends rule.

Ok, time for some more cuts - I'm ready to get in [Hunted Phantasm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm), and I want some early board reaction. [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth) is a fine card to bring in with the Phantasm, because I'm going to necessarily have [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) in my opening hand every game. It's a great bounce spell that has a lot of utility against token creatures, so I'm happy to add it. I also bring in a quartet of [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) - yes, the original [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell). I don't want this to be a counter-heavy deck, but I think it's fair to play one playset of [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) as a way to stop the most devastating of spells from resolving. Think of this as adding [Terror](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/103/terror) in black, [Incinerate](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/51/incinerate) in red, [Disenchant](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/572/disenchant) in white, and [Krosan Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/198/krosan-grip) in green - it's just a universal spell that plays to the strengths of the color against the weakness your deck might have.

**Game 9: Cashmere (Blue-Green Elves/Swords)**

I start with [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) in play while Cashmere leads with [Silhana Ledgewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/206/silhana-ledgewalker). When there's an attempt to ninjutsu out [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours) on turn three, I use [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth) to set my opponent's board back to three lands and nothing else. I then [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) the re-played [Silhana Ledgewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/206/silhana-ledgewalker) which lets me play [Magus of the Future](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/75/magus-of-the-future) on turn five without interruption. Cashmere replays the Ninja, and then drops [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice) the following turn, making it a 4/4 protection-from-my deck powerhouse! I keep playing lands and get double [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans) to move my deck along, allowing me to drop [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer). My opponent meanwhile drops [Sword of Light and Shadow](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/298/sword-of-light-and-shadow), and attacks me to 4. I get [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth), remove the [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice) from the board, and [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) the Ninja, clearing my adversary's board again.

Cashmere drops [Wellwisher](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/166/wellwisher) and [Silhana Ledgewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/206/silhana-ledgewalker), but I draw and play [Aboshan, Cephalid Emperor](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/58/aboshan-cephalid-emperor) with nine mana out, allowing me to use his UUU ability to tap down all of my opponent's attackers. I drop [Hunted Phantasm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm) and a second [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), allowing me to start playing multiple [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer)s to the board. Since they count as Cephalids, I am able to start tapping down Cashmere's lands and creatures with Aboshan, and then copy [Hunted Phantasm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm) to make multiple unblockable 4/6 beaters. Cashmere delays the inevitable with [Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace), but eventually I lock it down and go on to win the game.

Record: 5-4

Keys to the game: [Aboshan, Cephalid Emperor](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/58/aboshan-cephalid-emperor). Man, this guy was a house! With [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), I was able to tap all my opponent's blue mana during their upkeep, effectively denying Cashmere a color of mana - and then I was able to tap UUU to keep all my opponent's creatures from attacking. However, this deck doesn't have a ton of threats, and because of [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), I want to keep them varied - I'd rather draw a [Magus of the Future](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/75/magus-of-the-future) and an Aboshan in a game rather than two Aboshans (never mind that he's already legendary on his own).

**Game 10: Linnear (White-Black Cycling)**

[![Shapesharer](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/lrw/85?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) 
*A sharer of many shapes.*

Linnear starts by cycling a ton of cards, like [Secluded Steppe](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/177/secluded-steppe), [Barren Moor](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/262/barren-moor), and Renewed Faith. I drop two [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer)s, and start swinging. An attempt from my opponent to kill them by cycling [Decree of Pain](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/136/decree-of-pain) a few turns in is nullified when I save them both with [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth). I re-play a [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), and Linnear then drops [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem) and [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum), which I [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) and chump block with. After that my adversary drops Silver Seraph, and I drop [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), [Clone](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/47/clone) the [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum) (killing them both) and [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) the Silver Seraph. The next challenge comes in the form of a [Death Match](https://scryfall.com/card/ons/136/death-match) and [Phyrexian Arena](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/180/phyrexian-arena), and I use [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment) to kill the Match, allowing me to safely drop [Hunted Phantasm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm).

I start swinging for six a turn (one from the [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), four from Phantasm, and one from my opponent's own [Arena](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/117/arena)), but decide I'd rather not worry about Linnear drawing two cards a turn after an attempt to hard-cast [Decree of Pain](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/136/decree-of-pain) (which I [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell)), so I [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment) the [Arena](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/117/arena), killing it. My opponent tries to cast [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters) on my Phantasm, but I [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth) my 4/6 and replay it the following turn. Linnear then kills my Leyline with [Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp), and attempts to cycle Decree of Justice for six - but I have another [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth), returning all the tokens, and allowing me to swing for 8 (now that I can safely copy my own [Hunted Phantasm](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/55/hunted-phantasm)). I [Counterspell](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/114/counterspell) another Decree, allowing Linnear to hard-cast Renewed Faith to stay alive. My opponent draws and plays [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters) on my Phantasm, netting some breathing room by boosting their life total to 5. I copy the Phantasm with my [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer), and then copy Linnear's [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem) with another [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment). This allows me to swing for the last 5, and Linnear reveals a hand of double [Raise the Alarm](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/41/raise-the-alarm) and a second Decree of Justice.

Record: 6-4

Keys to the game: [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity), which is a house against any deck running a token-generation strategy. As long as I had Leyline out, my opponent couldn't effectively cast [Raise the Alarm](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/41/raise-the-alarm) (the tokens would kill each other), Decree of Justice (for more than one Angel or one Soldier), and it allowed me to turn all of my [Copy Enchantment](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/39/copy-enchantment)s into removal spells, which was key against an enchantment-heavy deck such as Linnear's.

So from the humble beginnings of a blue deck of random rares, [Leyline of Singularity](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/29/leyline-of-singularity) emerged with [Shapesharer](https://scryfall.com/card/lrw/85/shapesharer) to form a deck based around killing your opponent's board with the sincerest form of flattery - imitation.
