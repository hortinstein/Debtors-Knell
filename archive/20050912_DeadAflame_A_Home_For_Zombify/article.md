# Dead/Aflame: A Home For Zombify

**Author:** Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar  
**Date:** September 12, 2005  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm117>

---

Lots to do today, lots to do. The problem with giving me a week off because of a U.S. holiday is that I have twice as much time to play and write.

Before I start, I'd like to thank all of those who offered congratulations and well-wishes for the birth of my daughter. I had no idea there were so many fathers-of-two-children out there in **Magic** land. Your Message Boards posts, and especially your heart-felt e-mails, were great. Many thanks.

Now, onto another deckbuilding experiment!

As I said [two weeks ago](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042228/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm115), my plan this time around is to work with the *9th Edition* preconstructed decks (*Ravnica, City of Guilds* won't be available online for some time). The problem is that Core Set precons are only forty cards, so I've decided to choose two different precons, mash them together, then start building from there. To be more specific, here are the Guidelines over the next three weeks:

1. Start with two different *9th Edition* preconstructed decks, combined, to make an eighty-card deck.
2. Cull cards until I have a sixty-card deck.
3. Don't make more changes until playing the deck in at least five games.
4. Change no more than five cards at a time.
5. Build a respectable deck that's fun to play.
6. Build an affordable deck.

The deck I'm building this time around will be Standard-legal, meaning it will draw on not only *9th Edition*, but *Mirrodin* and *Kamigawa* blocks as well. I know I've made a couple of *Kamigawa* Block decks recently, but not only has that format wound down ([Blood Clock](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/153/blood-clock) pun intended), but I can't wait to get my hands on the new Standard. My decks in this column still aren't meant to be tournament-worthy, but Standard is my favorite format in which to build decks because of its dynamic nature and its relative accessibility for new players. I may stray to other formats frequently, but Standard remains my creative home.

Remember that the goal of these experiments is the journey much more than the destination. You won't always agree with my card choices or the direction I take the deck. That's okay--expected, even--and I hope more than anything that the ideas here will inspire you to build your own decks. If you post those decks on the Message Boards over the next three weeks, all the better.

Before I get into culling cards, I'll first just say what I generally do and don't like about this big cobbled-together deck.

First, what I like:

- There are lots of spiffy-cool creatures here. I particularly like [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger), [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber), [Anarchist](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/173/anarchist), [Dancing Scimitar](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/292/dancing-scimitar), [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore), [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal), and [Phyrexian Gargantua](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/178/phyrexian-gargantua). I'm not sure if all of these creatures will stick around to the final version, but at least they all make me smile.
- As you know by now, I love reanimation themes, so both decks (creature reanimation in Black, sorcery reanimation in Red) make me happy.
- The rares are all cool and are all interesting foundations to a deck. The problem is that I'm not sure they can all cohabitate, so don't expect more than one or two to make it through three weeks of cuts.
- One neat interaction is between [Raise Dead](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/18/raise-dead) and [Anarchist](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/173/anarchist), or maybe [Raise Dead](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/18/raise-dead)-[Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger)-[Anarchist](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/173/anarchist). That's a fun little chain of spells.
- Black/Red! Evil! EVIL! EEEEVVVVIIIIIIIL!!!!!

The list of what I don't like is a bit longer, but certainly not damning:

- This deck's got next to nothing going on in the early game. [Raise Dead](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/18/raise-dead) and [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength) cost one mana, sure, but they aren't spells to cast in the first turn. My creatures start around Turn 3 and really get going by Turn 4. That's slow.
- Speaking of which, no mana acceleration in such a slow deck sucks zombie toes.
- I can't see pushing the land destruction ([Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain), [Demolish](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/106/demolish), [Wildfire](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/134/wildfire)) theme in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*, where I play my games. I'm not one of those people who thinks LD isn't a “casual” strategy, but I know a lot of people do and will concede immediately once they see the first [Stone Rain](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/221/stone-rain). One day I'm sure to try an LD deck in this column, but not this time and not without [some cool twist to it](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042228/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/147).
- The deck doesn't look like it can decide whether to be aggressive or not. [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength) is fast, but as I said it has no creature to enchant early. [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) and [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior) are symbols of aggression. [Dancing Scimitar](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/292/dancing-scimitar) and lifegain are symbols of control. Weird.
- Some of the cards look horribly out of place when combining the two decks. For example, [Demon's Horn](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/57/demons-horn), [Dragon's Claw](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/206/dragons-claw), and [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare) look pretty weird in a two-color deck. [Underworld Dreams](https://scryfall.com/card/thb/121/underworld-dreams) looks nigh uncastable with no mana-fixers. Etc. Etc. Etc.
- Likewise, some of the cards from the two decks don't like each other. Take [Wildfire](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/134/wildfire), which helps to limit land while [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit) (and [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze), for that matter) wants to hoard it.
- Having so many double-mana Black cards like [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) and [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) alongside double-mana Red cards like [Balduvian Barbarians](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/174/balduvian-barbarians) and [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) makes me cringe. This seems to be more of a problem on the Black end than the Red end, though.
- Of course, consistency (all those one-of and two-ofs) and card quality (yay [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)! boo [Tanglebloom](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/311/tanglebloom)!) are always an issue in precons and even worse when you mash two unrelated precons together.
- Sorta looks like two different decks mashed together, doesn't it? It's going to take some work to get this acting like a focused, cohesive deck.

So those are some initial observations. Now the task is to start cutting to drop twenty cards from the decklist. I won't give as full an explanation here as I will once I start playing the deck, but I will let you know why each card will be sitting on the sidelines once I start.

I'll tackle the easy stuff first:

*OUT*: 5 [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain)

*OUT*: 5 [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)

Twenty-four land is the right initial number for a new sixty-card deck, to lower or raise as my games dictate. The thing to note here is that I'm deciding to keep the colors balanced right out of the gate, and that means I should drop roughly the same number of Black and Red cards at first.

*OUT*: 1 [Demon's Horn](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/57/demons-horn)

*OUT*: 1 [Dragon's Claw](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/206/dragons-claw)

It's sort of a neat idea to have each of these artifacts in the Core Set precon decks. Sort of. Lifegain on its own is generally a bad strategy unless it's complementing other cards in the deck. Dead Again can use the life more than World Aflame, but in both cases they probably don't make the cut in an improved deck and they certainly don't make sense in a two-color deck.

*OUT*: 1 [Tanglebloom](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/311/tanglebloom)

*OUT*: 1 [Contaminated Bond](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/132/contaminated-bond)

[![Contaminated Bond](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/10e/132?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/132/contaminated-bond)

These two get dropped for basic power reasons. Even if I was just evolving the Dead Again precon, these two would be in the first round of cuts. [Demon's Horn](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/57/demons-horn) is superior to [Tanglebloom](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/311/tanglebloom) in almost every way, and it's already gone. The deck has really nothing to do with Auras, and something like [Dark Banishing](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/123/dark-banishing) or [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit) would be strictly superior and better used in [Contaminated Bond](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/132/contaminated-bond)'s place.

That's the easy stuff. Here are some conscious choices I'm making apart from land and power level on the direction of the deck.

*OUT*: 1 [Panic Attack](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/150/panic-attack)

Something I could do with this deck is focus on a fast, hard-hitting aggressive deck that tries to kill you before you can blink. These sorts of decks can be fun, but I'm more interested right now in [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) and reanimation. As a result, [Panic Attack](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/150/panic-attack) just doesn't fit.

*OUT*: 2 [Hollow Dogs](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/139/hollow-dogs)

Honestly, when I first saw the Dead Again decklist I thought these were Vampire Hounds and I rejoiced. Turns out I was wrong, which is too bad since Vampire Hounds fits a reanimation deck really well whereas [Hollow Dogs](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/139/hollow-dogs) are just expensive and slow. The deck already has a serious manacurve problem, and I should be able to do a lot better than a 3/3 for five mana.

*OUT*: 2 [Lava Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/150/lava-axe)

A lot of people--especially newer players--just love [Lava Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/150/lava-axe). Five damage just feels so powerful and sexy. Besides, who doesn't want to yell “Catch!” in a game for the win? [Lava Axe](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/150/lava-axe) pretty much only fits in to-your-face burn decks, though, and that's not the deck I'm building.

*OUT*: 1 [Underworld Dreams](https://scryfall.com/card/thb/121/underworld-dreams)

Like I said, {B}{B}{B} just seems silly in a two-color deck with no [Sulfurous Springs](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/185/sulfurous-springs) or other dual-color lands. Besides, the deck doesn't have anything like [Teferi's Puzzle Box](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/312/teferis-puzzle-box) to take full advantage of it. I'm not making a Puzzle Box deck today, either.

Notice that those last five cards help one of my chief concerns about the deck: Mana. I still have no early game, but at least I won't die with a bunch of uncastable cards in my hand. [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare), [Wildfire](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/134/wildfire), and [Phyrexian Gargantua](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/178/phyrexian-gargantua) are still looming at six mana, but they're all too cool to drop just yet.

Normally, when I'm dealing with a preconstructed deck, I first spend some time walking through the deck's major and minor themes, its mana situation, consistency and power issues, and an overall fun quotient. I think I've hit on a lot of these issues with my commentary already, and I would rather spend my remaining time today playing the deck than obsessing about first impressions. Here are a few general comments that give you an idea where my head is at and may help explain some of the early changes I make to the deck:

- [![Magnivore](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/9ed/202?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) In terms of themes, I'm most attracted to the reanimation aspects of the deck and [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) Sorcery-shenanigans. I'm not sure if I can maximize both in the same deck but I'm willing to try until something better comes along. The trick will be keeping the deck distinct from [Ratimation](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042228/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm103).
- I'm still a little tired of straightforward beatdown decks, so expect me to neuter the hyper-aggressive cards like [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength) and [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) pretty quickly. We can do better than basic Red/Black aggro. As I said, you should also expect the land destruction parts to fall away sooner rather than later.
- As I've said, one of my chief concerns is the deck's manacurve. It's currently a very ugly deck, with few cards to play early and too many to play late, with no mana-acceleration to help out along the way.
- As with every precon evolution, expect me to drop single copies of cards in favor of more consistent three- and four-copies.

Enough preamble for you? Let's play this flamin' dead puppy and see what's what!

##### Playing Dead/Aflame

I play my games in ***Magic** Online* in the Casual Decks room under the name BuildingOnABudget. I don't seem to have regular days or times I'm online (especially these days with the new baby), so just keep a lookout and you're sure to find me. Please don't challenge me, though, as I like to keep my early games a little random by sitting down to whatever table happens to be open.

Of course, that strategy didn't serve me so well this time around...

*Game 1: Monogreen Elves*

[![Blanchwood Armor](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/fdn/213?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor)

Remember how I said the deck was too slow with no early game? Imagine how thrilled I was, then, to see my opponent's first four turns of Norwood Ranger, Elvish Warrior, [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor) on the Warrior, [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor) on the Ranger. I died with more land, [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore), and [Demolish](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/106/demolish) in my hand. Ouch.

*Game 2: Black/Green Aggro*

Remember how I said the deck was too slow with no early game? Imagine how thrilled I was, then, to see my opponent got a Dross Golem enchanted with [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength) and [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor), then two 5/5 One Dozen Eyes tokens, one with [Blanchwood Armor](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/213/blanchwood-armor) on it. Anyway, I was stuck on two [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)s for four turns, able to muster only [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) and [Drudge Skeletons](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/36/drudge-skeletons). By the time I finally found a [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain), it was way too late. I ended the game with seven cards in hand, all costing four or more mana.

*Game 3: Blue/White [Teferi's Puzzle Box](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/312/teferis-puzzle-box) deck*

His deck was interesting, using the Puzzle Box plus lots of bounce like Echoing Truth and [Evacuation](https://scryfall.com/card/lcc/156/evacuation). Not that he really needed it, though. Remember how I said the deck was too slow with no early game? I kept a hand with two land and five spells of four or more mana. I then didn't see another land for four turns. Eventually I made a game of it, but a single [Air Elemental](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/44/air-elemental) beat me over the head five turns in a row.

*Game 4: Monoblack Control*

[![Distress](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/m12/94?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/94/distress)

This game was slightly more promising, only because I drew enough land and could cast things. He [Distress](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/94/distress)ed a [Volcanic Hammer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/226/volcanic-hammer) out of my hand, and I used a second one to kill his [Ravenous Rats](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats). I played [Anarchist](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/173/anarchist) to get back a [Volcanic Hammer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/226/volcanic-hammer), then [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger) to retrieve the [Anarchist](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/173/anarchist) once it died. Booya! My opponent played all four of his [Distress](https://scryfall.com/card/m12/94/distress)es, along with a bunch of creature removal. Eventually we hit a place where he was at six life and I was at nineteen, with my hand empty and having just cast [Hill Giant](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/212/hill-giant). He used a [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit) for five on the Giant, then played an 8/8 [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare). I cast [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber) to drop him to nine and he hit me to thirteen. He then cast a second [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare), I drew [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior), and that was game.

*Game 5: White Weenie*

Remember how I said the deck was too slow with no early game? Imagine how thrilled I was, then, to see my opponent play [Lantern Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/32/lantern-kami), [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth), [Suntail Hawk](https://scryfall.com/card/m14/40/suntail-hawk), [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte), and two Grafted Wargear. Let's not kid ourselves, though: It was the Jitte that killed me. I blasted the first Kami with a [Volcanic Hammer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/226/volcanic-hammer), and attacked for a while with [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp). Once the Jitte became active, I was dead. I drew eleven land, which made a [Wildfire](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/134/wildfire) sort of entertaining the turn before I died.

0-5. Well that was just humiliating.

The mana issues are worse than I thought. The deck needs some early plays, and it needs them right away.

*OUT*: 2 [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior)

[![Serpent Warrior](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/8ed/161?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior)

If this were a hyper-aggressive deck, [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior) would still get cut. Compare it to Raving Oni-Slave or [Takenuma Bleeder](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/86/takenuma-bleeder), two creatures who are similar in effect but are both going to fit in Black aggro decks before [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior) gets the nod. Since I'm deciding to focus on reanimation and Sorceries, [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior) helps me not at all. In fact, it's sort of an anti-reanimation creature since its comes-into-play cost is not something I want to pay more than once.

*OUT*: 1 [Deathgazer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/124/deathgazer)

It's not that [Deathgazer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/124/deathgazer) is a bad creature per se. I remember playing an evening with the 8th Edition precons in which [Deathgazer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/124/deathgazer) made the difference for me in game after game. No, the problem is how much [Deathgazer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/124/deathgazer) suffers by comparison with the other four-mana creatures in the deck. I want more copies of [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal), [Highway Robber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddm/61/highway-robber), [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore), or [Gravedigger](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/103/gravedigger) before I want more [Deathgazer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/124/deathgazer)s. So out it goes to make room for something cheaper.

*OUT*: 1 [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength)

As I keep saying, I'm not building a really aggressive deck this time around. As a result, [Unholy Strength](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/47/unholy-strength) is one of those “nice to have” cards instead of a “need to have.” I probably wouldn't mind drawing or casting it during games, but it's not part of my central strategy. If this deck is going to be focused, it needs to drop cards that aren't part of its two central themes: Reanimation and Sorceries.

*OUT*: 1 [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare)

[![Ravenous Rats](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/gvl/37?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/37/ravenous-rats)

I'm pretty sure I intended to drop [Nightmare](https://scryfall.com/card/w17/17/nightmare) eventually, but certainly not this early. A six-mana creature that is only half as effective as it was in Dead Again, though, is not going to cut it during this emergency stage of the deck. Good bye, big horsie, and see you in another deck at another time. A Monoblack deck. With lots of [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)s.

*OUT*: 3 [Balduvian Barbarians](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/174/balduvian-barbarians)

With [Balduvian Barbarians](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/174/balduvian-barbarians), though, it's the {R}{R} mana that drives me insane. I almost never seem to have two [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain)s on the third turn when I am holding a Barbarians, and waiting until the fourth or fifth turn to cast a vanilla 3/2 seems ridiculous. I think [Balduvian Barbarians](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/174/balduvian-barbarians) is best off in a theme-based deck, but I'm not sure it otherwise has a lot of Constructed applications with things like [Ronin Houndmaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/184/ronin-houndmaster), Viashino Sandstalker, Vulshok Sorcerer, [Zo-Zu the Punisher](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/41/zo-zu-the-punisher), and [Oxidda Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/46/oxidda-golem) around.

*OUT*: 2 [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp)

[Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) has been a good attacker and a good blocker so far, but it absolutely doesn't fit the deck I'm making. I'm slowly making the deck less aggressive and focusing on two major themes: Reanimation and Sorceries. [Foul Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/ddr/49/foul-imp) doesn't help the Sorcery theme at all and is terrible in a reanimation deck. The double-black mana on a creature that should come out on Turn 2 is just added incentive to drop it.

Like I said: Nothing against [Hill Giant](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/212/hill-giant). He's a fine critter. He's just not someone I'd ever put on an All Star team.

*OUT*: 1 [Dancing Scimitar](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/292/dancing-scimitar)

Dropping [Dancing Scimitar](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/292/dancing-scimitar) makes me sad. It's one of those creatures that I'm nigh giddy to use effectively in a deck. I have to face facts, though, and realize that it's just out of place in the way my deck is shaping up. Before now it's been valuable because I could always cast it and because it gave me an extremely important defensive creature while my slow deck wound up. Now the deck isn't nearly as slow, and it can often handle threats by outright killing them rather than slowing them down. Dropping the last artifact from the deck also means any [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize)-like effects in my opponent's deck are now useless.

*OUT*: 1 [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit)

Twice I've looked at [Consume Spirit](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/56/consume-spirit) in my hand with one [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp) on the table. [Blaze](https://scryfall.com/card/bbd/167/blaze) or [Fireball](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/175/fireball), while not offering the lifegain, would be a significant upgrade in my two-color deck. Pain Kami might even be better, given my deck's reanimation theme. Whatever the case, this is a must-have for Monoblack decks of all kinds, but in my deck has only been useful about once in fifteen games.

*IN*: 2 [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)

[![Nekrataal](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/ema/99?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal)

I don't like the double-black cost, and [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) suffers in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*, which seems to be flooded with Black decks. Still, I can't imagine making a Black reanimation deck without including this skinny dude. The great thing about [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) is its tendency to give me a two-for-one trade with an opponent, killing a creature and using an opposing card to kill it. With reanimation, those two-for-ones add up pretty quickly. I've also been impressed with how often first strike is relevant. A [Nekrataal](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/99/nekrataal) twosome can face down a [Kodama of the North Tree](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/222/kodama-of-the-north-tree) without blinking, for example.

*IN*: 1 [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore)

I'm enamored enough with the Sorcery theme of the deck that I am willing to add a second [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) to the mix. I still think it's slightly tricky to work with reanimation--which requires lots of creatures--and [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore)--which requires lots of sorceries. The good news is that I'm planning to up the total number of Sorceries in the next few rounds of changes, which should make [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) a more significant (and hopefully game-ending) threat. The fact that it has haste works really well with reanimation cards. Cards exactly like...

*IN*: 2 [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify)

If [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) ever had a home, it is in a deck with both reanimation and Sorcery-based strategies. I can see using [Zombify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/187/zombify) to bring back almost every single creature in the deck now given different game situations. As such, I imagine I'll always be happy to draw this card.

I'm pretty ambivalent about adding two {B}{B} cards and one {R}{R} card after all of my hand-wringing over mana. This deck wants [Sulfurous Springs](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/185/sulfurous-springs), and it wants it bad. As such, the mana is still something with which I'll have to grapple.
