# Charge it!

**Author:** Nate Heiss  
**Date:** March 4, 2005  
**Original URL (Wayback Machine):** <https://web.archive.org/web/20090601202842/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/nh56>

---

Welcome to artifact week! Rather, welcome to the end of it, along with the end of a certain deck that has been abusing these trinkets to no end. Overall, I have high hopes for what Standard will look like post-bannings, but if you want the whole scoop make sure to check out [Aaron's article](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041838/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/af56) today. I am not here to steal his thunder…I am here to play with toys.

Charge it! No, I am not talking about credit cards, I am talking about charge counters! Now, I have done a charge counter deck in the past, and it was pretty cool, but now with the full power of *Mirrodin* and *8th Edition*, and the pesky Ravager deck out of the way…all new cool sorts of decks can come out of hiding.

Come forth cool decks! Fear not the Ravager!

[![Thunderstaff](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/cma/237?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff)

This deck takes advantage of the awesome power of [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff) with [Sculpting Steel](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/374/sculpting-steel). [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff) is simply an awesome card that never got the credit it was due. While one [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff) doesn't shut anyone down (although it's still powerful), playing a second or copying it with [Sculpting Steel](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/374/sculpting-steel) really hurts most creature decks, slowing them to a crawl. This extra time allows you to win in any cool ways you prefer!

…and I prefer [Lightning Coils](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/198/lightning-coils)! Ever since I drafted the Power Conduit deck in Grand Prix Kansas City to make Top 8 I have a soft spot in my heart for those crazy charge counter-based cards from *Mirrodin*. [Lightning Coils](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/198/lightning-coils) has many mini Ball Lightnings hidden in its wiring, and it's not afraid to unleash them! You can easily help it along with [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit) and a charge counter generator, or simply by using [Energy Chamber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/64/energy-chamber)s.

The lands in an artifact based deck get to do all sorts of cool things too, since they don't need the restriction of producing silly colored mana. [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/280/cloudpost)s, Urzatron, [Stalking Stones](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/75/stalking-stones)…you name it. Not to mention the charging powers of [Mirrodin's Core](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/492/mirrodins-core).

Let's check out this gizmo.

[![Coretapper](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/eoc/132?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper)

The deck plays out by gearing up with a Myr on the second turn, following up with some combination of shiny artifacts. There are a lot of different ways games can go, but the big decisions you are going to make will likely involve [Sculpting Steel](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/374/sculpting-steel). There are three great targets for [Sculpting Steel](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/374/sculpting-steel): [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff), [Energy Chamber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/64/energy-chamber), and [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper). [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper), while looking innocent, is actually a great beating in this deck, especially in combination with [Lightning Coils](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/198/lightning-coils). In fact, you can get a rather quick kill with them:

Turn 2: [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper)

Turn 3: [Lightning Coils](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/198/lightning-coils), put a counter on it.

Turn 4: [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper), tap the first one to put another counter on the Coils. Sacrifice both for 2 counters each, plus 1 counter each from them going to the graveyard.

Turn 5: During your upkeep, the Coils crack for 8 counters and you get 8 wonderful little Ball Lightnings that smash your opponent for 24 damage. W00t!

Note that you can use either a second [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper) or a [Sculpting Steel](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/374/sculpting-steel), so this isn't actually that uncommon of a draw. Not too shabby for a little dude eh?

Your other route to victory is building one of your little Myr into a giant monster with the aid of [Energy Chamber](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/64/energy-chamber)s and [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit). It takes surprisingly fewer turns than you might expect. One good strategy is to divide your counters among a few Myr so that they can all start to attack, then you can pump them on the offense with your [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff) to finish the job, and [Stalking Stones](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/75/stalking-stones) can help out if the going gets tough.

##### Tips on Playing the Deck

- Normally you won't want to pump your [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper) with the +1/+1 counters; he has other work to do instead of attacking.
- [![Altar of Shadows](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/mrd/143?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/143/altar-of-shadows) [Altar of Shadows](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/143/altar-of-shadows) is a nice catch-all. If you need a mana boost to activate it every turn, throw some counters on it. Just be sure to have a [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit) around in order to pull them off again later as +1/+1 counters. You can also activate a [Stalking Stones](https://scryfall.com/card/ddf/75/stalking-stones) over and over again as a mana sink.
- Don't store your charge counters from [Coretapper](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/132/coretapper) on itself…it is too fragile of a target. Throw them on an Icy or a [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit) or whatever random artifact you have to keep them safe for moving with the Conduit later.
- Try to get as many counters on the [Lightning Coils](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/198/lightning-coils) as possible once you are going to go over 5. This will maximize the amount of damage you do with each wave of Ball Lightnings.

##### Adding Money to the Deck

There are lots of cool things you can add with this theme, even just within the realm of artifacts. [Blinkmoth Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/311/blinkmoth-nexus) is the single best card to add to this deck. It gives you an evasion artifact creature that you can pump up with your [Power Conduit](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/376/power-conduit)s. It is also a great way to get around [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) effects. You can splash a little red for [Bosh, Iron Golem](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/237/bosh-iron-golem) or even do something a bit flashier with blue: add 4 [Fabricate](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/9/fabricate)s and the Kaldra pieces! You could also go with Urza's Armors to strengthen the [Thunderstaff](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/237/thunderstaff) theme. If you wanted to go more red, you could use [Atog](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/209/atog) and [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast).

Overall this deck is a ton of fun and is much more competitive than you might expect. I mostly wanted to illustrate that the rest of *Mirrodin* works fine even without the artifact lands. In many ways it is like a whole block of cards just got unbanned for competitive play! Enjoy the new format folks!

Until next time, restrict your staff.
