Concerted Effort

I suffer from what I've commonly called “rogue-itis.” That is, when building decks I tend to gravitate to the deck ideas I've never seen before, or where I think I can break new ground. If there's a card that everyone else has dismissed as junk, I want to build a deck around it. If I've never seen anyone succeed with a Standard Blue Weenie deck, I'll try it out. Rogue-itis works the other way, too; if a deck is popular, I almost immediately lose interest in its key cards. I let others chase the chase rares (except [rare multi-lands](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041156/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm133)). Once my own deck is tuned and winning, I tend to want to move on to new, different, deck ideas.

I've spent some time contemplating the implications of my affliction and have come to very few conclusions. It's weird: I don't always root for the underdog in sporting events, I don't poo-poo current fashion trends, and I didn't name my son “Banana.” So far, I haven't seen that my rogue-itis plays itself out anywhere else in life other than **Magic**. To compensate, I suppose, I have pretty extreme rogue-itis when it comes to my favorite card game.

Why is this relevant today, midway through my last preconstructed deck evolution? Well, [last week](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041156/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm141) I ended the article believing in my heart that I would be making a Concerted Effort deck. There just weren't enough cards in Standard with double strike to truly make a double strike deck, but I was happy with the idea that I could sometimes give all of my creatures double strike via Concerted Effort. I rubbed my hands together, intending to add Courier Hawks, Order of the Stars, and the like during my next article.

A funny thing happened, though. *Lots* of people on the Message Boards had this same idea. infidel suggested it in the fourth post to my article last week, and the sentiment was echoed time and again. I started to feel as if people assumed that Concerted Effort was a foregone conclusion for my fledgling deck.

You can guess where this is going. The groundswell of love for Concerted Effort had me feeling it was too obvious a direction for my Boros Legion deck. As a result, I decided that I would not be adding Concerted Effort to my deck, even though I had never seen an Effort deck online. Keep in mind that usually I'm very responsive to reader feedback and want to make you all happy, but my own weirdness was standing in the way this time. The mere fact that everyone expected a Concerted Effort deck turned me off to the idea.

Rogue-itis is an odd affliction to get one's mind around.

A lot of other interesting suggestions popped up on the Boards, though, ideas with less universal support. Some suggested Godo, Bandit Warlord, sometimes with a Samurai theme and sometimes without. Others suggested an Aura theme via the Magemarks and cards like [Galvanic Arc](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/126/galvanic-arc). Rabble Rouser, [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer), Plow Through Reito, Boros Fury Shield, Flaming Fusillade, [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail), Captive Flame, Master Warcraft, [Sunhome Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/233/sunhome-enforcer), [Flash Conscription](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/124/flash-conscription), Into The Fray... the suggestions came from every direction on the Boards, and most lit my imagination afire. Thank you to the many people who piped up on directions to take my deck. I've listened to everyone, and you've given me a lot to contemplate.

If I'm not turning this thing into a generic White/Red beatdown deck, and I'm not making a Concerted Effort deck, where am I headed?

##### Stars And Spikes

Wherever it is I'm headed, the deck clearly still needs a lot of work. I've been reluctant to really commit to a theme or central card (some readers rightly pointed out that I intended double strike as a starting point more than a key theme), which is slightly different from previous evolutions. Here, I've been feeling my way as I go and hoping I end up somewhere fun. All the more reason, then, to keep playing games with the deck to see what's what.

*Game 11: Green/Red Tokens*

[![Sosuke's Summons](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/bok/145?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/145/sosukes-summons)

I played two [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter)s, while my opponent played [Fists of Ironwood](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/139/fists-of-ironwood) on one Trumpeter, then [Sosuke's Summons](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/145/sosukes-summons). Thankfully, I had the mana for [Cleansing Beam](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/78/cleansing-beam) to wipe out his tokens. On the next turn, he played [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves) and I played [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) followed by [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/201/flame-kin-zealot). In desperation, he tried another [Fists of Ironwood](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/139/fists-of-ironwood) and [Scatter the Seeds](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/159/scatter-the-seeds), but I had [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/324/sunhome-fortress-of-the-legion) and [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire) for the win.

*Game 12: Mono-Green Snakes*

So close, and yet so far. I hammered my opponent with two [Boros Recruit](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/243/boros-recruit)s, a [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter), and a [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted). What? That doesn't sound like “hammering?” Well, two [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous) and an [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) on my Tenderfoot and my opponent was finding it hard to keep creatures on the table as well as life points. I managed to kill not only a bunch of [Orochi Sustainer](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/236/orochi-sustainer)s and [Orochi Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/235/orochi-ranger)s, but also two Seshiro the Annointed and [Sosuke, Son of Seshiro](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/244/sosuke-son-of-seshiro). At two life, though, my opponent played a second Sosuke, then [Orochi Hatchery](https://scryfall.com/card/c15/260/orochi-hatchery) for two, then [Coat of Arms](https://scryfall.com/card/dds/58/coat-of-arms). My offense dwindled to nothing, and in about three turns my opponent had a batch of 11/10 Snakes with which to decimate me.

*Game 13: White Weenie*

We both started out with [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted)s, which I followed up with [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter) while my opponent played [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar). Hey! That's a good idea! When he tried to equip his little guy, though, I used [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) to kill it. My two creatures hit him around for a while, but then Inner-Chamber Guard showed up. I kept it from attacking with the Trumpeter while equipping my Tenderfoot with [Sunforger](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/473/sunforger), then playing [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/324/sunhome-fortress-of-the-legion). My opponent played [Blinding Angel](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/7/blinding-angel) and decided not to block my 4/1 Tenderfoot, so I was able to knock him down to three life. I stopped the Angel with my Trumpeter, allowed the Guard to hit me down to twenty-one, then activated [Sunforger](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/473/sunforger) to get [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) for the win.

*Game 14: Green/Black Golgari*

I had a slow start thanks to [Boros Garrison](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/231/boros-garrison), but managed a third-turn [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire) to fly over his [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder). He eventually got a [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), so I played another Legionnaire to make him lose it. He dredged the next turn and in response to him playing [Shambling Shell](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/57/shambling-shell), I used [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) to kill his Imp. On the next turn I played [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) and [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/201/flame-kin-zealot) to hammer him down to eight life. He played [Vigor Mortis](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/74/vigor-mortis) on the Imp and dredged his Shell, giving him two blockers to my two remaining creatures. I had [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) to make his Imp disappear, then played my second Zealot to attack him down to two life. He dredged the Shell again, giving him two blockers once more. I drew [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) off the top of my library and that was game.

*Game 15: Black/Green Spirits*

This was a long and not particularly interesting game. I kept a hand with only a Mountain and [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/324/sunhome-fortress-of-the-legion) as land, and although I was able to play a first-turn [Boros Recruit](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/243/boros-recruit) everything else in my hand required White mana. My opponent didn't put a lot of pressure on me thanks to two [Traproot Kami](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/147/traproot-kami), two [Bile Urchin](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/61/bile-urchin)s, and an [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/128/elder-pine-of-jukai). I finally drew a Plains, used [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) to kill his Elder Pine, and on the next turn I played [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling). My opponent used [Death Denied](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/92/death-denied) to get back his Spirit, then [Horobi's Whisper](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/88/horobis-whisper) to kill my Forgeling. I played [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade) to keep his force at bay, but I could only play one spell a turn thanks to my one Plains. After putting about twenty land on the table, my opponent had an army of small Spirits and [Seizan, Perverter of Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/143/seizan-perverter-of-truth). I tried to play [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous) to kill Seizan with my Swiftblade, but he had another [Horobi's Whisper](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/88/horobis-whisper) to kill it in response. I died with a full seven cards in hand.

Okay, the mana requirements for this deck are starting to get annoying, as exemplified by that last game. It's not the thing about the deck that is most concerning me, though. Right now I'm not satisfied that I'm taking enough advantage of double strike, nor am I really able to make [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) scary for my opponent. It's time to make a few more adjustments, some of which may surprise you.

OUT: 1 [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling)

[![Greater Forgeling](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rvr/109?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling)

In the mythical Flowstone deck that I'll now have to build someday, [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling) may have had a place. The whole “become a 6/1” trick sounds pretty good for a double strike deck, too. The problem is how slow this sort of trick is. [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling) won't hit the table until the fifth turn, and starting on the sixth turn I would have to invest {3}{R}{R}{W} to activate my Forgeling and Sunhome. Is ten mana worth a 6/1 (read: fragile) double-striker? [Firemane Angel](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/199/firemane-angel) is a much better deal for one more mana. Heck, for the same cost as [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling), there is [Hunted Dragon](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/106/hunted-dragon), [Serra Angel](https://scryfall.com/card/m15/273/serra-angel), and [Kumano, Master Yamabushi](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/176/kumano-master-yamabushi) available in Standard. Which is all to say that unless I'm massively adding to [Greater Forgeling](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/109/greater-forgeling)'s toughness to take full advantage of its ability, I don't think it makes sense in my deck.

*OUT*: 1 [Terrarion](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/488/terrarion)

[Terrarion](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/488/terrarion) is easy to leave in a deck because it is completely innocuous. It only takes one mana of investment, and it replaces itself when used. The problem with [Terrarion](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/488/terrarion), though, is that it is completely innocuous. It doesn't help me win the game. It doesn't affect the board at all. It's not a fast cantrip to cycle through my deck like Opt or even [Festival of the Guildpact](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/17/festival-of-the-guildpact). As a result, it's a very easy card to drop in order to make room for better spells. My deck needs help with its mana, yes, but the answer isn't to add cards like [Terrarion](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/488/terrarion). The answer is to minimize cards that are tough to cast and use the proper land. If I need help from an artifact, I would rather use something that can always help smooth my mana like [Boros Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/314/boros-signet).

*OUT*: 1 [Boros Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/168/boros-guildmage)

Here is where the surprises begin. In [Empire Maker](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041156/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm126), my Selesnya Conclave deck, I used a full four [Selesnya Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/207/selesnya-guildmage)s. In [Moldermort](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041156/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm138), my Golgari deck, I used four [Golgari Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/194/golgari-guildmage)s. I love the Guildmages. They're especially good tools for budget deckbuilders who don't have access to rare multi-lands. Since I've been complaining about the mana in this deck, it looks sort of bizarre to drop [Boros Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/168/boros-guildmage).

The problem is that neither of [Boros Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/168/boros-guildmage)'s abilities particularly suit the direction of my deck. First strike is great, but it doesn't help [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade), [Nightguard Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/26/nightguard-patrol), or [Boros Recruit](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/243/boros-recruit). Haste is great, but I'm currently not very preoccupied with an all-out aggro deck. I'm not saying that the Guildmage's abilities are irrelevant to what I'm trying to do, only that they're not optimal. Without its abilities, [Boros Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/168/boros-guildmage) is just an easy-to-cast Grizzly Bears, and I'm absolutely certain that my deck needs more than that.

*OUT*: 2 [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous)

Surprise! I'll say right now that [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous) may come back into the deck at some point. Its bump to my creatures' power is terrific with double strike and I like it as a combat trick. I'm still concerned about the deck's mana, though, and [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous) is not only tough to cast but fairly expensive as combat tricks go. Yes, I like untapping my [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter)s for a second use, and it's this ability that may bring Rally back into the deck. Right now, though, I would rather have something easy to cast that is more than a one-shot support of double strike, and something that helps flip [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted). Something like...

*IN*: 4 [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar)

[Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar) is like the base model of equipment. It doesn't do anything fancy, but it helps both a creature's power and toughness while being completely reasonable in mana requirements. For this reason, I see the Morningstar show up in a lot of budget decklists that don't have access to cards like [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte). The Jitte would clearly be better to use here than [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar) (and really scary with double strike), but adding to my, for example, [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade)'s stats is never a bad thing.

Cxwf on the Boards said, “[Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) is cool and all, but you can't add him first and add the flipping enablers in the next evolution. You have to add them together, or he's useless. Like the Baron said, he's going to be a vanilla 1/1 98% of the time.” I agree, actually, although I don't think you can underestimate the value of a creature that almost never gets blocked. Still, I can do better by my poor Tenderfoots. Hopefully by the end of today it will have earned its place.

I suppose one question to address is why I would choose [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar) over [Manriki-Gusari](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari). After all, Jitte is part of Standard for another nine months, and the Fork of Doom is certainly popular in the Casual Decks room of ***Magic** Online*. In this case, I like the extra point of power to a potential double-striker. There are certainly situations in which I'll wish [Manriki-Gusari](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari) is in my deck, but right now I'm going to see if I can survive on the [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar)'s muscle alone.

*IN*: 1 [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire)

I may not eventually want [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire) in my deck. Adding it feels a bit schizophrenic, since I've said that I don't want a basic Boros aggro deck and I don't want to put more strain on my deck's mana. Still, I just love [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire). I like the art, I like the cost for a 2/2 flying guy with haste, and I am almost never sorry to draw it. Right now I'm seeing it as the third best gold card in my deck behind [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) and [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade). I think that almost any deck I make at this point can probably use the Legionnaire, although I admit it's harder to stay away from aggro using a full four of these critters.

Actually, if you look back at my additions to the deck so far - [Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix), [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade), [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted), [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire), Sunhome, [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar), and [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) - I'm not sure that I've convinced anyone that this isn't a generic beatdown deck. In my mind, the deck is shaping into something different than aggro (and different from a Concerted Effort deck... again, my apologies), or at least that's been the intention behind the changes. My guess is that very soon I'll start to add and remove cards that run against generic beatdown's grain. In the meantime, here is the deck:

*Game 16: Mono-Blue Control*

I had a first-turn [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) and a second-turn [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar). *Game 17: Red/Green [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore)*

My opponent's late [Magnivore](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/202/magnivore) was just a speedbump.

*Game 18: Green/Black Shirei*

Shirei, Shizo's Caretaker

Once again, [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) came out on the first turn, again followed by [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter). ##### Turning Gold Into Armor

The mana requirements for the deck are getting in the way less often than I had expected, but they still drive me nuts. It's time to look closely at my gold cards to see which are expendable.

*OUT*: 3 [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter)

As I've said, this deck more than any other has been stuffed full of multicolor gold cards. This is bad news for a budget deck that can't afford land like [Sacred Foundry](https://scryfall.com/card/eoe/256/sacred-foundry) and [Battlefield Forge](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/362/battlefield-forge). One of my goals, then, is to minimize the number of gold cards in my deck.

[Lightning Helix](https://scryfall.com/card/mkm/218/lightning-helix) is too good not to use and establishes the threshold for quality I want out of a gold card. [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade) is in the same boat, and is clearly a card I'm committed to keeping. [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire) makes me smile, but I'm wondering if it meets this same quality standard, since it's not advancing any of my core themes per se. Right now the Legionnaire is sitting on the bubble. Every other gold card in the deck, by my way of thinking, is nice to have but expandable.

Now let's talk [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter) specifically. Expecting it to come down on the second turn is asking a lot of my deck, so its ability is one I should want throughout the duration of the game. Well, the “can't block” ability is one I actually don't want to promote since I'm hoping to flip [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted). The “can't attack” is more useful, but if all I care about is this ability then I would be better served by [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters), [Devouring Light](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/15/devouring-light), or [Ghostly Prison](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/146/ghostly-prison). I also think that Master Warcraft does everything I want [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter) to do, but better. The only difference between the Trumpeter and these other options is that [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter) can grab a Morningstar and start swinging it around, which is important for a deck hoping to win through combat damage.

Here's the thing, though: I don't actually care that much about the attacking and blocking of my opponent's creatures, at least no more than any other deck cares about it. If my deck is working correctly, I should be able to blow through most blockers (or, as I've discovered with [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted)'s effect on the game, blow by them). Opposing attackers may end up being a problem, but I prefer [Faith's Fetters](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/18/faiths-fetters) as an option if so. Right now I want to keep my creature count up and pick something that is both easier to cast than [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter) and also something that helps out my other creatures.

*OUT*: 1 [Agrus Kos, Wojek Veteran](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/190/agrus-kos-wojek-veteran)

Agrus Kos, meanwhile, helps out my other creatures but does so Way. Too. Slowly. He's not going to give a boost to my creatures until the sixth turn at the earliest, and at that point most of my creatures are probably dead. Then there's the fact that Agrus Kos himself is only a 3/3, making him vulnerable to about every single removal spell that's popular outside of [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock). Don't get me wrong: I like Agrus Kos, and I much prefer a gold card of five mana to one of two. In my experience, though, he just hasn't enhanced my creatures reliably enough. There's also the issue that Agrus Kos begs to have *more* gold cards included in a deck, whereas I want to get away with less.

*IN*: 4 [Veteran Armorer](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/34/veteran-armorer)

In comes [Veteran Armorer](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/34/veteran-armorer). The Armorer isn't as versatile as [Thundersong Trumpeter](https://scryfall.com/card/mm3/195/thundersong-trumpeter), but it's easier to cast and should help out my whole team. [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) and [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade) get a little tougher with an Armorer nearby, and now [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/225/skyknight-legionnaire) survives most one-on-one combat with opposing fliers. Life gets a lot better with two Armorers on the table, a situation where my whole army is difficult to kill outside of [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god). Add [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar) to the mix and suddenly I have a sort of “build your own creature” deck in which I start with something small and start adding permanent bonuses and equipment to make them combat monsters. If there is a theme it feels like I'm pursuing with my deck, build-a-critter feels like it.

Yes? You say Concerted Effort sounds like it fits such a deck? Sit down, please.

*OUT*: 2 [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/201/flame-kin-zealot)

I believe I called [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/201/flame-kin-zealot) a “house” last week. I haven't reversed my opinion since then, but I have softened it. I do love Overrun effects, and I've always enjoyed casting the Zealot with this deck. The problem, again, is that mana cost. If my deck focused on throwaway creatures a la Promise of Bunrei, Belfry Spirit, and Twilight Drover, I'm pretty sure that [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/201/flame-kin-zealot) would be my feature card. Right now, I'm realizing that I want my creature-pump effects to either be permanent ([Veteran Armorer](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/34/veteran-armorer)) or reusable ([Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar)). [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) doesn't help me get a second pop out of my Zealot, and in at least three games I've either been unable to cast a Zealot in hand or unable to find quite the right time to cast it for maximum effect. I still think I'll use him in future decks, but I'm sitting him on the sidelines for now.

[![Kabuto Moth](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/chk/20?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth)

*IN*: 2 [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth)

Folks who played around with [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) when Kamigawa cards debuted are sure to have used [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth). The Moth's ability obviously makes my creatures difficult to kill, especially with all of the other bonuses I'm likely to be adding to them. Note that I'm not necessarily going to surprise my opponent into flipping [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted) with this card or the others in the deck. Instead, I'm putting my opponent into a difficult spot: Block my 4/6 Tenderfoot this turn and make it worse when Kenzo shows up, or keep letting me beat your head untouched. To me, I'm happy if Kenzo the Heardhearted never shows up as long as my Tenderfoot has given my opponent nightmares. I know that other deckbuilders have (and want me to do likewise) obsessed about how to flip the Tenderfoot, but I see him more as “often unblockable.”

Of course with [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth) I want [Rally the Righteous](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/222/rally-the-righteous) back in my deck. Then again, I suppose that culling the gold cards down to the bare essentials makes Radiance less effective. As a result, I've got my eye on [To Arms!](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/30/to-arms!) as a possible future addition.

##### Of Moths And Enforcers

I seem to be pretty long-winded today, so let's dive right into the last round of changes for today.

*OUT*: 2 [Nightguard Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/26/nightguard-patrol)

Other than the one toughness, I've got nothing bad to say about [Nightguard Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/26/nightguard-patrol). It's great in a Concerted Effort deck, has a splashable mana cost, and is a good way to slow things down as a non-tapping, first-striking, perma-blocker. I've decided not to go the Concerted Effort route, though, which means that there are simply other cards I want to use more, even cards for {2}{W} mana. Cards exactly like...

*IN*: 2 [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth)

My games slowed down dramatically once [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth) hit the table. I really like how well the Moth establishes the game as a game of combat math, where every attack and block has potentially-huge consequences. You may not see a mere +1/+2 bonus that way, but I do. More importantly, I can see my opponent's hesitation once [Kabuto Moth](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/20/kabuto-moth) becomes active so I know they see it too. The only downside is that it's incredibly fragile for a turn, and I find myself holding my breath until it can start pumping my creatures. Hopefully four of them in my deck will help ensure that I have one to help out [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/2/bushi-tenderfoot-kenzo-the-hardhearted), [Boros Swiftblade](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/172/boros-swiftblade), and company.

*OUT*: 2 [Cleansing Beam](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/78/cleansing-beam)

[Cleansing Beam](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/78/cleansing-beam) has remained in the deck too long simply because there were other needs I wanted to address first. The truth is that [Cleansing Beam](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/78/cleansing-beam) is rarely as good as [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) and can, in a deck with gold creatures, often backfire. If Selesnya decks were running rampant, I might be able to see the need for [Cleansing Beam](https://scryfall.com/card/cma/78/cleansing-beam) so I'm happy that it exists in Standard. Right now, though, it does much too little for way too much mana. Without it in the deck I maintain a rather large vulnerability to [Hand of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/71/hand-of-cruelty), but I'm going to assume that a) [Hand of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/71/hand-of-cruelty) isn't an incredibly popular card in the Casual Decks room since I've faced it one game out of twenty-five, and b) this is something I can handle in my sideboard. Right now I'm just as happy to keep increasing my creature count and ignoring the need for Red burn.

*OUT*: 2 [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey)

I believe a quote from TheOtherJohnny on the Boards was, “I'll just [convince] Jay to stop being a WOG-fearing wuss and get rid of Otherworldy Journey for another post.” Cheza said the same thing a bit more diplomatically: “Don't be afraid of [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) and the like. The [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) won't save you. The better thing would be an additional creature. You can "save" it in your hand...so if your opponent plays a Wrath, it's almost the same effect as with the Journey.”

[![Sunhome Enforcer](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rav/233?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/233/sunhome-enforcer)

I'm not sure I agree with the logic that another creature has the same effect as [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey), since the Journey can also “fizzle” Auras and is a better tool against cards like [Wildfire](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/134/wildfire). Still, I'm willing to concede that my fear of [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god) may be a bit exaggerated. We all have our hot buttons as deckbuilders. There are some situations that you must guard against psychologically because if you don't it will drive you crazy. I have several such buttons. For example, I almost never skimp on land nor overload my decks with things I might not be able to cast because I think manascrew is the least fun way to lose in Magic. Clearly another one of my issues is that when using a swarm of creatures, I like to have a plan against Wrath effects.

That said, I've found [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) to be decidedly mediocre in my deck. I'm not using comes-into-play or leaves-play effects. I'm not using board-clearing effects of my own. My deck isn't a foot-to-the-floor beatdown deck that can't afford to hold threats back. All in all, I'm happy to wander a bit out of my comfort zone, making myself vulnerable to Wrath in order to include cards that are likely to shine in my deck.
