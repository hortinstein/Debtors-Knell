**Author:** Ben Bleiweiss  
**Date:** July 30, 2007

Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! This is the final part of the Great Dredge contest. For those just joining us, I threw out a list of 80 cards with point (ticket) values, and [challenged people](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042730/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb118) to build a deck worth 30 tickets or less. There were over a hundred responses, and I [picked twelve decks](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042730/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb121) to highlight from among those and ended up going with DELangley's Gruntcursion deck as [the winner that I would playtest and modify](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042730/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb122). In today's article, I take the deck for a spin, and work out some of the kinks.

**Game 1: noles (Mono-White Flankers)**

Noles starts out with some [Icatian Javelineers](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/9/icatian-javelineers), and follows with [Children of Korlis](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/14/children-of-korlis), [Outrider en-Kor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/30/outrider-en-kor) and [Gustcloak Cavalier](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/22/gustcloak-cavalier). I drop a second-turn [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug), and it gets hit by his Javelineers. This is when I learn the timing on the Thug—if is it the only creature in your graveyard when it goes to the graveyard, it will return itself to the top of your library! This completely foiled my plans for dredging it on the third turn to get the deck going, plus it set my draw back a turn (I'd have to draw the Thug again the following turn).

[![Golgari Thug](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/rvr/76?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug)

I finally start getting guys down on the table, and I draw a [Greenseeker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/207/greenseeker) to get [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) to the graveyard to start dredging. This lets a [Mystic Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/192/mystic-enforcer) get threshold, and lets me drop [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt)s to start swinging. I knock him to 10, but run into two straight [Dawn Charm](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/371/dawn-charm)s to allow him to survive a Grunt/Enforcer swing.

I also misread [Gustcloak Cavalier](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/22/gustcloak-cavalier) and think it can fly, which leads me to lose while I'm at 2 life—he has two attackers, and I could have dropped triple [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) to block his Outrider *en*-Kor and Cavalier (even through the tap ability) to allow for a third attack with my [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt) and [Mystic Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/192/mystic-enforcer). Frown!

Record: 0-1

I'd like to take a little time to talk about misplays. As you'll notice, I made two pretty significant errors during Game 1, both of which probably cost me the game. The first (letting my Thug die as the only creature in my graveyard) was due to being unfamiliar with both the Thug and with this deck. The second, thinking that the Cavalier had flying in addition to flanking, was due to not reading a card that I'm unfamiliar with.

While the latter comes down to laziness (it takes two seconds to read a card—if I'm at 2 life and about to die, I should be reading every piece of electronic cardboard on the screen to see if I have any outs), the first mistake came more from bad rules judgment than from sloppiness. [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) reads, "When [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) is put into a graveyard from play, put target creature card in your graveyard on top of your library." Note that the triggered ability (put target creature card in your graveyard on top of your library) happens upon the Thug being put into the graveyard (when [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) is put into a graveyard from play), so the timing works out, of course, that it will return itself if it is the only creature.

This is why, last week, I thought that [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp) might be a better choice for this deck than [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug). I knew that whenever I saw an opponent play [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) in previous matches, I loved it—because I knew I could kill their Thug and make them return a creature they didn't want to draw from their graveyard to their library (or force them to dredge the Thug itself). I didn't make the connection that, if the Thug was the only creature around, it would return itself.

**Game 3: Mastercard_169 (Red/Blue Storm)**

Mastercard_169 starts building up cards in hand with [Sleight of Hand](https://scryfall.com/card/woe/67/sleight-of-hand) and [Foresee](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/69/foresee), and I get a slowish start without anything to dredge with my [Greenseeker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/207/greenseeker) except for [Dakmor Salvage](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/156/dakmor-salvage). On turn five, he drops [Grinning Ignus](https://scryfall.com/card/stx/104/grinning-ignus). On turn six, he plays multiple [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/109/blazing-firesinger-seething-song)s, returns [Grinning Ignus](https://scryfall.com/card/stx/104/grinning-ignus), and replays/returns [Grinning Ignus](https://scryfall.com/card/stx/104/grinning-ignus) multiple times until he gets down to four mana. At that point, he plays [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens) with a storm count of seven, and runs me over with sixteen goblins. I do have a couple of guys on the board, but [Mortivore](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/69/mortivore) and [Nether Traitor](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/220/nether-traitor) aren't going to be able to get through for enough damage with that many goblins on the board. I stare at the [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim)s in my hand and decide it's time for some changes.

Record: 1-2

It's time for the first round of changes! So far, there have been two main offenders in the deck – [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) and [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim). I've already talked about [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug) above, but let me mention a little bit about [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim).

Last week, I contrasted [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) and [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect). In practice this week, I found that [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) was absolutely the inferior choice to [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) for a dredge deck. If all is going according to plan with this deck, I want to be dredging every single turn of the game until I reach the end of my library and can get Gruntcursion going. Every time I cast [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim), that's a turn I can't dredge—I have to basically stop going through my library so I can draw the card I want to [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim).

[Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) doesn't set me back a dredge turn. [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) does. The extra two mana and sorcery speed be danged, [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) just *works* with dredge, whereas [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) works *against* dredge.

G'bye [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim)!

Last, I want to get another [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll) into the deck. He is king dredge, the big six cards, and there's every reason to up the count in this deck. To make ticket room for [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll), I yank out the [Bitter Ordeal](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/80/bitter-ordeal)s. I don't see a time when I'll get that many creatures into the graveyard in one turn (barring a flashback on [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return)), and it doesn't affect the board position of the game at all.

**Out:** 3 [Golgari Thug](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/76/golgari-thug), 2 [Bitter Ordeal](https://scryfall.com/card/fut/80/bitter-ordeal), 4 [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) (-3.7 tickets)
**In:** 1 [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll), 4 [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), 4 [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) (+2.2 tickets)
**New Cost:** 28.5 Tickets

He drops [Shambling Shell](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/57/shambling-shell) and [Vulturous Zombie](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/170/vulturous-zombie), and I trade my [Greenseeker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/207/greenseeker) for his 3/1 ground pounder. **Game 7: Vaportrain (G/U [Doubling Season](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/216/doubling-season))**

I can't keep up with a [Doubling Season](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/216/doubling-season), Evolution Vat, a [Spike Feeder](https://scryfall.com/card/wc98/bs118/spike-feeder), and a [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves). He takes his Elves from 3/3 to 13/13 in a turn—it's a 1/1 with two +1/+1 counters. He uses Evolution Vat to give it two more +1/+1 counters (four +1/+1 counters), and then uses the "double the counters" ability from Evolution Vat to give it another eight +/1+1 counters, making it a 13/13 to kill me. I had a [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect) in hand, but I really, desperately needed to dredge a [Mortify](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/662/mortify) into my graveyard to take out his [Doubling Season](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/216/doubling-season).

Record: 3-4

It's time for another round of changes. I haven't been getting a fast enough start due to the inability to drop dredge cards into my graveyard consistently, so I feel it's time to get [Llanowar Mentor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/216/llanowar-mentor)s into the deck—the other one-drop green creature that can enable a turn-two dredge. I also want another [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) in the deck (to support a full eight green one-drops), and a third [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return)—I find myself wanting to use the Return, but never having one in my graveyard when I need it!

To make room for these, I yank out a [Selesnya Sanctuary](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/246/selesnya-sanctuary) (straight for the [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest)), all four [Nether Traitor](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/220/nether-traitor)s (for the Mentors) and a [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt) (one) for the [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return). Three Grunts seems plenty for what this deck wants to do (get down to no cards in library, and then use [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt)s to set up the rest of the deck via recursion).

The [Nether Traitor](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/220/nether-traitor)s were virtually impossible to hardcast, and there isn't a self-sacrifice outlet aside from [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return) with which to kill your own creatures to enable a return of the Traitors. In addition, 1/1 haste creatures aren't where this deck is at—when I can drop 12/12 [Mortivore](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/69/mortivore)s, 6/6 [Mystic Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/192/mystic-enforcer)s and 4/4 Grunts as kill conditions, a lone set of 1/1 shadow creatures isn't going to really tip the game in my favor.

**Out:** 4 [Nether Traitor](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/220/nether-traitor), 1 [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt), 1 [Selesnya Sanctuary](https://scryfall.com/card/clu/246/selesnya-sanctuary) (-5.1 tickets)
**In:** 4 [Llanowar Mentor](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/216/llanowar-mentor), 1 [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/153/dread-return), 1 [Forest](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/295/forest) (+1.6 tickets)
**New Cost:** 25 Tickets

**Game 9: Devillion (Four-Color Control)**

**Game 10: Frocious (Blue-White Bounce)**

This is a long an epic game that ends up lasting 22 turns! We go back and forth, with him casting Venser, [Seht's Tiger](https://scryfall.com/card/c17/72/sehts-tiger), [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/82/riftwing-cloudskate), [Draining Whelk](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/62/draining-whelk) and [Momentary Blink](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/15/momentary-blink). I end up, for the first time, getting the Gruntcursion plan to work—I dredge my entire library into my graveyard except for one card (just in case he bounces my Jötun Grun) and start recurring all of my [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect)s. I set up my library with four [Recollect](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/197/recollect)s, and two [Jötun Grunt](https://scryfall.com/card/cmd/16/j%C3%B6tun-grunt)s, and then let my first Grunt die. This lets me bring back creature kill or [Mortivore](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/69/mortivore)s/[Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll)s at will, and after a long-sought game, I am able to finally break through with an army of 22/22 creatures against his draw of double Epochrasite, [Urza's Factory](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/288/urzas-factory), and other sundry creatures.

Record: 6-4

If I were to continue to change this deck further, I would get in one more land (to help mulligan issues), swap out a couple of [Dakmor Salvage](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/156/dakmor-salvage)s for [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/291/swamp)s, and add in a fourth [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/96/life-from-the-loam) (to allow me to "draw" the lands I've dredged) for a [Mystic Enforcer](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/192/mystic-enforcer). However, this last game (10) was the only game where I got the Gruntcursion plan to work—in other games, I was either ending the game quickly (with 12/12 or larger creatures) or getting run over by weenie creatures. As a black-green dredge deck, this deck would show a lot of promise—I could bring in more dredge cards or focus more on recursion. As a black-green-white deck, this is about the end of the line—the Grunts and Enforcers would probably go in favor of a solid black-green build, and I didn't want to test this deck out of the original intent. Consider Gruntcursion 4 the final build of this deck while retaining the white!
