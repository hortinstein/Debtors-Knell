Did you play in your Standard State Championships last weekend? Chances are, if you're from Paraguay, you did not. However, if you are from a state, let's say one between Alabama and Wyoming, you might have competed in one of the first major post-*Time Spiral* Standard tournaments in the world!

As many of you may know, I work for Star City Games, and we ran the Virginia State Championships. The turnout for States was great, everybody got their jiggy thing going on, and nobody was trampled underfoot – all in all, a successful 172-person tournament. Hoo daddy, do I have a scoop for you today! I present to you the 2nd place finisher from the Virginia State Championships!

…circa 2003.

You can stop looking at me like I'm Hurkylme Jenkelfors now. *I* wasn't the one who returned all your artifacts – I just brought you a deck that *has* a lot of artifacts!

“Ben Bleiweiss, truly you have lost your marbles. If not marbles, then immediately inspect your deck of cards to see that it's full. Failing that, schedule an inspection to see the relative tightness of your screws.”

Fie. Upon. Thee.

John Upton piloted this deck to a second place finish against young former state champion Wesley Moss in 2003. He would have won the tournament if not for a particularly nasty set of timing rules, that I'll go into later. This deck was designed to beat Affinity and Goblins, two of the nastier decks running around in those days. John himself was a terrific **Magic** writer (where have you gone, John Uptonmaggio?), and you can see him talk about this (untitled) deck [here](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042610/http://ww2.wizards.com/Site/LeavingWizards.aspx?url=http://www.starcitygames.com/php/news/article/6029.html&origin=).

I'll say one thing for sure – this deck absolutely looks like whatever pile of excrement you're thinking of! Seriously – there are random two-ofs in the deck all over the place, no way to tutor for anything other than basic lands, Skeleton Shards with only eight artifact creatures in the entire deck, six basic land searchers for only ten basic lands, and triple-black [Visara the Dreadful](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/114/visara-the-dreadful) with only eleven sources of black mana in the entire deck!

And this deck wins like nobody's business.

I found that out after watching John pilot the deck through the Top 8 of states that year. In the last game of the finals, he was targeted by a [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) while holding double [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast), with his opponent at less than 10 life. He was fearful of a response, so he let the other guy name a color, and then tried to respond by casting double [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) for the win.

I began taking the deck to various and sundry Friday Night Magics, and I always pulled a respectable finish with the deck. Unfortunately, this was pre-*Darksteel* Standard, and the deck immediately fell out of favor when Arcbound Ravager and [Skullclamp](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/210/skullclamp) ruined made Standard interesting for a while.

The reason this week's article isn't *Time Spiral*-oriented is because *Time Spiral* was only legal for a couple of days before my deadlines were due into the powers that be. In addition, the prices on cards from a new set are completely wacky the first few days after a set release as people scramble to get ahold of the cards. I didn't want to have to price up a deck against artificially inflated prices on singles, so I decided to dredge up an old favorite.

The problem is, John Upton's deck, janky-yet-consistant as it may appear, is nowhere near budget. I researched the prices of the cards in this deck on ***Magic** Online*, and came up with the following:

[Bloodstained Mire](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/216/bloodstained-mire): 10 Tickets
[Hammer of Bogardan](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/193/hammer-of-bogardan): 1 Tickets
[Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone): 2 Tickets
[Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute): 2 Tickets
[Promise of Power](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/54/promise-of-power): 2 for 1 Ticket
[Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast): 4 for 1 Ticket
[Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum): 4 Tickets
[Starstorm](https://scryfall.com/card/nec/110/starstorm): 2 Tickets
[Visara the Dreadful](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/114/visara-the-dreadful): 3 Tickets
 Total cost of the deck: 56 Tickets

In addition, [Disciple of the Vault](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/86/disciple-of-the-vault) is banned in Extended. Yes, even when you're not playing Affinity. Grumble rassa frazzle dimples.

It was up to me to start taking out cards from the deck. The [Bloodstained Mire](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/216/bloodstained-mire)s were the first to go – they accounted for twenty of the twenty-six ticket surplus cost, so without them, the deck hits the thirty-six ticket mark. I also didn't need Visara – she didn't make much sense in the deck, nor did [Promise of Power](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/54/promise-of-power) (which was a holdover from a Standard environment long since gone). That trimmed another four tickets off of the deck cost.

The other cuts were harder, since the remaining rares were a little more integral to the deck. In the end, I decided I wanted to stick more with the recurring artifact creatures theme of the deck, and less with the burn. To that effect, I took out the two [Starstorm](https://scryfall.com/card/nec/110/starstorm)s and the lone [Hammer of Bogardan](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/193/hammer-of-bogardan).

**Out**: 2 [Bloodstained Mire](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/216/bloodstained-mire), 4 [Disciple of the Vault](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/86/disciple-of-the-vault), 1 [Hammer of Bogardan](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/193/hammer-of-bogardan), 2 [Promise of Power](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/54/promise-of-power), 2 [Starstorm](https://scryfall.com/card/nec/110/starstorm), 1 [Visara the Dreadful](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/114/visara-the-dreadful)

That brought the total from 56 tickets down to a svelte 27 tickets. I knew I wanted more of a focus on artifact creatures, so I scoured [Gatherer](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603042610/http://ww2.wizards.com/gatherer/index.aspx) for a list of Extended-legal artifact creatures which could send themselves, or others, to the graveyard. After much deliberation, I settled on the following list:

- [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion)
- [Arcbound Reclaimer](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/101/arcbound-reclaimer)
- [Etched Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/136/etched-oracle)
- [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog)
- [Atog](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/209/atog)
- [Nim Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/220/nim-replica)

I didn't want to fiddle with multiple colors in my deck, so [Etched Oracle](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/136/etched-oracle) was out. The others were all fine – [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion) is a much better six-drop than Visara, and [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog) can kill people in a single swing. [Arcbound Reclaimer](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/101/arcbound-reclaimer) lets me get back my [Skeleton Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/127/skeleton-shard)s (or [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb)s, or [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone)s) if need be, and [Nim Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/220/nim-replica) can kill utility creatures.

I also needed to replace the [Bloodstained Mire](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/216/bloodstained-mire)s, and I did so with a [Great Furnace](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/161/great-furnace) and a Swamp. The [Terror](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/103/terror)s were the best creature kill spells available to Black three years ago, but I upgraded them with [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh). [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) tends to kill a lot more than [Terror](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/103/terror). I ain't afraid of no ghosts!

I also tricked the deck out with a third [Skeleton Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/127/skeleton-shard) (that's the point of the deck, no?) and a third [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes). In the end, here's what I ended up with:

**In**: [Great Furnace](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/161/great-furnace), Swamp, [Skeleton Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/127/skeleton-shard), [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes), 4 [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh), 2 [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion), 2 [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog), 1 [Arcbound Reclaimer](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/101/arcbound-reclaimer), 1 [Nim Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/220/nim-replica)

**Game 1: Folkdsb (U/B Leyline)**

He drops [Hunted Horror](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/758/hunted-horror), and I use [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) and [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb) to kill it. He drops a second [Hunted Horror](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/758/hunted-horror), just in time for me to draw [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh). Much to my frustration, he has a [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) for my creature kill spell. He doesn't have one for the second [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) I draw on the next turn – how lucky!

We go into a long battle of move and countermove – he casts [Remove Soul](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/95/remove-soul) on my [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes), casts triple [Clutch of the Undercity](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/197/clutch-of-the-undercity) on my lands, and [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)s a [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog). When he casts [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) and passes the turn, I untap, [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) his hand out from under him, and then burn him out with multiple [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast)s and [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb)s.

*Record: 1-0*

**Game 2: Wwen (Urzatron)**

His first two plays are [Urza's Tower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1053/urzas-tower) and [Urza's Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1051/urzas-mine). I steel myself for a quick game, but Wwen proceeds to draw triple [Urza's Tower](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1053/urzas-tower) and double [Urza's Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/1051/urzas-mine), with no Power Plant in sight! I cast a first-turn [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb), cycle it and a [Barren Moor](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/262/barren-moor) on turn two, and drop [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum)s on turns four and five. [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog) joins up on turn six, and he answers with a [Vault of Whispers](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/322/vault-of-whispers) and a [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion). I drop [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) and [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb), feed all my artifacts to the [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog), and hit him to death with a 21/22 trampler.

*Record: 2-0*

**Game 3: swp777 (G/W/B?)**

He gets Windborn Muse and then puts double [Sword of Light and Shadow](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/298/sword-of-light-and-shadow) on it, with [Glorious Anthem](https://scryfall.com/card/m21/21/glorious-anthem) backup. I stare at the [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) in my hand and drop [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone). He proceeds to beat me about the head with his 7/8 flyer, and despite drawing a [Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/rix/114/shatter) and multiple [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes), I never get to my fifth mana to blow up the Stone and reset the game.

*Record: 2-1*

Note: Need more mana. Replace [Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/rix/114/shatter)s with Signets?

**Game 4: Landmark (G/B Elves)**

He gets [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves), and I [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb) them dead. He then drops triple [Elves of Deep Shadow](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/47/elves-of-deep-shadow). I play [Nim Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/220/nim-replica), take a hit, and then trade the Replica for two Elves after damage is on the stack. He drops [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer) on his last Elf, but I have a [Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/rix/114/shatter) ready. Landmark goes to the “B” plan of [Moldervine Cloak](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/150/moldervine-cloak), but I respond by Rending the Flesh of his last elf.

Momentum shifts to me, as I drop back-to-back [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion)s. He plays [Troll Ascetic](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/146/troll-ascetic) to hold up my guys, and then dredges back his Cloak the following turn to swing for six. I take 6, go down to 6, and swing back for 8. I play [Goblin Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/178/goblin-replica), with double-[Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) in hand. He attacks, and I block, throw [Goblin Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/178/goblin-replica) at his head, attack for 7 more, and then shoot him to death with the Trikes.

*Record: 3-1*

**Game 5: MikeMossbarger (U/B Grave Pact)**

He drops [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) and puts [Grafted Wargear](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/312/grafted-wargear) on it. I drop turn-three [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone) and then cast a fourth-turn [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) naming black. The only card I hit is Grave Pact, but he has four blue flyers in hand. He casts [Aven Fisher](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/41/aven-fisher) the following turn, and I [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) out his entire hand and then set off [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone) after taking another 7 damage. He draws [Surveilling Sprite](https://scryfall.com/card/ddh/43/surveilling-sprite), but I draw Triskelion and that's that. Despite his set-up of double Grave Pact over the next few turns, I draw [Skeleton Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/127/skeleton-shard) and am able to infinitely recur my [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion) until he runs out of creatures. Finally, he draws into only lands, and I beat him to death.

*Record: 4-1*

A think a trio of [Rakdos Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/250/rakdos-signet)s would help the deck – I keep falling *just* short on mana, and I'd love to accelerate into a turn-three [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) or [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum). Artifact signets give me more food for [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog), as well as [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast). In exchange, I remove [Shatter](https://scryfall.com/card/rix/114/shatter) (which was very metagame specific to begin with, and probably shouldn't have made the first cut of the deck) along with a single [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh).

**Game 6: Kaja Rang (R/B Burn)**

He hits turn-one [Frostling](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/103/frostling), turn-two [Seal of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/50/seal-of-fire), and turn-three [Seal of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/50/seal-of-fire). I [Persecute](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/151/persecute) red and hit [Stalking Yeti](https://scryfall.com/card/csp/98/stalking-yeti), [Yamabushi's Flame](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/198/yamabushis-flame) and [Hit // Run](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/152/hit-run). We trade [Frostling](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/103/frostling) and my [Nim Replica](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/220/nim-replica), and he drops a third [Seal of Fire](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/50/seal-of-fire). I [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh)[Rakdos Guildmage](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/214/rakdos-guildmage). I drop [Oblivion Stone](https://scryfall.com/card/ncc/373/oblivion-stone), and he casts [Hit // Run](https://scryfall.com/card/dis/152/hit-run) (I sacrifice a [Vault of Whispers](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/322/vault-of-whispers)). He then drops [Frostling](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/103/frostling) and Genju of the Spires and attacks me to 9. I crack Stone on my turn (so he doesn't keep his animated land alive), and he shoots me down to 3 with his enchantments.

I [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) [Dark Confidant](https://scryfall.com/card/fin/94/dark-confidant) on his next turn and drop [Rakdos Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/dsc/250/rakdos-signet). He drops [Frostling](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/103/frostling), and I drop [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes). He passes, and I play [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion), shoot [Frostling](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/103/frostling), attack for one, and then sacrifice [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes). Usually, I would hold off sacrificing my guy, but I don't want to die in response to sacrificing [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes) to a burn spell. He sends [Volcanic Hammer](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/226/volcanic-hammer) at my head, and I drop [Megatog](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/100/megatog) and attack for three with my [Triskelion](https://scryfall.com/card/moc/387/triskelion). I then get [Solemn Simulacrum](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/215/solemn-simulacrum), sacrifice it to he Tog, draw into another [Bottle Gnomes](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/177/bottle-gnomes), use it to go to 6, and attack for 9. He passes the turn, and I finish the comeback by swinging with everything sacrificed to Mr. Giant Teeth.

*Record: 5-1*

**Game 7: CommissionerGordon (I'm Batman! Errr…I mean U/R Burn)**

He casts double [Hatching Plans](https://scryfall.com/card/gpt/27/hatching-plans) and double Crack the Earth. This is three decks in a row now that I've been really happy with, and this one gets the B+ BoaB seal of approval!

It's not for everybody, because a lot of the games will be won by the skin of your teeth. You will have a lot of options each turn, so playing around with the deck a lot before really doing well with it is a must. It does play differently than almost any other deck I've ever played, so I highly recommend giving it a whirl if you have a chance.
