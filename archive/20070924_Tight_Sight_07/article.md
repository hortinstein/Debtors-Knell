Of course, this version of the deck hasn't been updated since Onslaught block, and it's off a budget. The first thing we need to do in order to budgetize and update Tight Sight is to understand how it works.

The goal of Tight Sight is to drop [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) (the enchantment, not the set!) onto the board, and then go into an infinite mana loop with [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) and [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis)/[Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict).

Let's walk through the process step by step.

Step one: Play [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) onto the board.

**Rules Note: Playing a card "as thought it were in your hand" does not mean you can use activated abilities on that card, or use it for payment on other cards. For instance, imagine you have a [Secluded Steppe](https://scryfall.com/card/eoc/177/secluded-steppe) on top of your library with [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight). You cannot cycle the Steppe, and you cannot "discard" it to a [Wild Mongrel](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/194/wild-mongrel) to give [Wild Mongrel](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/194/wild-mongrel) +1/+1.**

Step two: Reduce your library to some arbitrarily low number of cards by playing multiple cards a turn with [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight).

Step three: Use [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) to untap your lands.

Step four: Use [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) to shuffle two cards back into your deck. If one of the two Reclamations is in your graveyard, you want to [Reclaim](https://scryfall.com/card/ori/195/reclaim) the other Reclamation and an [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest). If you don't have the other Reclamation in your graveyard yet, Reclamation two [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)s.

Step five: Since [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is in play, you will eventually reach a point where the only cards in your library are [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest) and [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation). Cast each of these as they are revealed on the top of your library with [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight). Generate infinite mana (or as much mana as you want).

Step six: Once you have an arbitrarily large amount of mana, begin shuffling [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) and [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict)/[Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) into your library. Target your opponent with either of these. Repeat this until they are decked. Use [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) to force your opponent to draw a card with none left in their library. Win.

There are three stages that you go through when playing Tight Sight:

**Stage One: Achieve Threshold**

[Far Wanderings](https://scryfall.com/card/znc/66/far-wanderings) is key to this deck, because it is amazing mana acceleration if you can achieve an early threshold. This deck has many cards which enable that goal—[Mental Note](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/46/mental-note) puts three cards into your graveyard, as does [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/70/careful-study). [Quiet Speculation](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/68/quiet-speculation) gets four (itself plus three flashback cards), and [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict) is good for a couple of cards. Once you build up to threshold, you'll be able to go nuts with [Far Wanderings](https://scryfall.com/card/znc/66/far-wanderings), which in turn enables you to play [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) or get larger [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)s.

Note the synergies in Stage One. The deck plays several flashback cards ([Moment's Peace](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/251/moments-peace), [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis)), so milling yourself with [Mental Note](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/46/mental-note) or discarding cards with [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/70/careful-study) can be advantageous. At the same time that you are building towards threshold, you are often putting spells to-be-cast-later into your graveyard.

**Stage Two: Thin your Deck**

Once [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is on the table, you need to get yourself down to as few cards left in your deck as quickly as possible. Use all the card drawing at your disposal to burn through your deck. Cast multiple [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)s and [Far Wanderings](https://scryfall.com/card/znc/66/far-wanderings) to generate mana. Use [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) to recur [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest)s if need be. [Mental Note](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/46/mental-note) and [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/70/careful-study) can push you past dead cards (such as extra [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight)s, or lands past the first you play each turn).

**Stage Three: Go Infinite, Deck Your Opponent**

This is detailed above, but it involves recurring [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) and [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), and ending the game with infinite [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict) or [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis).

The core of this deck revolves around four copies of [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight), four copies of [Early Harvest](https://scryfall.com/card/9ed/235/early-harvest), four copies of [Far Wanderings](https://scryfall.com/card/znc/66/far-wanderings), and two copies of [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation). The cost of these fourteen cards is 11.5 tickets, and these cards are non-negotiable. The rest of the deck can be changed around as we see fit, so let's start seeing how Tight Sight looks on a budget.

First, let's remove all the sideboard cards. This removes 16.41 tickets from the cost of the deck, reducing it to a 70.75 ticket deck. Next, let's remove any card which costs more than a couple of tickets. This takes out three copies of [Cunning Wish](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/37/cunning-wish) (33 tickets), a [Flooded Strand](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/220/flooded-strand) (10 tickets) and a [Windswept Heath](https://scryfall.com/card/mh3/235/windswept-heath) (10 tickets), reducing the cost of the deck by a tremendous 53 tickets! This leaves us with a deck that costs only 17.75 tickets, and makes it so we have five holes in the deck to fill, at the least.

17.75 tickets we can work with!

Let's remember that the last time we touched the Tight Sight deck was between the release of *Onslaught* and *Legions*. This means we have a ton of sets to choose from when picking cards to add to this deck. This includes *Scourge*, all of *Mirrodin* Block, all of Kamigawa Block, all of *Ravnica* Block, all of *Time Spiral* Block, *Ninth Edition*, *Tenth Edition*, and *Coldsnap*. Keeping in mind that our three goals (achieve threshold, thin our deck, go infinite), let's see what these sets have added to the card pool for this deck.

**Dredge**

Dredge cards help us achieve two of our three goals: Achieving threshold and thinning our deck. Let's say that we discard a [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll) to a first-turn [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/card/ody/70/careful-study) or mill it with [Mental Note](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/46/mental-note). On the second turn, if we dredge [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll), voila! Instant threshold! Dredge also helps us get past dead cards while [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is in play. Imagine that the top card of your library is land, and the next two cards on top of your library are lands. With [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/96/life-from-the-loam) or [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/144/golgari-grave-troll), you can use a [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) not only to mill past those cards, but to draw a second card afterwards as well.

**Storm**

The old version of Tight Sight dedicated a lot of space to [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict) and [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis). Scourge introduced the storm mechanic, which lets you copy the storm spell for each previous spell that has been played that turn. Games tended to take a while to finish as you generated a ton of mana, and then had to spend time recurring [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) over and over again. Storm might help this deck kill a lot more quickly and efficiently, mainly with the use of something like [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/card/mb2/24/brain-freeze). Instead of having to recur [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) thirty times to deck an opponent, you need to cast one large [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/card/mb2/24/brain-freeze) and follow it with a single [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis) against an opponent with a depleted library.

In addition to those two mechanics, there are several cards which add to the power of this deck. The first and foremost of those is [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top). The Top not only smooths out your early draws, but helps you control which cards are getting milled by [Mental Note](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/46/mental-note), keeps you from getting stuck with a dead card on top of your deck when [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is in play, and lets you draw a ton of cards while [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is in play. If you have a Divining Top and [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) in play, you can tap Divining Top to draw a card, then replay it immediately from the top of your deck. This not only allows you to rapidly draw through your deck, but it also sets up a large storm count.

[Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth) was the alternate mana-acceleration spell to [Far Wanderings](https://scryfall.com/card/znc/66/far-wanderings) for Tight Sight, but [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder) is superior in nearly every way to [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth), due to the fact that it can block. It seems like a simple swap to switch out [Rampant Growth](https://scryfall.com/card/sos/162/studious-first-year-rampant-growth)s for [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder)s.

The original version of Tight Sight ran a couple of Fetch Lands. This was both to thin the deck, and to have a way to shuffle if there was a dead card on top of the deck with [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight). [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) makes it extremely hard to be stuck with a card you can't play while [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) is around, so this is less of a problem post-Kamigawa. Still, it might be advisable to test a version of this deck with [Terramorphic Expanse](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/273/terramorphic-expanse)—it is not great that the land comes into play tapped, but it serves the purpose of thinning, color-fixing or shuffling.

Please note that I took out the [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict)s entirely. [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict) might have a place in this deck still (especially with Divining Top), but Divining Top serves almost every purpose that [Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict) did—making sure the top card of your library wasn't a dead (unplayable) card, looking further down in the deck, and card drawing. A single [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/card/mb2/24/brain-freeze) acts as an alternate kill condition to [Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis)—and if you discard it early, that's fine because you don't need it until the end of the game when you can easily [Krosan Reclamation](https://scryfall.com/card/jud/122/krosan-reclamation) it when it's time to go for the kill.

I'm not sure that three dredge cards are the right number, but I want to test the deck and see how they work out. It might be better to play more cantrips ([Predict](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/98/predict)) and cut out dredge entirely, or it might be better to add in more dredge and cut out some of the card-drawing ([Deep Analysis](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/149/deep-analysis)?). This is why playtesting is so important to deck building, and deck building can't be done in a vacuum—I need to get a feel of how much the dredge cards help me achieve threshold quickly and/or thin the deck, as opposed to having card-drawing spells that I could cast to get further down in the deck.

I'm also keeping in the back of my mind the possibility of adding in Cloud Key to help power up [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top). This deck really, really, really has troubles with graveyard hate, and people are packing [Tormod's Crypt](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/235/tormods-crypt) all over the place these days. With Cloud Key naming artifact, I can chain two [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) with a [Future Sight](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/53/future-sight) out (each is played and cast for free), allowing me to get an infinite (or arbitrarily large) storm count for [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/card/mb2/24/brain-freeze) without needing to dip into my graveyard.

I played a handful of games with the first build of Tight Sight, and then decided that this deck really needed to be in the tournament room, and not in the casual room. While it was a blast to play, people in the casual room weren't having that much fun playing against me. Plus, this deck will win a lot of Game 1s on the strength of an opponent not having graveyard hate main-deck—but I want to see if I can stand up to a sideboarded match. Next week's article will deal with building a sideboard for Tight Sight '07 and bringing it to the tournament room. For now, enjoy the game logs below, and feel free to bring your suggestions about how to improve Tight Sight for my article next week!
