Something I intend to do every now and then is revisit a past deck and update it with new sets or a different format. Since starting my stint at Building On A Budget, I've been eager to circle back to where it all started... Samurai Fish.

Remember me?

In many ways, my four-week experiment transforming the Way of the Warrior preconstructed deck into a White/Blue aggro-control deck laid the groundwork for this column. The Samurai experiment was so successful and positively received that I was sure I could turn it into the focus of a column all its own. For those of you missed it, you can check out [part one](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x= link=mtgcom/daily/jm75), [part two](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm76), [part three](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm77), and [part four](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm78) to catch up.

Once you've read all of that, you'll see that below is the last version of the deck. Actually, I ended up with version 1.3 (a slightly quirky label since when I renamed the deck Samurai Fish I started over with version 1.0), but in playing the deck after the fourth article realized that I wanted to swap the maindeck Thoughtbind for the sideboard [Hisoka's Defiance](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/67/hisokas-defiance).

Now, a few things to note here. First, it's a *Kamigawa* Block deck as contrasted with the three Standard decks I've done since. Second, I made these changes without access to *Betrayers of Kamigawa*. As a result, my goal here will be to update the decklist for Standard and *Betrayers*. For those people who carry “pet decks” over from set to set and format to format, what I'm doing here should resonate with you. For those of you who salivate at any mention of the word "Standard" around the time of Regionals, this isn't intended to be a tournament-worthy answer to anything.

It may seem strange to update my Samurai deck on the eve of Saviors of Kamigawa being available to ***Magic** Online*. Shouldn't I, you know, wait a week or two? All I can say is: Please hang with me this week, because there is a method to my madness (not the least of which is wanting to jump into a *Saviors* preconstructed deck as soon as the set's available online). That said, I will do the same sort of *Saviors*-hypothesizing at the end of this column as I did at the end of the [Rat's Nest evolution](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm103).

Now, where to begin?

##### Parallel Universe

I think the first task of updating a deck for a new format or set is a theoretical one. For Samurai Fish, I looked across Standard to see if any superior analogues existed to the cards I was already using. That is, by expending the card pool by four sets, were there cards that were functionally similar to what was in my deck, only more powerful, cheaper, or both? This is the same sort of advice Doug Beyer gave when [discussing Prismatic decks](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Article.aspx?x=magic/magiconline/prismaticprimer), a format where analogues are king.

As far as I could tell, the Samurai were all staying put. Cards such as [Leonin Skyhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/7/leonin-skyhunter) are very good, but they aren't Samurai, which is the dominant theme of my deck. I can't see any white Samurai in *Betrayers* that are strictly better –- or even as good -- as what I'm already playing. Indebted Samurai is a cute idea, but I'm happy with [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) at the top of my mana curve.

That left me with the support cards. Here, analogues abound.

[![Mana Leak](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/2x2/58?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)

*OUT*: 4 [Hisoka's Defiance](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/67/hisokas-defiance)
*IN*: 4 [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)

[Hisoka's Defiance](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/67/hisokas-defiance) is a powerful counterspell in *Kamigawa* Block Constructed, and against many decks is almost as good as its {U}{U} uncle. In Standard, though, Defiance is a lot more situational. Although many Standard decks use Spirit and Arcane cards, few are built with them as a core strategy. Along comes [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak), a counter for the same cost that is much more likely to take down a key spell at a key time in the game. If [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) were available in *Kamigawa* Block, I would be hard pressed to decide which one Samurai Fish would want more. For a Standard deck, there's no competition.

*OUT*: 4 [Consuming Vortex](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/54/consuming-vortex)
*IN*: 4 [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth)

I was faced with a choice: Keep the splicing possibilities with [Consuming Vortex](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/54/consuming-vortex) and [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath) or look for non-Arcane analogues from *Mirrodin* Block and *Eighth Edition*. It may have been a harder choice if I didn't like [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth) so much more than [Consuming Vortex](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/54/consuming-vortex). [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth) can hit non-creatures and is a much better weapon against [Beacon of Creation](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/82/beacon-of-creation), [Sosuke's Summons](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/145/sosukes-summons), and Myr tokens, all of which I see frequently online.

*OUT*: 4 [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath)
*IN*: 4 [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith)

Finally, I had my toughest decision, whether to leave [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath) alone or replace it, then whether I liked [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) or [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) as the better analogue. Let me take each of these decisions in turn.

First, do I keep [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath)? I like its low cost and the ability to splice it. There are tricks the Breath allows me to do that are surprisingly useful -- things like stopping a [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh), making a [Cage of Hands](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/14/cage-of-hands) fall off, or making a creature unblockable to administer lethal damage. As a huge fan of Shelter, [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath) holds a dear place in my heart. The problem is that without any other arcane cards in the deck, the opportunity to splice it isn't going to come up terribly often. That means I need to decide if it's useful to dedicate four spots in my deck to its one-mana effect, and in that light it underwhelms me enough to drop it.

Next, [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) or [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith)? I think most people like [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) better. It can still save a creature from a [Rend Flesh](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/140/rend-flesh) or [Cage of Hands](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/14/cage-of-hands) in a way that [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) can't, and maybe its best use is to combat board-sweeping effects like [Wrath of God](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/70/wrath-of-god), [Hideous Laughter](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/115/hideous-laughter), or [Death Cloud](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/76/death-cloud), something even [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath) can't do. Since I'm also a fan of Flicker, I'm surprised that I've turned away from [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey).

Which is all to say that although the Journey probably has a place in more serious decks, the reasons I choose [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) are twofold. First, I can't get it out of my head that a [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster), [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain), or [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) with three +1/+1 counters on it is going to win the game single-handedly. The fact that it makes [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) into a legitimate threat is amazing too. The second reason is that I sort of rebuffed [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) once during the precon evolution, so returning to it now seems sort of wonky. Maybe these are dumb reasons, but they're the reasons that I'm starting my testing with [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith).

After those analogue decisions, I made another tweak that felt increasingly necessary over the past few months of play:

*OUT*: 4 [Cloudcrest Lake](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/274/cloudcrest-lake)
*IN*: 2 Plains
*IN*: 2 Island

I caught some flak when adding [Cloudcrest Lake](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/274/cloudcrest-lake) to my deck originally because “tap lands” don't tend to suit an aggressive deck well. At the time, my reasoning was that mana stability was vitally important to a two-color aggressive deck and that my deck could usually survive a missing land for a turn without missing a step. I've now played the deck enough to be swayed back to the other side of the argument. Yes, consistency is important, but in practice too often the turn of a tapped land is a turn I can't afford to lose. Using basic land means a harder time casting [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain) on the second turn or having the blue mana necessary to use [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) at a key time, but it's a chance I'll have to take. If I had access to [Tendo Ice Bridge](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/165/tendo-ice-bridge), that would be an ideal analogue to [Cloudcrest Lake](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/274/cloudcrest-lake). [City of Brass](https://scryfall.com/card/tmc/62/city-of-brass) wouldn't be horrible, either.

I had quite a few other questions about the deck beyond analogues. For example, the weakest links seemed to me to be [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) and [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law). Surely given a Standard card pool I could do better with these six slots. The Cloudskater is there to get me out of land-flooding later on since the deck only really needs four land to operate. Would [Spiketail Hatchling](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/111/spiketail-hatchling) or [Sage Owl](https://scryfall.com/card/m10/69/sage-owl) be better than [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater)? How about [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://scryfall.com/card/c18/95/ninja-of-the-deep-hours) or [Aether Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/456/aether-spellbomb)? The [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law) was there to focus on the Hondens, primarily, and some other problematic enchantments in *Kamigawa* Block such as [Ghostly Prison](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/146/ghostly-prison) and [Night of Soul's Betrayal](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/99/night-of-souls-betrayal). Are there enough enchantments in Standard that matter, or should I rely on something like [Terashi's Grasp](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/37/terashis-grasp)? Was Indebted Samurai worth a peek after all, despite its seemingly prohibitive cost? What about the aforementioned [Leonin Skyhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/7/leonin-skyhunter)?

As you can see, these aren't questions that involve direct analogues. To see if I would be better served by different cards, I would first need to play the deck to see why I was losing.

##### Playing Samurai Fish

I'll list some game logs since I continue to receive e-mail that they're people's favorite part of my preconstructed articles. Here's what I faced in the Casual Constructed room of ***Magic** Online* and how the deck performed:

[![Sensei Golden-Tail](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/chk/44?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail)

*Game 51 (how crazy is that?): Blue-green Aggro*

I did some early damage with a [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail) and [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto). I dropped [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster), then bounced his [Elvish Pioneer](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/243/elvish-pioneer) wearing Empyrial Armor to keep up my attack. He equipped an [Ornithopter](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/233/ornithopter) with his Armor, and I stalled with him at 10 life. I spent some time playing [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) and [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law), making each a Samurai. Then I played [Nagao, Bound by Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) and it was time to attack. He unloaded his hand for chump-blocking weenies like [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder), two more [Ornithopter](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/233/ornithopter)s, and his replayed Pioneer, but I overran him pretty easily. I had [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory), [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak), and [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) in hand as combat tricks just in case.

*Game 52: Monogreen Snakes*

I had a second-turn [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) and he had one, then two, then three [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/285/sakura-tribe-elder). Between Elders one and two I [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)ed a [Tangle Asp](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/94/tangle-asp), then played [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster) and [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain). My Samurai died to [Duplicant](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/946/duplicant). I finally drew my fourth land for [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor), putting pressure on my poor mana-flooded opponent. He tried [Sosuke's Summons](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/145/sosukes-summons) as a last-ditch defense, but I had [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth). A [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory) combined with Nagao's morale boost gave me the last points of damage I needed. It was a rout, plain and simple.

*Game 53: Green-red Aggro*

His deck was a little wacky, using a Snake base but also with burn and [Ogre Recluse](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/113/ogre-recluse). I [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)ed his [Orochi Ranger](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/235/orochi-ranger), then went on the offensive with [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster) and [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail). His Orochi Leafcaller blocked my Sensei and added [Kodama's Might](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/224/kodamas-might), but I had [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) to give me a 5/4 Golden-Tail. [Kodama's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ecc/113/kodamas-reach) let my opponent cast [Sosuke, Son of Seshiro](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/244/sosuke-son-of-seshiro) and a Recluse, two [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory) gave me lethal damage. Again, a rout.

[![Toshiro Umezawa](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/bok/89?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/89/toshiro-umezawa)

*Game 54: Black/Blue Toshiro Umezawa deck*

His deck was scary, using [Toshiro Umezawa](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/89/toshiro-umezawa), [Night of Soul's Betrayal](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/99/night-of-souls-betrayal), and [Forbidden Orchard](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/323/forbidden-orchard) to create a combo. His enchantment killed my [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) while he [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)ed my [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster). [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain) slipped through his countermagic, though, and that proved to be the difference. I made him replay [Night of Soul's Betrayal](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/99/night-of-souls-betrayal) via [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth), then killed it with [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law). He let my Samurai do damage until finally deciding to block with Toshiro. I used [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith). He [Hinder](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/54/hinder)ed. I played a second [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith). He tried to [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang) my Samurai. I had [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak). That was pretty much game. In the waning turns he managed to bounce my Samurai and then play [Remove Soul](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/95/remove-soul) on it, but ironically he had to use [Forbidden Orchard](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/323/forbidden-orchard) to do it, giving me a 1/1 token to do the last point of damage.

*Game 55: Green-blue Aggro*

This deck was very similar to the French Regionals deck I listed [last week](https://web.archive.org/web/20090603041801/https://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm104), with [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice), [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte), [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), [Iwamori of the Open Fist](https://scryfall.com/card/a25/174/iwamori-of-the-open-fist), and [Beacon of Creation](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/82/beacon-of-creation). I'm not entirely sure what he was doing in the Casual room of ***Magic** Online*, but maybe that's just sour grapes. The fact is that my small Samurai army got him down to 1 life before he cast Beacon and took over the game with a Sword- and Jitte-wielding Birds. If I had attacked with my [Nagao, Bound by Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) the first turn I could have instead of being intimidated by his Iwamori, that would have been game. Drat. I guess I can't complain about my showing, though, going 4-1 with four routs and a close loss to a polished tournament deck.

*Game 56: Five-Color Hondens*

The first several turns were amusing. I cast [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail), stuck on two lands. He played [Terror](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/103/terror), also stuck on two lands. I played a second Golden-Tail. He tried to [Echoing Decay](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/41/echoing-decay) it, but I had [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth). Three or four turns later, we both still had two mana and he was at 14 life from my Sensei. I found [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law) when my Sensei died, and it became a 4/4 thanks to [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) and a blocking [Eternal Witness](https://scryfall.com/card/cmm/286/eternal-witness). We both recovered our mana, him playing [Honden of Night's Reach](https://scryfall.com/card/ema/91/honden-of-nights-reach) and Genju of the Realm. I had just enough Samurai and combat tricks in hand to get him exactly to zero. Whew.

*Game 57: Five-Color Cogs*

I was very lucky to have a second-turn [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain), which prevented his Spellbombs from hitting the graveyard as he desperately drew cards for an answer to my swarm. His [Trinket Mage](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/41/trinket-mage)s were hiding, which allowed my Samurai, [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto), and two [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law) to wreak havoc. He tried to [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/283/pyrite-spellbomb) my Samurai, but I had [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith). When he tried [Crystal Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/159/crystal-shard), I had [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak). It was brutal.

*Game 58: Mesmeric White Weenie*

Mesmeric Orb

His deck was interesting, built around Mesmeric Orb and vigilance critters like Razor Golem and Skyhunter Prowler. He played a quick Golem and Orb, while I had [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law), [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto), and [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater). I plinked away for awhile, dropping to 11 from Golem attacks. Eventually my Cloudskater helped me find [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) at about the same time my [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law) became a 5/5 thanks to [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith). A second Kami killed his [Cage of Hands](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/14/cage-of-hands), and that was game.

*Game 59: Monoblue Control*

It's nice to know I can win a game stuck on only Plains. My opponent was a little mana-flooded, dropping Island after Island, while I got off to a fairly anemic start with two [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto). He bounced and countered one of my Hatamoto, opening the door for me to play [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail). He used Bribery to get a [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor). I played [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain), using [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory) to kill Nagao but none of my guys. He drew a [Silver Myr](https://scryfall.com/card/nec/158/silver-myr), conceded, and showed me the two [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) in hand I had been playing around.

[![Konda's Hatamoto](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/chk/31?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto)

*Game 60: Black-green Shirei deck*

My opponents, on the other hand, have a harder time winning stuck on one color. He could only find four Forests, playing [Myr Servitor](https://scryfall.com/card/uma/230/myr-servitor) and [Skeleton Shard](https://scryfall.com/card/hop/127/skeleton-shard). I had a near ideal start, with [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto), [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster), and [Nagao, Bound by Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) on the first four turns. After I swarmed over my opponent, he showed me a hand full of black cards, including [Nezumi Graverobber](https://scryfall.com/card/cm2/71/nezumi-graverobber-nighteyes-the-desecrator), Shirei, Shizo's Caretaker, and two [Horobi's Whisper](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/88/horobis-whisper).

*Game 61: Five-Color Myr Matrix*

I got a [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) that quickly earned its Samurai badge via [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail). My flier took out a 3/3 Skyreach Manta and became 4/4 via [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith). My opponent killed my Sensei with [Infused Arrows](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/133/infused-arrows), then dropped [Myr Matrix](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/132/myr-matrix). I used a lot of combat tricks -- including two [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory) and a [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) -- to keep [Nagao, Bound by Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) alive through a sea of Myr. Nagao, the beefy Cloudskater, two [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto), and a [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster) finally overwhelmed the Myr army for the win.

*Game 62: White-black Equip*

I shouldn't have won this game. I got stuck on three Islands for something like six turns while my opponent played Leonin Den-Guard, [Auriok Steelshaper](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/4/auriok-steelshaper), and [Auriok Glaivemaster](https://scryfall.com/card/dst/1/auriok-glaivemaster). The saving grace was that I had two [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak), taking out his Vulshok Battlegear and [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer). He found another Warhammer when I finally found a Plains and played [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor). I bounced his Warhammer to take out a creature blocking Nagao, then played my third [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) on his recast Warhammer. My opponent found some black creature removal and a [Whispersilk Cloak](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/225/whispersilk-cloak), but by then I was drawing land and could win via a second Nagao, [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail) and [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto).

Opal-Eye, Konda's Yojimbo

*Game 63: Monowhite Samurai*

He played a turn-three Opal-Eye, Konda's Yojimbo, and followed it up with an [Eiganjo Castle](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/275/eiganjo-castle). That was enough to completely screw me up for the rest of the game. I tried to bounce it with [Echoing Truth](https://scryfall.com/card/dmc/109/echoing-truth), but my opponent used [Otherworldly Journey](https://scryfall.com/card/mm2/30/otherworldly-journey) to save it (and make it bigger). I managed to get in a whopping three points of damage through major calisthenics, but his [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) was eating into my life. I finally drew another Truth thanks to [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater), but my opponent had [Blessed Breath](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/1/blessed-breath). Ouch. Beaten by all of the cards I've rejected! Oh, the humility!

*Game 64: Monored Coin-Flipping deck*

I had a second-turn [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater), and I was able to [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) his first Krark's Thumb. He [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock)ed a [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain) while my Cloudskater kept plinking away at his life. I then played [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster), and used [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith) when my opponent tried another [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock). Now I was off to the races, attacking with my 1/1 flier and 4/4 beatstick while he used [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/314/senseis-divining-top) to find an answer. He successfully cast another Thumb, but couldn't seem to find the coin-flipping cards to go with it. He tried [Ensnaring Bridge](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/253/ensnaring-bridge), which I [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak)ed, then he used [Shrapnel Blast](https://scryfall.com/card/ddu/48/shrapnel-blast) (sacrificing the Thumb) to try and kill my Blademaster. I had a second [Test of Faith](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/33/test-of-faith), and my 7/7 Fox ended the game on his next attack.

*Game 65: Blue-green Intruder Alarm*

My poor opponent was stuck on one Forest for a loooong time. The good news for him was that he used that Forest to play [Birds of Paradise](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/170/birds-of-paradise), then [Vine Trellis](https://scryfall.com/card/gvl/4/vine-trellis), then two [Silver Myr](https://scryfall.com/card/nec/158/silver-myr). I, meanwhile, had [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater), [Kitsune Blademaster](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/25/kitsune-blademaster), and [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain) on the attack. I used my Cloudskater to find a [Mana Leak](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/58/mana-leak) for my opponent's [Intruder Alarm](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/86/intruder-alarm), then to get [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor). My opponent found a second Alarm, but he had to tap his creatures to do it and I could attack with Nagao for the win.

Pause. Reflect.

Okay, I hadn't quite expected a 13-2 start right out of the gate. Just to be sure, I played another 10 games and went 7-3. My losses seem to be about my own mana issues, primarily. Still, let's say the deck is a-okay in its 2.0 incarnation, since I think the solution to its mana problems is probably the rare [Tendo Ice Bridge](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/165/tendo-ice-bridge). Without it, I'm either losing a turn to [Cloudcrest Lake](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/274/cloudcrest-lake) or getting stuck with off-color cards in hand without multicolor lands. Expect me some day to discuss the value of rare multi-lands to budget deckbuilding.

##### Two Paths of Temptation

The deck is performing well enough that to make changes now would be mostly for change's sake. That said, as I pondered cards in *Betrayers of Kamigawa* and Standard, I was very tempted by two ideas that take the deck in slightly different directions. Here are two “Paths Not Taken” before I discuss what *Saviors of Kamigawa* has to offer my Samurai Fish deck.

Patron of the Kitsune

*Life of the Kitsune*

Someone online pointed out that I should use Patron of the Kitsune in my deck since it's mostly Foxes anyway. I immediately fell in love with the idea. The deck probably loses any need for blue in it, and drops cards like [Konda's Hatamoto](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/31/kondas-hatamoto), Nagao, and [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law) in favor of the Patron, [Eight-and-a-Half-Tails](https://scryfall.com/card/clb/692/eight-and-a-half-tails), Pious Kitsune, and probably a copy of Opal-Eye, Konda's Yojimbo or two. That's still an aggro-control strategy, but the question would be what to do with the life I gain during the game. Phyrexian Colossus? [Well of Lost Dreams](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/275/well-of-lost-dreams)? As you can see, taking the deck down this path flirts with a lot of rares, but the creature type Fox is a minor theme of the deck that I would love to see expanded.

*Samurai Equip*

The more obvious direction to take Samurai Fish is to start thinking of the very solid equipment available in Standard as a supporting strategy to bushido. The rares -- [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte), the *Darksteel* Swords, Oathkeeper, Takeno's Daisho, [Konda's Banner](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/259/kondas-banner), and Empyrial Plate -- are the ones that probably first come to mind here. I think there is great promise, though, in anything that makes a Samurai scarier in combat, including Tenza, Godo's Maul, [Grafted Wargear](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/312/grafted-wargear), [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer), [Vulshok Morningstar](https://scryfall.com/card/ddi/66/vulshok-morningstar), and [Fireshrieker](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/674/fireshrieker). [Mask of Memory](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/324/mask-of-memory) might give a Samurai deck just the fuel it needs to survive the late-game, [Lightning Greaves](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/202/lightning-greaves) obviously makes the deck a turn faster, and a less obvious choice is [Horned Helm](https://scryfall.com/card/5dn/132/horned-helm), which really gives bushido a kick in the pants. I wouldn't want to use all of those artifacts in my deck, obviously, but it would be fun experimenting with them to see which ones most enhance my Samurai goodness. This probably also opens the door for [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/298/aether-vial) in the deck.

##### Saving Samurai Fish

*Saviors of Kamigawa* will be available to ***Magic** Online* on July 27. Since Samurai is a creature type introduced in *Champions of Kamigawa*, it makes sense that a Samurai strategy will be strongest once the full *Kamigawa* block has run its course. It should be no surprise, then, that I am drooling over the *Saviors* cards to add to my modified Way of the Warrior deck. Below are four cards I'd like to at least try in my deck, roughly in order from most to least probable of making it into the final decklist. No points for guessing my first choice.

[![Hand of Honor](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/sok/12?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/12/hand-of-honor)

*Hand of Honor*

Okay, seriously. That's one nifty Samurai. Cool name. Cool art. Great cost. Great abilities. Remember, too, that the Casual Constructed room of ***Magic** Online* is rife with black decks. The only downer, really, is that it's competing with so many other good two-mana cards (and generally making them look bad). A Samurai deck's two best tools are now [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/43/samurai-of-the-pale-curtain) and [Hand of Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/12/hand-of-honor), which is enough to probably push me away from splashing a color and return to a monowhite deck. That's how good [Hand of Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/12/hand-of-honor) is. Without him, I think Samurai decks are more interesting splashing a support color. With him, I would rather stay in one color. That's one nifty Samurai.

*Charge Across the Araba*

If you squint, it's sort of like a white [Overrun](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/230/overrun). Score another point for the monowhite argument. [Charge Across the Araba](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/4/charge-across-the-araba) is a game-ender in a White Weenie deck, plain and simple. Also, recall that my deck never really needed more than four mana to be effective. In the current build I use [Soratami Cloudskater](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/86/soratami-cloudskater) as a solution to extra land, but [Charge Across the Araba](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/4/charge-across-the-araba) is just as good a use for extra land and a heck of a lot more deadly. The problem with it is that it becomes the most expensive card in the deck, and thus pushes the deck's mana curve a little higher. I'm not overly concerned about it, though, especially moving to a monocolored deck that will have fewer mana problems on principle.

Araba Mothrider

*Araba Mothrider*

“Wow, neat,” you may be saying, “your moth can lay into an opponent for exactly one damage a turn and can block Meloku's Illusion tokens without dying. You must be thrilled.” True enough, Araba Mothrider doesn't look like it has much offensive firepower. For effectively the same price you get [Leonin Skyhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/7/leonin-skyhunter). For one more mana you get Diving Griffin. The important distinction is that these cards aren't Samurai. Why should that matter? Two reasons: [Nagao, Bound By Honor](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/36/nagao-bound-by-honor) and [Call to Glory](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/4/call-to-glory). Add Oathkeeper, Takeno's Daisho, [Konda's Banner](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/259/kondas-banner), etc. to your deck and I can imagine wanting Araba Mothrider more than any other cheap flier. I still think with [Sensei Golden-Tail](https://scryfall.com/card/chk/44/sensei-golden-tail) in the deck I may prefer [Leonin Skyhunter](https://scryfall.com/card/ddg/7/leonin-skyhunter), but it would be worth testing to see.

*Manriki-Gusari*

It's cheap, it makes my Samurai better in combat and it's a definitive stance against power rares such as [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte) and [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/297/sword-of-fire-and-ice). If I decide to go the equipment route, [Manriki-Gusari](https://scryfall.com/card/sok/156/manriki-gusari) is something to try. I like the idea of O-Naginata on Nagao, too, but that's too narrow to take seriously.

For *Saviors* rares, [Pithing Needle](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/312/pithing-needle) and Kataki, War's Wage are the two cards I can see making it into the deck. [Pithing Needle](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/312/pithing-needle) obviously stops so many virtual auto-loss cards for Samurai, including [Vedalken Shackles](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/218/vedalken-shackles), [Umezawa's Jitte](https://scryfall.com/card/bok/163/umezawas-jitte), [Arc-Slogger](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/85/arc-slogger), and [Meloku the Clouded Mirror](https://scryfall.com/card/cmr/399/meloku-the-clouded-mirror), among others. Kataki, while not a Samurai, is aggressive and doesn't slow you down if you're not playing many (or any) artifacts yourself. He would easily replace [Kami of Ancient Law](https://scryfall.com/card/soc/150/kami-of-ancient-law), I think.

Based on these thoughts, here's a theoretical (untested) Samurai deck I might start to play post-*Saviors*. You can see I've left some decision points that would need working out through playtesting.
