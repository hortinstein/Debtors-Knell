Before I continue along with the deck doctorin', I'd like to address a couple of concerns I've had from readers regarding last week's columns.

##### Question: Is this deck budget?

Answer: Absolutely! Remember, all prices are based on the going rates using ***Magic** Online*. Island, [Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks), Mountain, [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode), Dwarven Patrol, [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), [Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip), [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger), [Reminisce](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/22/reminisce) and [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) can all be had for a song and a dance. [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand) can be found at half a ticket each. [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) can be found at half a ticket each. [Opt](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/158/opt) can be found at two for a ticket as well.

Niv-Mizzet is the sole rare in the deck. I checked the trading message boards, and found dozens of Niv-Mizzet at three to four tickets each. The total cost on this deck? Eleven tickets! As a spoiler, I go up to four Niv-Mizzet (still the only rare in the deck) by the last build of Pinball. The cost of that version of the deck tops out at seventeen tickets – still well within my thirty ticket budget constraint.

##### Question: Enough with Extended! Can we get back to Standard?

Answer: The Magic 8-ball says definitely so. It's been a fun jaunt into Extendedland, but I'll be heading back to Standardnation again next week.

**Game #1: HanG21 (R/W Aggro)**

Han gets down [Slith Firewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/39/slith-firewalker)s on turns 2 and 3, and I am without any [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)s or [Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip). A Jitte on the fourth turn ensures a losing board position, as my pingers suddenly become ineffective, and I don't have a way to keep Niv-Mizzet on the board long enough to combo HanG21 out.

*0-1*

Although it's sometimes frustrating to lose to Jitte, my deck definitely had ways to come back from this game. If I had drawn any [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)/[Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip), I could have kept his [Slith Firewalker](https://scryfall.com/card/jvc/39/slith-firewalker)s tapped down long enough to get [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) onto the board, and then used [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) to shoot his Firewalkers multiple times. Unfortunately, I kept a hand with multiple pingers and few fast effects, so I got outraced this time.

**Game #2: Omchizen (Enchantress)**

Enchantress's Presence

Omchizen drops a quick Enchantress's Presence, followed with [Heartbeat of Spring](https://scryfall.com/card/2xm/171/heartbeat-of-spring) and [Loxodon Warhammer](https://scryfall.com/card/c21/249/loxodon-warhammer). I draw double [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode), and kill every creature I see, untapping the [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode)s multiple times with [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) and [Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip). With two [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode)s on the board, one [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) equals five points of damage (the initial two shots, plus the two untap triggers from playing an instant, plus the untap from [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) itself). Omchizen is unable to get an offense going, and I get Niv-Mizzet enchanted with [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) for the instant win.

*1-1*

One reader asked if it would be better to run [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) in this deck, rather than [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)/[Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip). [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) is decent, but [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) has more uses in this particular deck, plus [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) fits this deck's theme better. [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) allows me to draw extra cards when I have a pinger enchanted with [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity). [Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip) lets me tap down my opponent's land at the end of turn, which can spell the difference between getting Niv-Mizzet on the board past countermagic, and losing a game. [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) is definitely a solid spell, but it doesn't combo with [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) and [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger) as well as [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle).

**Game #5: Penguinhhj (B/R Beatdown)**

He gets [Stinkweed Imp](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/36/stinkweed-imp), and I match it with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode). He casts [Cruel Edict](https://scryfall.com/card/dvd/48/cruel-edict), kills my only creature, and then uses [Reckless Charge](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/144/reckless-charge) to turn his Imp into a 4/3 beater. I dig further into my deck with [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), and he answers with Barbarian Bully. [Dwarven Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/61/dwarven-patrol) comes down to make sure the Bully doesn't take all my lunch money, and [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) deals with the flashbacked [Reckless Charge](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/144/reckless-charge) on the Imp. Niv-Mizzet hits on turn 6, lives through the turn, and then wears the [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) cap. Twenty cards and damage later, the curiously strong Niv-Mizzet wins the game.

*3-2*

Although a main focus of the deck were the [Dwarven Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/61/dwarven-patrol)s, I found that they weren't really working out all that well in this deck. They don't untap from multi-colored Red spells, meaning that [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode), Niv-Mizzet, [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger), and [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter) left the Patrol tapped. Without [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger), [Dwarven Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/61/dwarven-patrol) was just a 4/2 dork with a major drawback.

I also wanted to get the deck up to four Niv-Mizzet, since Niv-Mizzet was doing a majority of the killing in my endgames. If it's the #1 killer in my deck, I want to have the best chance I could of drawing the big ol' lizard every game. Last, I wanted to speed the deck up a little, so I brought my [Razorfin Hunter](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/119/razorfin-hunter) count up to four, added in a couple of [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand)s, and took out the [Opt](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/158/opt)s. The [Opt](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/158/opt)s were great with [Dwarven Patrol](https://scryfall.com/card/apc/61/dwarven-patrol), but sans Patrol they were just another cantrip. Since I didn't want to be left with one [Opt](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/158/opt), I took it out in favor of another [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research).

[![Thieving Magpie](https://api.scryfall.com/cards/dmr/68?format=image&version=normal)](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie)

Right about here is where part one of Pinball went live on **magicthegathering.com**. I received quite a few suggestions for the deck, and made a few changes to the deck based on reader feedback.

1. I traded [Reminisce](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/22/reminisce) for [Mnemonic Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/59/mnemonic-nexus). The one mana difference between the two is negligible, since the purpose of reshuffling my graveyard into my library is to give Niv-Mizzet/[Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) more fuel should my opponent have more life than I have cards. Being able to restock my library mid-[Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) is key.
2. I was short a little on offense, and more than a few people suggested adding [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie) to the deck. [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie) draws cards whenever it damages the opponent, so if it is enchanted by [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger), I'd draw two cards per shooting. Without any enchantments, [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie) still swings for a card a turn.
3. Although I didn't want to run [Shock](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/809/shock) in the deck, [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) was another story. [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) flashes back for no mana (only the sacrifice of a Mountain), making it the perfect direct-damage compliment to [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode). [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) allows [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) to shoot twice – [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) does the same for the cost of {R}, sacrifice a Mountain.

In order to make room for the reader suggestions, some cards need to go. [Reminisce](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/22/reminisce) obviously gets cut, since it is swapping one-for-one for the more desirable [Mnemonic Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/59/mnemonic-nexus). [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research) has always been a thrown-in for the deck, and [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie) also allows me access to card-drawing, so they can swap as well. Last of all, I have had games where I get all [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) and no action. Eight [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle) effects are too many, so I can afford to give up a couple of those in favor of [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart)s – especially since both are designed mainly to play teammate to [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode).

**In**: [Mnemonic Nexus](https://scryfall.com/card/rav/59/mnemonic-nexus), 3 [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie), 3 [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart)
**Out**: [Reminisce](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/22/reminisce), 4 [Compulsive Research](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/147/compulsive-research), 2 [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)

**Game #8: Goblin361 (B/G)**

Quicksilver DaggerI get turn 3 [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) and turn 4 [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity). I shoot for six cards over two turns with untap spells, until the [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) gets taken out by Sundering Vitae. [Thieving Magpie](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/68/thieving-magpie) joins the party, and draws into [Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger). The Dagger gets hit with [Naturalize](https://scryfall.com/card/m19/190/naturalize), but this leaves Goblin361 open for another [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity) on the [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode). *5-3*

The great thing about [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity)/[Quicksilver Dagger](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/195/quicksilver-dagger) on [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) is that it enables me to draw into more ways to untap [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode). [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle), [Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip) and [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) all cost only one (or zero) mana to cast. If I have six mana available on a turn, I can do close to a full twenty damage: Shoot with [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode) (1), untap six times with three [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)/[Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip) (7), cast [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) three times (13, three from the darts, and three from [Gelectrode](https://scryfall.com/card/ddj/5/gelectrode)), and flash back [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) three times (19). With one [Curiosity](https://scryfall.com/card/jmp/147/curiosity), I'd draw thirteen extra cards during that turn. With two, I'd draw twenty-six.

*9-3*

Time for one last change to the deck. Although one of the reasons I ran [Twiddle](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/111/twiddle)/[Dream's Grip](https://scryfall.com/card/mrd/34/dreams-grip) in this deck was to untap [Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks) in a pinch, I found that the Boilerworks just weren't working out as planned. I almost never wanted to play a land tapped, since the deck could maximize use of mana each turn. In addition, I was finding myself short on early Red mana that didn't come into play tapped, and [Lava Dart](https://scryfall.com/card/mh1/134/lava-dart) needs Mountains to come back from the graveyard. The mana needs of the deck had evolved from the beginning builds, and so the mana base of the deck needs to evolve as well.
