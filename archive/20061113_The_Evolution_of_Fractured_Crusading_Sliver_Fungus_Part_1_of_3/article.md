I chose to start with Reality Fracture because it focuses the most on the new mechanics of *Time Spiral* - in this case, suspend. Suspend is a really interesting ability, because it has multiple game play implications.

1) You can trade time for mana. [Errant Ephemeron](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/66/errant-ephemeron) is a 4/4 flyer - much like [Air Elemental](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/44/air-elemental). [Air Elemental](https://scryfall.com/card/m20/44/air-elemental) would cost five mana, but [Errant Ephemeron](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/66/errant-ephemeron) costs seven. Doesn't seem like a deal so far, right? Well, here's the catch - you can suspend [Errant Ephemeron](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/66/errant-ephemeron) on turn two and have it come into play as a hasted 4/4 flyer on turn six. You know what else comes into play as a hasted 4/4 flayer on turn six? Volcanic Dragon. The difference is, Volcanic Dragon makes you tap out turn six. [Errant Ephemeron](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/66/errant-ephemeron) allows you to have all your mana open for other spells.

2) Suspend allows you to store up cards for future consideration. Take, for example, [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt). Let's say my opponent casts [Llanowar Elves](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/227/llanowar-elves). I suspend [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt), and it changes my opponent's choices. Maybe they were going to drop Ohran Viper. If they do, then it will surely get fried by the bolt. If they don't, that's one less Elf they'll have.

3) Suspend works really, really well with storm. When you remove the last suspend counter from a card, that's when it counts as "being played." This allows the Reality Fracture deck to set up some really big turns, in which multiple cards become unsuspended at once, and then a storm spell ([Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/118/empty-the-warrens), [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot), [Ignite Memories](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/164/ignite-memories)) copies itself five or six times. Good stuff.

Let's take a look at the contents of the Reality Fracture deck, so we can understand what makes it tick.

For a deck that relies half on suspend and half on storm, this deck is awfully light on suspend creatures. There's only eleven here, and they don't even seem to be the right eleven. [Errant Ephemeron](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/66/errant-ephemeron) is almost always better than [Viscerid Deepwalker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/91/viscerid-deepwalker), and [Pardic Dragon](https://scryfall.com/card/mma/124/pardic-dragon) can be kept out of play by an opponent who plays, you know, spells. Unfortunately, [Greater Gargadon](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/111/greater-gargadon) is the only other Suspend creature in red or blue not represented in this deck. (Note to everyone: Ith does not count.) I'm sure that if I evolve the deck towards the suspend theme, this configuration of creatures will change.

All right, here's the problem - the support creatures in this deck are on the essive side of depressive. [Jhoira's Timebug](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/269/jhoiras-timebug) makes a lot of sense in this deck - it helps you accelerate all of your suspend cards by a turn (or push them back, if you want to delay them for some reason - like if you have a [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/82/riftwing-cloudskate) about to come into play and your opponent has no creatures). The other four creatures are dubious at best.

[Clockwork Hydra](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/264/clockwork-hydra), [Giant Oyster](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/22/giant-oyster): Both of these are designed to work with [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning). The problem is, [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning) is mainly here for Suspend, and the effects of [Clockwork Hydra](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/264/clockwork-hydra) and [Giant Oyster](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/22/giant-oyster) are both underwhelming. Moreover, [Giant Oyster](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/22/giant-oyster) needs to have a tapped creature to work. There's no ways in this entire deck to artificially tap a creature. Is there an [Icy Manipulator](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/225/icy-manipulator) in the house? Mangara, did you bring your Amber Prison? Anyone?

Am I playing to a dead house in here?

[Coal Stoker](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/114/coal-stoker) is a neat trick to use to improve your storm count...but if you aren't going to storm, chances are you're getting a [Hill Giant](https://scryfall.com/card/10e/212/hill-giant) with three points of mana burn. If I wanted that, I'd play [Serpent Warrior](https://scryfall.com/card/8ed/161/serpent-warrior).

[Sage of Epityr](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/74/sage-of-epityr) helps you set up your draws, but doesn't necessarily have anything to do with either storm or suspend. However, since setting up all your suspend spells in the early game can help allow for one huge storm-count turn, the Sage helps there.

**Lands:**

12 [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island)
 11 [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain)
 1 [Dreadship Reef](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/276/dreadship-reef)

Do not like. [Dreadship Reef](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/276/dreadship-reef)? I don't see an X-spell in this deck - why is a half-off color land in a deck that tends to not need extra mana thanks to suspend? Also, there are 23 blue-mana symbols in the costs of spells in this deck, and 17 red. Of these, many of them need to be online by turn two (the suspend cards), so if a deck ever needed a [Gemstone Mine](https://scryfall.com/card/dmr/247/gemstone-mine), it's this one. (Why is that in the Sliver deck and not this one? Woulda worked well with [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning), folks!)

In short, this deck wants to suspend a bunch of guys, time them so they all come into play around the same turn, and then hit large storm counts. Unfortunately, the wrong quantities of each suspend creature are in this deck, the support spells and creatures are pretty awful, and there's too little theme ([Jhoira's Timebug](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/269/jhoiras-timebug)s and [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning)) and too much chaff ([Ground Rift](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/162/ground-rift), [Clockwork Hydra](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/264/clockwork-hydra), [Claws of Gix](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/107/claws-of-gix)).

I don't expect the first few practice games to be pretty.

**Game 1:** Darktama (Modified Reality Fracture)

Ha ha, funny - my first practice game with the non-modified Reality Fracture deck is against a modified Reality Fracture deck! I mulligan a one-land hand. On my first turn, I suspend a first turn [Viscerid Deepwalker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/91/viscerid-deepwalker), and on the second turn, [Keldon Halberdier](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/173/keldon-halberdier). He suspends [Wheel of Fate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/198/wheel-of-fate) and then drops a lot of mana artifacts ([Prismatic Lens](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/264/prismatic-lens), [Izzet Signet](https://scryfall.com/card/tdc/320/izzet-signet)) and [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate)s an [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island). I respond by [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning) my Halberdier and throwing [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/82/riftwing-cloudskate) into the aether. The Halberdiers and [Viscerid Deepwalker](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/91/viscerid-deepwalker) come into play and hit him, and I suspend [Rift Bolt](https://scryfall.com/card/2x2/122/rift-bolt).

[Wheel of Fate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/198/wheel-of-fate) goes off, and he [Remand](https://scryfall.com/card/rvr/59/remand)s the Cloudskate (but why? He has no creatures!) and then suspends Deep-Sea Kraken. He steals two more lands, leaving me with one. I play [Sage of Epityr](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/74/sage-of-epityr), get a second land, and suspend [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/82/riftwing-cloudskate)s on consecutive turns. He [Electrolyze](https://scryfall.com/card/ima/198/electrolyze)s the Halberdier and [Confiscate](https://scryfall.com/card/fdn/709/confiscate)s the Deepwalker. I drop [Claws of Gix](https://scryfall.com/card/tsb/107/claws-of-gix) (which would have been good against all his Confiscating/Annexing action), land my first Cloudskate (returning the Deepwalker back to my hand), and then eat [Pyroclasm](https://scryfall.com/card/dsk/149/pyroclasm).

On my next turn the second Cloudskate comes onto the stack, which removes the last counter from his Kraken. The Kraken comes into play, then my Cloudskate comes into play, bouncing his Kraken. Ka-ching! I make a note that being able to engineer the time that my Cloudskates come into play (through Timebugs or [Clockspinning](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/53/clockspinning)) will be really powerful. He [Boomerang](https://scryfall.com/card/5ed/75/boomerang)s my Cloudskate, and I cast [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot) for 4 to finish him off.

**Record: 1-0**

This draws a concession.

**Record: 3-2**

Although I ended up with a winning record, I had to exercise all my self control to keep from heaving my keyboard across the room. Although there is some luck involved in **Magic**, I completed five straight games where I had mana problems in each and every game. The above games involved three mulligans, one land flood, and four games where I didn't draw a second color of mana until past turn four. Mana issues take any fun out of the game, since you literally sit there not being able to play spells while your opponent wails on you.

The first change I made to Reality Fracture was to add in four [Izzet Boilerworks](https://scryfall.com/card/otc/302/izzet-boilerworks). These would smooth out the mana a lot and would keep me from having as many lack-of-mana issues in future games. To make room for them, I took out the [Dreadship Reef](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/276/dreadship-reef), two [Island](https://scryfall.com/card/msh/289/island)s, and a [Mountain](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/858/mountain).

I also wanted to play around more with the cards which allow my suspended guys to resolve earlier, so I added in two [Jhoira's Timebug](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/269/jhoiras-timebug) and took out a [Clockwork Hydra](https://scryfall.com/card/tsr/264/clockwork-hydra) and a [Ground Rift](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/162/ground-rift). I didn't want to fiddle too much with the deck, since my mana issues had prevented me from really seeing the deck in full action. What I did see showed a lot of potential - huge turns with multiple suspend cards resolving at once, resulting in 5-6 [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/card/msc/803/grapeshot)s, 12-20 damage from [Ignite Memories](https://scryfall.com/card/tsp/164/ignite-memories), or 8-12 Goblins coming into play at once.
